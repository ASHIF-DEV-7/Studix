Previous Year Questions & Detailed Solutions

Important Questions on Reflection of Light

1. Define the principal focus of a concave mirror.
The principal focus of a concave mirror is a point on the principal axis where all the light rays which are parallel to the principal axis converge after reflection from the concave mirror.
2. The radius of curvature of a spherical mirror is 20 cm. What is its focal length?
Focal length (f) = Radius of curvature (R) / 2
Given R = 20 cm
f = R/2 = 20/2 = 10 cm
So, the focal length is 10 cm.
3. Name the mirror that can give an erect and enlarged image of an object.
A concave mirror can give an erect and enlarged image of an object when the object is placed between the pole and the principal focus of the mirror.
4. Why do we prefer a convex mirror as a rear-view mirror in vehicles?
We prefer a convex mirror as a rear-view mirror in vehicles because:
  • It always produces an erect image
  • It has a wider field of view compared to plane or concave mirrors
  • It forms diminished images, which allows us to see more traffic behind the vehicle
5. Define the following terms in the context of spherical mirrors:
(a) Pole
(b) Center of curvature
(c) Principal axis
(d) Principal focus
(a) Pole: The center point of the mirror surface, denoted by P.
(b) Center of curvature: The center of the sphere of which the mirror is a part, denoted by C.
(c) Principal axis: The straight line passing through the pole and the center of curvature of the mirror.
(d) Principal focus: The point on the principal axis where all the light rays parallel to the principal axis converge (for concave mirror) or appear to diverge from (for convex mirror) after reflection.

7. An object is placed at a distance of 10 cm from a convex mirror of focal length 15 cm. Find the position and nature of the image.
Using mirror formula: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u
Given: Object distance, u = -10 cm (using Cartesian sign convention)
Focal length, f = +15 cm (for convex mirror)

1/15 = 1/v + 1/(-10)
1/v = 1/15 + 1/10 = (2 + 3)/30 = 5/30 = 1/6
So, v = +6 cm

The positive sign indicates that the image is formed behind the mirror.
Magnification, m = -v/u = -6/(-10) = 0.6
The positive magnification less than 1 indicates that the image is virtual, erect and diminished.
8. State the laws of reflection.
The laws of reflection are:
  1. The incident ray, the reflected ray and the normal to the reflecting surface at the point of incidence all lie in the same plane.
  2. The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection (i = r).
9. An object 4 cm in height is placed at a distance of 15 cm from a concave mirror of focal length 10 cm. Find the position, nature and size of the image.
Given: Object height, h = 4 cm
Object distance, u = -15 cm (using Cartesian sign convention)
Focal length, f = -10 cm (for concave mirror)

Using mirror formula: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u
1/(-10) = 1/v + 1/(-15)
-1/10 = 1/v - 1/15
1/v = -1/10 + 1/15 = (-3 + 2)/30 = -1/30
So, v = -30 cm

The negative sign indicates that the image is formed in front of the mirror.
Magnification, m = -v/u = -(-30)/(-15) = 30/(-15) = -2
The negative magnification indicates that the image is inverted.
|m| = 2 > 1, so the image is enlarged.

Size of image, h' = m × h = -2 × 4 = -8 cm
The negative sign indicates the image is inverted, and its size is 8 cm.
10. Differentiate between real and virtual images.
Real Image:
  • Formed when light rays actually meet after reflection
  • Can be obtained on a screen
  • Always inverted
Virtual Image:
  • Formed when light rays appear to meet after reflection
  • Cannot be obtained on a screen
  • Always erect
11. What is meant by the term 'lateral inversion'?
Lateral inversion is the phenomenon where the left side of an object appears as the right side in its mirror image and vice versa. This happens in plane mirrors.
12. Define magnification and write its formula for spherical mirrors.
Magnification is defined as the ratio of the height of the image to the height of the object.
m = h'/h = -v/u
where:
m = magnification
h' = height of the image
h = height of the object
v = image distance
u = object distance
13. An object is placed at a distance of 20 cm from a concave mirror. A real image is formed at 40 cm in front of the mirror. What is the focal length of the mirror?
Given: Object distance, u = -20 cm
Image distance, v = -40 cm (since real image is formed in front of the mirror)

Using mirror formula: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u
1/f = 1/(-40) + 1/(-20) = -1/40 - 1/20 = -1/40 - 2/40 = -3/40
So, f = -40/3 ≈ -13.33 cm
The focal length is approximately 13.33 cm (negative sign indicates it's a concave mirror).
14. Draw a ray diagram to show the formation of image when an object is placed at the center of curvature of a concave mirror.

Characteristics of the image formed:
  • Image is formed at the center of curvature (C)
  • Image is real and inverted
  • Image is of the same size as the object
15. Why is a convex mirror used as a reflector in street lamps?
A convex mirror is used as a reflector in street lamps because it diverges the light rays over a wider area, thus spreading the light and illuminating a larger area of the road.
16. An object 2 cm high is placed at a distance of 16 cm from a concave mirror which produces a real image 4 cm high. Find the focal length of the mirror.
Given: Object height, h = 2 cm
Image height, h' = -4 cm (negative since real image is inverted)
Object distance, u = -16 cm

Magnification, m = h'/h = -4/2 = -2
Also, m = -v/u
So, -2 = -v/(-16) => -2 = v/16 => v = -32 cm

Using mirror formula: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u
1/f = 1/(-32) + 1/(-16) = -1/32 - 2/32 = -3/32
So, f = -32/3 ≈ -10.67 cm
The focal length is approximately 10.67 cm.
17. What is the number of images formed when an object is placed between two plane mirrors inclined at an angle of 60° to each other?
Number of images, n = (360/θ) - 1
Given: θ = 60°
n = (360/60) - 1 = 6 - 1 = 5
So, 5 images will be formed.
18. Define the following terms:
(a) Focal length
(b) Aperture
(c) Radius of curvature
(a) Focal length: The distance between the pole and the principal focus of a mirror.
(b) Aperture: The diameter of the reflecting surface of the mirror.
(c) Radius of curvature: The radius of the sphere of which the mirror is a part.
19. An object is placed at a distance of 12 cm from a convex mirror of focal length 15 cm. Find the position and magnification of the image.
Given: Object distance, u = -12 cm
Focal length, f = +15 cm (for convex mirror)

Using mirror formula: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u
1/15 = 1/v + 1/(-12)
1/v = 1/15 + 1/12 = (4 + 5)/60 = 9/60 = 3/20
So, v = +20/3 ≈ +6.67 cm

The positive sign indicates that the image is formed behind the mirror.
Magnification, m = -v/u = -(20/3)/(-12) = (20/3)/12 = 20/36 = 5/9 ≈ 0.56
The positive magnification less than 1 indicates that the image is virtual, erect and diminished.
20. State the mirror formula and explain the sign convention used.
Mirror formula: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u
where:
f = focal length of the mirror
v = image distance from the pole
u = object distance from the pole

Sign convention:
  • All distances are measured from the pole of the mirror
  • Distances measured in the direction of incident light are taken as negative
  • Distances measured opposite to the direction of incident light are taken as positive
  • Height measured upwards is positive, height measured downwards is negative
