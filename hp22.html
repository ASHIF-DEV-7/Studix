Class 10 Biology Previous Year Questions (2022) - Heredity

  1. Define heredity and explain its significance in living organisms. (3 marks)

    Answer: Heredity is the transmission of traits from parents to offspring via genes. Significance: Ensures species continuity, passes genetic information.

  2. What are genes? Where are they located? (3 marks)

    Answer: Genes are DNA segments controlling traits. Located on chromosomes in the nucleus.

  3. Explain Mendel’s law of dominance with an example. (4 marks)

    Answer: Law of dominance: One allele dominates over recessive. Example: In peas, tall (TT/Tt) dominates short (tt).

  4. Describe Mendel’s law of segregation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Law of segregation: Alleles segregate during gamete formation, each gamete carries one allele.

  5. What is Mendel’s law of independent assortment? Give an example. (4 marks)

    Answer: Law of independent assortment: Genes for different traits assort independently. Example: Pea shape (round/wrinkled) and color (yellow/green).

  6. What is a monohybrid cross? Explain with a diagram description. (4 marks)

    Answer: Monohybrid cross: Cross for one trait. Example: TT x tt yields Tt (tall). Diagram: Punnett square showing 3:1 ratio.

  7. Explain the phenotypic and genotypic ratio in a monohybrid cross. (3 marks)

    Answer: Phenotypic ratio: 3 dominant:1 recessive (e.g., 3 tall:1 short). Genotypic ratio: 1 TT:2 Tt:1 tt.

  8. What is a dihybrid cross? State its phenotypic ratio. (3 marks)

    Answer: Dihybrid cross: Cross for two traits. Phenotypic ratio: 9:3:3:1 (e.g., round yellow:round green:wrinkled yellow:wrinkled green).

  9. Explain how a Punnett square is used in genetic crosses. (3 marks)

    Answer: Punnett square predicts offspring genotypes by combining parental gametes, showing possible trait ratios.

  10. What is the difference between dominant and recessive traits? (3 marks)

    Answer: Dominant traits express in heterozygous condition (e.g., Tt = tall). Recessive express only in homozygous (e.g., tt = short).

  11. Describe the structure of DNA and its role in heredity. (4 marks)

    Answer: DNA: Double helix with nucleotides (A-T, G-C). Role: Carries genetic information, replicates for inheritance.

  12. What are alleles? Explain with an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Alleles: Alternative forms of a gene. Example: T (tall) and t (short) in pea plants.

  13. Explain the term homozygous with an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Homozygous: Two identical alleles (e.g., TT or tt). Example: TT for tall pea plants.

  14. What is heterozygous? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Heterozygous: Two different alleles (e.g., Tt). Example: Tt for tall pea plants.

  15. Explain sex determination in humans. (4 marks)

    Answer: Humans have XX (female) or XY (male) chromosomes. Father’s sperm (X or Y) determines offspring sex.

  16. What is the role of chromosomes in heredity? (3 marks)

    Answer: Chromosomes carry genes, transmitting hereditary information during cell division to offspring.

  17. Explain the significance of meiosis in reproduction. (4 marks)

    Answer: Meiosis produces gametes with half chromosome number, ensuring genetic variation and stable chromosome count in offspring.

  18. What is a genotype? Differentiate it from phenotype. (3 marks)

    Answer: Genotype: Genetic makeup (e.g., Tt). Phenotype: Physical expression (e.g., tall). Genotype determines phenotype.

  19. Explain the inheritance of blood groups in humans. (4 marks)

    Answer: Blood groups (A, B, AB, O) controlled by alleles Iᴬ, Iᴮ, i. Iᴬ and Iᴮ dominant, i recessive; codominance in AB.

  20. What is codominance? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Codominance: Both alleles express equally in heterozygote. Example: AB blood group (IᴬIᴮ expresses both A and B antigens).

  21. Explain incomplete dominance with an example. (4 marks)

    Answer: Incomplete dominance: Neither allele fully dominant, blended phenotype. Example: Snapdragon flowers (RR red, rr white, Rr pink).

  22. What is a test cross? Explain its purpose. (3 marks)

    Answer: Test cross: Cross between organism with dominant trait and recessive homozygote. Purpose: Determine genotype (homozygous/heterozygous).

  23. Describe Mendel’s experiment with pea plants for seed color. (4 marks)

    Answer: Mendel crossed yellow (YY) and green (yy) seed plants, F1 all yellow (Yy), F2 3:1 yellow:green ratio, proving segregation.

  24. What are traits? Differentiate between inherited and acquired traits. (3 marks)

    Answer: Traits: Characteristics of organism. Inherited: Genetic (e.g., eye color). Acquired: Gained during life (e.g., learned skills).

  25. Explain the role of DNA replication in heredity. (3 marks)

    Answer: DNA replication copies genetic information, ensuring accurate transmission of traits to offspring during cell division.

  26. What is a genetic disorder? Name two examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Genetic disorder: Disease due to gene/chromosome mutation. Examples: Sickle cell anemia, hemophilia.

  27. Explain the cause of sickle cell anemia. (4 marks)

    Answer: Sickle cell anemia: Mutation in hemoglobin gene causes abnormal RBC shape, leading to oxygen transport issues and pain.

  28. What is the significance of crossing over in meiosis? (3 marks)

    Answer: Crossing over exchanges genetic material between homologous chromosomes, increasing genetic diversity in gametes.

  29. Explain the inheritance pattern of hemophilia. (4 marks)

    Answer: Hemophilia: X-linked recessive disorder. Males (XY) more affected; females (XX) carriers if heterozygous.

  30. What is a pedigree chart? Explain its use. (3 marks)

    Answer: Pedigree chart: Diagram showing trait inheritance in family. Use: Tracks genetic disorders, predicts inheritance patterns.

  31. Explain the role of gametes in heredity. (3 marks)

    Answer: Gametes (sperm, egg) carry half parental chromosomes, combining during fertilization to pass traits to offspring.

  32. What is polygenic inheritance? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Polygenic inheritance: Multiple genes control one trait. Example: Human skin color influenced by several genes.

  33. Explain the term ‘homozygous recessive’ with an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Homozygous recessive: Two recessive alleles (e.g., tt). Example: Short pea plants (tt).

  34. What is the role of RNA in gene expression? (4 marks)

    Answer: RNA transcribes DNA (mRNA), translates into proteins (tRNA, rRNA), expressing genetic traits.

  35. Explain the significance of genetic variation in a population. (3 marks)

    Answer: Genetic variation enhances adaptability, survival, and evolution by providing diverse traits.

  36. What is a mutation? Explain its role in heredity. (4 marks)

    Answer: Mutation: Change in DNA sequence. Role: Introduces new traits, may cause disorders or beneficial variations.

  37. Describe the inheritance of color blindness in humans. (3 marks)

    Answer: Color blindness: X-linked recessive. Males (XY) more affected; females (XX) carriers if heterozygous.

  38. What is a dominant lethal allele? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Dominant lethal allele: Causes death if present. Example: Huntington’s disease (dominant, fatal in later life).

  39. Explain how Mendel’s experiments contributed to genetics. (4 marks)

    Answer: Mendel’s pea plant experiments established laws of dominance, segregation, and independent assortment, forming the basis of modern genetics.

  40. What is the role of chromosomes in sex-linked inheritance? (3 marks)

    Answer: Sex chromosomes (X, Y) carry genes for sex-linked traits (e.g., hemophilia on X), affecting males more often.
