Real Numbers - Class 10

Definition of Real Numbers

Real numbers are the set of all rational and irrational numbers. They include all numbers that can be represented on the number line.

Key Points about Real Numbers

  • Real numbers include both rational and irrational numbers
  • Rational numbers can be expressed as p/q where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0
  • Irrational numbers cannot be expressed as a fraction of two integers
  • Every rational number has either a terminating or repeating decimal representation
  • Irrational numbers have non-terminating, non-repeating decimal representations
  • The set of real numbers is denoted by the symbol ℝ
  • Real numbers satisfy the properties of closure, commutativity, and associativity
  • Zero is the additive identity for real numbers
  • One is the multiplicative identity for real numbers
  • Every real number (except zero) has a multiplicative inverse
  • The distributive property holds for real numbers: a(b + c) = ab + ac
  • Real numbers are ordered, meaning any two real numbers can be compared
  • The density property states that between any two real numbers, there exists another real number
  • Real numbers include natural numbers, whole numbers, integers, and decimals
  • √2, √3, π, and e are examples of irrational numbers
  • 0.5, -3, 7/4, and 2.25 are examples of rational numbers
  • The sum of two rational numbers is always rational
  • The sum of a rational and an irrational number is always irrational
  • The product of two non-zero rational numbers is always rational
  • The product of a non-zero rational and an irrational number is always irrational
  • Real numbers can be represented geometrically on the number line
  • The absolute value of a real number represents its distance from zero
  • Real numbers form a complete ordered field
  • Every bounded set of real numbers has a least upper bound (supremum)
  • Real numbers are used in measuring continuous quantities like length, area, and volume

Important Theorems and Properties

Euclid's Division Algorithm

For any two positive integers a and b, there exist unique integers q and r such that:

a = bq + r, where 0 ≤ r < b

Here, a is the dividend, b is the divisor, q is the quotient, and r is the remainder.

Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic

Every composite number can be expressed as a product of primes, and this factorization is unique, apart from the order of the prime factors.

Solved Examples

Example 1: Using Euclid's Division Algorithm

Problem: Use Euclid's division algorithm to find the HCF of 455 and 42.

Step 1: Apply the division algorithm to 455 and 42
455 = 42 × 10 + 35
Step 2: Since remainder ≠ 0, apply the algorithm to 42 and 35
42 = 35 × 1 + 7
Step 3: Since remainder ≠ 0, apply the algorithm to 35 and 7
35 = 7 × 5 + 0
Answer: Since the remainder is now 0, HCF(455, 42) = 7

Example 2: Proving Irrationality

Problem: Prove that √3 is an irrational number.

Step 1: Assume √3 is rational, i.e., √3 = p/q where p, q are integers with no common factor and q ≠ 0
Step 2: Squaring both sides: 3 = p²/q²
Therefore: 3q² = p²
Step 3: This means p² is divisible by 3, so p is also divisible by 3
Let p = 3k for some integer k
Step 4: Substituting: 3q² = (3k)² = 9k²
Therefore: q² = 3k²
Step 5: This means q² is divisible by 3, so q is also divisible by 3
But this contradicts our assumption that p and q have no common factor
Answer: Therefore, √3 is irrational

Example 3: Finding LCM and HCF

Problem: Find the LCM and HCF of 12 and 18 using prime factorization.

Step 1: Find prime factorization
12 = 2² × 3
18 = 2 × 3²
Step 2: For HCF, take the lowest power of common prime factors
Common factors: 2 and 3
HCF = 2¹ × 3¹ = 6
Step 3: For LCM, take the highest power of all prime factors
LCM = 2² × 3² = 4 × 9 = 36
Verification: HCF × LCM = 6 × 36 = 216
12 × 18 = 216 ✓

Example 4: Decimal Representation

Problem: Without actually performing the division, determine whether 17/8 has a terminating or non-terminating decimal representation.

Step 1: Check if the fraction is in its simplest form
HCF(17, 8) = 1, so 17/8 is already in simplest form
Step 2: Find the prime factorization of the denominator
8 = 2³
Step 3: Check if the denominator has factors other than 2 and 5
Since 8 = 2³ has only powers of 2, the decimal will be terminating
Answer: 17/8 has a terminating decimal representation
(17/8 = 2.125)

Remember

A rational number p/q (in lowest terms) has a terminating decimal representation if and only if the prime factorization of q contains only powers of 2 and 5.
