Bholi

By K.A. Abbas | Class 10 English - Footprints Without Feet

About the Story

"Bholi" is a touching story about a simple-minded girl named Sulekha, called Bholi, who transforms from a neglected child to a confident young woman through education.

Key Message:

Education has the power to transform lives and empower individuals to stand up for themselves.

Main Characters

  • Bholi (Sulekha): The protagonist - a simple-minded but kind-hearted girl
  • Ramlal: Bholi's father - the revenue official of the village
  • Bholi's Mother: Who is worried about Bholi's marriage
  • Bishamber Nath: The greedy bridegroom
  • The Teacher: Who helps Bholi gain confidence

Summary

Bholi was the youngest daughter of Ramlal. She fell off the cot when she was ten months old, which damaged her brain and made her simple-minded. She also had pockmarks on her face from smallpox.

When a school for girls opened in the village, the Tehsildar asked Ramlal to set an example by sending his daughters. Since Bholi's sisters were good-looking and could get married easily, Ramlal decided to send Bholi to school.

At school, Bholi's teacher treated her with kindness and encouraged her to speak. Slowly, Bholi gained confidence and education transformed her life.

Transformation:

From a stammering, fearful girl to a confident woman who values self-respect over social approval.

Important Points

  • Bholi means "simpleton" - everyone called her that because she was slow
  • She was neglected by her family due to her appearance and mental condition
  • Education became the turning point in her life
  • Her teacher's encouragement helped her overcome her fears
  • She refused to marry a greedy man who demanded dowry
  • The story highlights the evil of dowry system in Indian society

Social Issues:

The story addresses discrimination against girls, neglect of special needs children, and the dowry system.

About the Author

Khwaja Ahmad Abbas (1914-1987) was an Indian film director, novelist, screenwriter, and journalist. He was known for his progressive and socialist views in his writings.

Vocabulary

  • Revenue official: A government officer who collects taxes
  • Tehsildar: A tax officer in charge of a tehsil
  • Pockmarks: Scars or pits on the skin left by smallpox
  • Simpleton: A person who is not very intelligent
  • Dowry: Property or money brought by a bride to her husband

Character Development

Bholi's journey from a timid, neglected girl to a confident, self-respecting woman shows how education can empower individuals to challenge social injustices and stand up for their rights.

Themes

  • Empowerment through education
  • Women's rights and dignity
  • Social discrimination
  • Transformation and growth
  • Importance of self-respect
