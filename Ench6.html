Mijbil the Otter — Class 10 Notes

Introduction (10 Lines)

1. "Mijbil the Otter" is a true and touching story by Gavin Maxwell.
2. The author brings a young otter, named Mijbil, to live with him as a pet.
3. Mijbil is playful, curious and soon becomes part of the household.
4. The story shows how the otter adapts to a new home and learns new habits.
5. Gavin cares for Mijbil deeply and builds a special pool for him.
6. Mijbil learns to fish, enjoys water, and loves human company.
7. The author describes both funny moments and serious lessons.
8. Mijbil’s behaviour reveals the intelligence and emotion of animals.
9. The story teaches respect for wildlife and animal feelings.
10. Overall, it is a warm tale of friendship between man and animal.

Chapter Explanation (20 Points)

  • 1. Gavin Maxwell finds a young otter and brings it home as a pet.

  • 2. He names the otter Mijbil and takes care of him lovingly.

  • 3. Mijbil is very playful and enjoys splashing in water.

  • 4. The author builds a special pool so Mijbil can swim safely.

  • 5. Mijbil quickly learns to catch fish and becomes a good swimmer.

  • 6. He shows affection towards Gavin and follows him around.

  • 7. Mijbil learns some house manners but remains lively and mischievous.

  • 8. Guests are amazed by Mijbil’s tricks and intelligence.

  • 9. The author describes small incidents that show the otter’s character.

  • 10. Mijbil sometimes becomes jealous and wants attention from Gavin.

  • 11. He also dislikes strangers at times, showing his protective nature.

  • 12. The writer stresses how well animals can bond with humans.

  • 13. Mijbil’s love for water is constant — he sleeps, plays, and eats near it.

  • 14. Gavin notices the otter’s cleverness in solving small problems.

  • 15. The story shows the author’s concern for Mijbil’s health and happiness.

  • 16. There are tender moments when Mijbil shows trust and affection.

  • 17. Gavin learns about responsibility through caring for the otter.

  • 18. The chapter describes how animals feel emotions like joy and sorrow.

  • 19. It also warns that wild animals are not meant to be kept as pets forever.

  • 20. Finally, the reader understands the deep bond possible between humans and animals.

Conclusion (7–8 Lines)

Mijbil the Otter is a warm story that celebrates the loving bond between a man and an animal.

Through careful care and attention, the author creates a safe, happy life for Mijbil.

The otter’s playful nature brings joy, laughter and sometimes trouble — all of which show his personality.

Gavin Maxwell’s writing makes us feel Mijbil’s intelligence, curiosity and loyalty.

The chapter teaches that animals are sentient beings with feelings and needs.

It also reminds us that looking after a wild creature needs patience, respect and responsibility.

In short, the story asks us to treat animals kindly and understand their nature.

Moral / Theme

  • Animals have feelings: they can love, play and grieve.

  • Respect wildlife: wild animals are best admired, not always kept as pets.

  • Care & responsibility: looking after another life needs patience and kindness.

  • Human-animal bond: friendship between species teaches compassion.
