Class 10 English - PYQs

Q1: Who is the main character in 'Footprints Without Feet'?

A: Griffin, the invisible man, is the main character.

Q2: How did Griffin become invisible?

A: He became invisible by taking a special chemical potion he invented.

Q3: Who is the protagonist in 'Madam Rides the Bus'?

A: Miss Malini, a young school girl, is the protagonist.

Q4: Why did Malini decide to take the bus alone?

A: She wanted to experience freedom and explore the city on her own.

Q5: What did Mathilde lose in 'The Necklace'?

A: Mathilde lost a diamond necklace that she borrowed from her friend Madame Forestier.

Q6: How did losing the necklace change Mathilde's life?

A: She had to work for 10 years to repay the cost of the lost necklace, leading to hardship.

Q7: Who are the main characters in 'The Proposal'?

A: Lomov and Natalia are the main characters.

Q8: What causes the argument between Lomov and Natalia?

A: Their argument starts over a piece of land and trivial issues despite their love.

Q9: What is the main theme of 'Fire and Ice'?

A: The poem explores the destructive power of desire (fire) and hatred (ice).

Q10: How did Malini feel after her journey on the bus?

A: She felt thrilled and empowered by her experience of independence.

Q11: How does Amanda react to her mother’s strict rules?

A: Amanda feels constrained and frustrated but understands her mother’s care.

Q12: What is the significance of trees in the story?

A: Trees represent life, resilience, and connection with nature.

Q13: What does fog symbolize in the poem 'Fog' by Carl Sandburg?

A: Fog symbolizes silence, mystery, and quiet movement over the city.

Q14: How can one recognize a wild animal according to the chapter?

A: By observing its tracks, behavior, and environment carefully.

Q15: Who is Miljbil in the story?

A: Miljbil is an intelligent and playful otter who interacts with humans.

Q16: What lesson does Amanda learn?

A: Amanda learns to balance her freedom with responsibility.

Q17: What is the main lesson of 'The Hack Driver'?

A: The story teaches about cleverness and human ingenuity.

Q18: Why is 'The Midnight Visitor' thrilling?

A: It is thrilling because of suspense and the clever plan to catch the culprit.

Q19: How does 'A Tiger in the Zoo' highlight animal captivity?

A: It shows how animals lose their freedom and natural instincts when kept in captivity.

Q20: What is the theme of 'Sergeant at Benares'?

A: It emphasizes discipline, courage, and moral responsibility.

Q21: How does Anne Frank's diary provide insight into WWII?

A: It gives a personal perspective on fear, hope, and life under Nazi occupation.

Q22: What is the moral of 'The Book That Saved the Earth'?

A: Knowledge and intelligence can overcome challenges and protect humanity.

Q23: Why is 'The Proposal' considered a comedy?

A: Because of the humorous arguments and misunderstandings between characters.

Q24: What role does nature play in the poem 'Fog'?

A: Nature is depicted as quiet, mysterious, and dominating urban life.

Q25: How does 'Fire and Ice' end?

A: The poem ends by questioning whether the world will end in desire (fire) or hate (ice).

Q26: What is the central conflict in 'The Hack Driver'?

A: The conflict arises from the challenges of transportation and trickery of the hack driver.

Q27: How does 'Miljbil the Otter' inspire readers?

A: It inspires readers about animal intelligence and the bond between humans and animals.

Q28: What is the significance of freedom in 'Madam Rides the Bus'?

A: It shows the joy of self-reliance and adventure in a young girl’s life.

Q29: What is the lesson from 'The Necklace'?

A: The story teaches about honesty, humility, and the danger of vanity.

Q30: How does 'Footprints Without Feet' show the consequences of science?

A: It shows that misuse of scientific knowledge can lead to isolation, crime, and suffering.
