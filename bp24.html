Class 10 Biology Previous Year Questions (2024) with Answers

  1. Explain the process of photosynthesis with the balanced equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants convert CO₂ and H₂O into glucose using sunlight and chlorophyll. Equation: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ (in presence of sunlight and chlorophyll).

  2. What are stomata? Describe their role in transpiration. (3 marks)

    Answer: Stomata are pores on leaf surfaces. They facilitate transpiration by allowing water vapor to escape, aiding in cooling and nutrient transport.

  3. Differentiate between autotrophs and heterotrophs with examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Autotrophs make their own food (e.g., green plants via photosynthesis). Heterotrophs depend on others (e.g., animals consuming plants or other animals).

  4. Describe the structure of a chloroplast and its function. (4 marks)

    Answer: Chloroplast has a double membrane, stroma, grana (thylakoids with chlorophyll). Function: Site of photosynthesis, where light and dark reactions occur.

  5. Explain the significance of chlorophyll in photosynthesis. (3 marks)

    Answer: Chlorophyll absorbs sunlight (mainly blue and red wavelengths), converting light energy into chemical energy for glucose production.

  6. What is the role of light in the light-dependent reactions of photosynthesis? (3 marks)

    Answer: Light splits water (photolysis), releases O₂, generates ATP and NADPH for the Calvin cycle.

  7. Describe the human alimentary canal and the digestion of starch. (4 marks)

    Answer: Alimentary canal: Mouth, esophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine, anus. Starch digestion: In mouth by salivary amylase to maltose, then in small intestine by pancreatic amylase to glucose.

  8. Explain the process of digestion in the small intestine. (3 marks)

    Answer: In small intestine, bile emulsifies fats, pancreatic enzymes (amylase, lipase, trypsin) break down carbs, fats, proteins; intestinal enzymes complete digestion, nutrients absorbed.

  9. What is the role of villi in absorption? (3 marks)

    Answer: Villi are finger-like projections in small intestine, increasing surface area for nutrient absorption into bloodstream.

  10. Differentiate between egestion and excretion. (3 marks)

    Answer: Egestion: Removal of undigested food (feces). Excretion: Removal of metabolic wastes (urine, CO₂).

  11. Describe the structure and function of the human heart. (4 marks)

    Answer: Heart has four chambers (two atria, two ventricles), valves prevent backflow. Function: Pumps oxygenated blood to body, deoxygenated to lungs via double circulation.

  12. What is double circulation? Explain with a diagram description. (3 marks)

    Answer: Blood passes through heart twice per cycle: Pulmonary (lungs) and systemic (body). Description: Right ventricle to lungs, left ventricle to body.

  13. Explain the transport of oxygen in blood. (3 marks)

    Answer: Oxygen binds to hemoglobin in RBCs forming oxyhemoglobin, transported to tissues; dissociates where needed.

  14. What is the lymphatic system? Name its functions. (3 marks)

    Answer: Lymphatic system: Network of vessels and nodes carrying lymph. Functions: Returns tissue fluid to blood, absorbs fats, immunity via lymphocytes.

  15. Describe the mechanism of urine formation in kidneys. (4 marks)

    Answer: Urine formation: Filtration (glomerulus removes wastes), reabsorption (tubules reclaim water/nutrients), secretion (adds wastes). Forms urine.

  16. What is osmoregulation? Explain its importance. (3 marks)

    Answer: Osmoregulation: Maintenance of water/salt balance. Importance: Prevents cell dehydration/shrinking, maintains homeostasis.

  17. Explain the structure of a nephron with a diagram description. (4 marks)

    Answer: Nephron: Bowman's capsule, glomerulus, proximal tubule, loop of Henle, distal tubule, collecting duct. Description: Renal corpuscle for filtration, tubules for reabsorption.

  18. What are the differences between arteries and veins? (3 marks)

    Answer: Arteries: Thick walls, carry oxygenated blood from heart. Veins: Thin walls, valves, carry deoxygenated blood to heart.

  19. Define reflex action and give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Reflex action: Automatic, rapid response to stimulus. Example: Knee-jerk (touch hot object, hand withdraws).

  20. Describe the structure of a neuron. (3 marks)

    Answer: Neuron: Dendrites (receive signals), cell body, axon (transmit signals), synaptic terminals.

  21. What is the central nervous system? Name its components. (3 marks)

    Answer: CNS: Brain and spinal cord. Controls voluntary/involuntary actions, processes information.

  22. Explain the role of hormones in coordination. (4 marks)

    Answer: Hormones: Chemical messengers from glands (e.g., insulin regulates blood sugar, thyroxine controls metabolism).

  23. Differentiate between tropic and nastic movements. (3 marks)

    Answer: Tropic: Directional response to stimulus (e.g., phototropism). Nastic: Non-directional (e.g., touch-me-not).

  24. What is feedback mechanism? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Feedback: Self-regulating process (negative: reduces stimulus; positive: increases). Example: Insulin lowers high blood sugar.

  25. Describe the human reproductive system in males. (4 marks)

    Answer: Male: Testes (produce sperm/testosterone), vas deferens, prostate, penis. Functions: Spermatogenesis, hormone production.

  26. Explain the process of fertilization in humans. (3 marks)

    Answer: Fertilization: Sperm fuses with ovum in fallopian tube, forming zygote.

  27. What is placenta? Describe its functions. (3 marks)

    Answer: Placenta: Organ connecting fetus to uterus. Functions: Nutrient/oxygen supply, waste removal, hormone production.

  28. Differentiate between monozygotic and dizygotic twins. (3 marks)

    Answer: Monozygotic: Identical, from one zygote. Dizygotic: Fraternal, from two zygotes.

  29. Explain the menstrual cycle and its phases. (4 marks)

    Answer: Menstrual cycle: 28 days; Phases: Menstrual (shedding), follicular (egg maturation), ovulation, luteal (corpus luteum).

  30. What is contraception? Name two methods. (3 marks)

    Answer: Contraception: Preventing pregnancy. Methods: Barrier (condom), hormonal (pills).

  31. Describe the inheritance of traits as per Mendel's laws. (4 marks)

    Answer: Mendel's laws: Segregation (alleles separate), independent assortment (genes assort independently). Example: Pea plant traits.

  32. What is a monohybrid cross? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Monohybrid: Cross for one trait. Example: Tall (TT) x short (tt) → 3:1 ratio.

  33. Explain sex determination in humans. (3 marks)

    Answer: Sex determination: XX (female), XY (male). Y chromosome from father decides sex.

  34. What is evolution? Describe Darwin's theory. (4 marks)

    Answer: Evolution: Change in heritable traits over generations. Darwin: Natural selection, survival of fittest.

  35. Differentiate between acquired and inherited traits. (3 marks)

    Answer: Acquired: Gained during life (e.g., muscles from exercise). Inherited: Genetic (e.g., eye color).

  36. What are homologous organs? Give examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Homologous: Similar structure, different function (e.g., human arm, whale flipper).

  37. Explain speciation with an example. (4 marks)

    Answer: Speciation: Formation of new species via reproductive isolation. Example: Darwin's finches on Galapagos.

  38. What is artificial selection? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Artificial selection: Humans breed for desired traits. Example: Different dog breeds from wolves.

  39. Describe the structure of DNA. (3 marks)

    Answer: DNA: Double helix, nucleotides (sugar, phosphate, bases A-T, G-C).

  40. What is biodiversity? Why is it important? (3 marks)

    Answer: Biodiversity: Variety of life forms. Important: Ecosystem stability, medicine, food.

  41. Explain the greenhouse effect and its impact. (4 marks)

    Answer: Greenhouse effect: Gases trap heat, warming Earth. Impact: Global warming, climate change.

  42. What are biodegradable and non-biodegradable wastes? Give examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Biodegradable: Decomposed naturally (e.g., food waste). Non-biodegradable: Not (e.g., plastic).
