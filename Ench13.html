Class 10 English –
“How to Tell Wild Animals” by Carolyn Wells

Introduction (10 Lines)

1. “How to Tell Wild Animals” is a humorous and descriptive poem by Carolyn Wells.

2. It teaches readers how to identify wild animals in a playful way.

3. The poet mixes fun with facts about animals.

4. She describes their looks, sounds, and behaviors.

5. The poem turns dangerous encounters into light-hearted jokes.

6. It covers animals like lions, tigers, leopards, bears, crocodiles, and hyenas.

7. Each stanza gives a “fun test” to identify the animal.

8. The poet uses rhyme and rhythm to keep it lively.

9. Despite the humor, it gives real hints about these creatures.

10. It entertains while teaching the uniqueness of wild animals.

Explanation – 20 Points

  • 1. The poem opens with the suggestion to visit Asian jungles.
  • 2. If a large, tawny beast roars and kills, it is a Lion.
  • 3. The roar is the main way to identify this king of beasts.
  • 4. Next, she describes a striped animal that leaps to eat you—this is the Tiger.
  • 5. Tigers have beautiful stripes and are known for sudden attacks.
  • 6. Then comes the Leopard, which jumps and bites repeatedly.
  • 7. Even after you cry out, the leopard will not stop biting.
  • 8. The poet humorously warns to be sure it’s a leopard by the number of bites.
  • 9. The Bear is recognized by its tight hug.
  • 10. The poet jokes that the bear hugs you to death.
  • 11. Next, she moves to the Crocodile who weeps while eating you.
  • 12. The “crocodile tears” are a famous expression for fake sadness.
  • 13. The Hyena is identified by its loud, mocking laughter.
  • 14. The Chameleon is last—tiny and hard to see.
  • 15. It looks like a lizard but changes its color to match the tree.
  • 16. The poet notes you may not even realize you are looking at it.
  • 17. Each stanza uses rhyme to create a playful tone.
  • 18. The humor softens the danger of meeting wild animals.
  • 19. The poem blends education and entertainment perfectly.
  • 20. It highlights the amazing variety of animals in the wild.

Conclusion (7–8 Lines)

1. Carolyn Wells turns a lesson on wildlife into an enjoyable rhyme.

2. She presents dangerous animals in a friendly and funny manner.

3. The poem shows nature’s diversity with wit and charm.

4. Readers learn to identify animals without fear.

5. It celebrates the uniqueness of each wild creature.

6. The light-hearted tone hides valuable knowledge about wildlife.

7. It encourages curiosity and respect for animals.

8. Overall, the poem mixes fun, facts, and appreciation for nature.

Moral of the Poem

Moral: The poem teaches that every animal is unique and deserves recognition and respect. While humor makes learning enjoyable, it also reminds us to admire wildlife from a safe distance and protect their habitats.
