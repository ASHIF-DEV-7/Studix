Class 10 Science - PYQs (2023)

Q1: What is the chemical formula of water?

A: H₂O

Q2: Define Newton's second law of motion.

A: The rate of change of momentum of an object is directly proportional to the applied force and occurs in the direction of the force.

Q3: What is the SI unit of electric current?

A: Ampere (A)

Q4: Name the process by which plants prepare their food.

A: Photosynthesis

Q5: What is the main function of the respiratory system?

A: To facilitate the exchange of gases (oxygen and carbon dioxide) between the body and the environment.

Q6: What is the law of reflection?

A: The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection.

Q7: What is the difference between speed and velocity?

A: Speed is a scalar quantity representing distance traveled per unit time, while velocity is a vector quantity representing displacement per unit time.

Q8: Define the term 'ecosystem'.

A: An ecosystem is a community of living organisms interacting with each other and their physical environment.

Q9: What is the principle behind the working of a lever?

A: A lever works on the principle of mechanical advantage, where a small effort can move a large load by applying force at a distance from the fulcrum.

Q10: What is the role of chlorophyll in photosynthesis?

A: Chlorophyll absorbs light energy, primarily from the sun, and converts it into chemical energy during photosynthesis.

Q11: What is the difference between conductors and insulators?

A: Conductors allow the flow of electric current, while insulators resist the flow of electric current.

Q12: Define the term 'refraction'.

A: Refraction is the bending of light when it passes from one medium to another of different density.

Q13: What is the function of the digestive system?

A: To break down food into simpler substances for absorption and assimilation by the body.

Q14: What is the difference between renewable and non-renewable resources?

A: Renewable resources can be replenished naturally over time, while non-renewable resources are finite and cannot be replaced once used.

Q15: What is the significance of the ozone layer?

A: The ozone layer protects life on Earth by absorbing harmful ultraviolet radiation from the sun.

Q16: Define the term 'work' in physics.

A: Work is done when a force is applied to an object, and the object moves in the direction of the force.

Q17: What is the role of the circulatory system?

A: To transport nutrients, gases, hormones, and waste products throughout the body.

Q18: What is the difference between a series and parallel circuit?

A: In a series circuit, components are connected end-to-end, while in a parallel circuit, components are connected across common points.

Q19: Define the term 'density'.

A: Density is the mass per unit volume of a substance.

Q20: What is the function of the excretory system?

A: To remove waste products and excess substances from the body to maintain internal balance.

Q21: What is the principle behind the working of a pulley?

A: A pulley works on the principle of changing the direction of the applied force to lift a load more easily.

Q22: What is the role of the nervous system?

A: To coordinate and control the body's activities by transmitting signals between different parts of the body.

Q23: What is the difference between a physical and chemical change?

A: A physical change alters the form or appearance of a substance without changing its composition, while a chemical change results in the formation of new substances.

Q24: Define the term 'energy'.

A: Energy is the capacity to do work or produce heat.

Q25: What is the function of the skeletal system?

A: To provide support, protect internal organs, and facilitate movement.

Q26: What is the difference between an acid and a base?

A: Acids release hydrogen ions (H⁺) in solution, while bases release hydroxide ions (OH⁻).

Q27: Define the term 'magnetism'.

A: Magnetism is the force exerted by magnets when they attract or repel each other.

Q28: What is the role of the immune system?

A: To protect the body from harmful pathogens and foreign substances.

Q29: What is the difference between a conductor and an insulator?

A: A conductor allows the flow of electric current, while an insulator resists it.
