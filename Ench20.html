Class 10 English –
“For Anne Gregory” by Alfred Lord Tennyson

Introduction (10 Lines)

1. “For Anne Gregory” is a short poem by Alfred Lord Tennyson.

2. It expresses the poet’s love and admiration for Anne Gregory.

3. The poem highlights the importance of inner beauty over outer appearance.

4. Tennyson admires Anne’s qualities of heart and mind.

5. He contrasts superficial beauty with moral and emotional worth.

6. The poet believes true love is based on inner qualities.

7. He praises Anne’s simplicity, grace, and intelligence.

8. The poem uses simple and elegant language.

9. It reflects Victorian ideals of love, honor, and virtue.

10. Overall, it emphasizes that real beauty comes from within.

Explanation – 20 Points

  • 1. The poet addresses Anne Gregory directly with admiration.
  • 2. He appreciates her charming personality and moral integrity.
  • 3. Tennyson says external beauty is fleeting and less important.
  • 4. Inner beauty, kindness, and intelligence are lasting qualities.
  • 5. Anne’s good nature brings joy to those around her.
  • 6. The poet contrasts shallow beauty with true worth.
  • 7. He praises Anne’s grace, simplicity, and modesty.
  • 8. Tennyson considers her inner beauty perfect and admirable.
  • 9. The poem is short but filled with deep feelings.
  • 10. It emphasizes emotional and spiritual connection in love.
  • 11. External appearance alone cannot inspire true love.
  • 12. Anne’s intelligence and thoughtfulness are celebrated.
  • 13. The poet highlights the importance of virtue over vanity.
  • 14. He shows admiration for Anne’s honesty and sincerity.
  • 15. The poem is meant to praise character over looks.
  • 16. Tennyson’s tone is respectful and loving.
  • 17. The poet uses simple and elegant diction for clarity.
  • 18. The poem teaches readers to value inner qualities.
  • 19. It reflects Victorian ideals of morality and proper conduct.
  • 20. Overall, the poem encourages appreciation of true beauty.

Conclusion (7–8 Lines)

1. “For Anne Gregory” emphasizes that true beauty lies within.

2. The poem celebrates inner qualities like kindness, intelligence, and virtue.

3. External beauty is temporary and less important.

4. Tennyson admires Anne Gregory for her character and grace.

5. The poem teaches readers to value personality over looks.

6. Love should be based on appreciation of inner worth.

7. Overall, it reflects timeless ideals of love, admiration, and respect.

8. It encourages nurturing good character and appreciating it in others.

Moral / Theme

Moral: The poem teaches that true beauty comes from one’s heart, mind, and virtues. Outer appearance may fade, but inner qualities such as kindness, intelligence, and moral character are everlasting and deserve admiration.
