Footprints Without Feet 2024 PYQs
Q1: Who is the author of 'Footprints Without Feet'?
A: H.G. Wells.
Q2: Who is the main character in the story?
A: Dr. Kemp.
Q3: What scientific discovery is central to the story?
A: The invention of invisibility.
Q4: Who was the invisible man?
A: Griffin, the scientist who turned invisible.
Q5: Where did Dr. Kemp meet the invisible man?
A: At Dr. Kemp's house in Iping.
Q6: What was Griffin's goal as an invisible man?
A: To rule and terrorize people using his invisibility.
Q7: How did Griffin become invisible?
A: By experimenting with chemicals and optics.
Q8: Why did Griffin visit Dr. Kemp?
A: He wanted to recruit Dr. Kemp in his plan of domination.
Q9: What moral lesson does the story teach?
A: Misusing science for selfish purposes leads to disaster.
Q10: Who helped Griffin in the story?
A: Initially, no one helped him willingly; he tried to force Dr. Kemp.
Q11: How did people react to the invisible man in the village?
A: They were terrified and confused because they could not see him.
Q12: What was Griffin’s weakness?
A: His arrogance and overconfidence led to his capture.
Q13: Did Dr. Kemp approve of Griffin's plan?
A: No, he refused to help Griffin.
Q14: How did the story end?
A: Griffin was eventually hunted down and killed.
Q15: What element of suspense is used in the story?
A: The fear of an invisible person and unknown attacks.
Q16: How does the author describe invisibility scientifically?
A: Griffin explains light refraction and optical experiments.
Q17: What is the significance of the title 'Footprints Without Feet'?
A: It symbolizes visible evidence of an invisible being’s actions.
Q18: How did Griffin terrorize the town?
A: He stole, threatened, and attacked while invisible.
Q19: What is Dr. Kemp’s role in the story?
A: He is the witness to Griffin’s actions and morally opposes him.
Q20: What does the story say about power?
A: Power without responsibility is dangerous.
Q21: How does Griffin’s invisibility affect his mental state?
A: It increases his arrogance and sense of superiority.
Q22: How does the village finally react to Griffin?
A: They form groups to hunt him down after repeated attacks.
Q23: What lesson about science is depicted in the story?
A: Science should be used ethically; misuse leads to chaos.
Q24: Who narrates the main events of the story?
A: The story is narrated from Dr. Kemp's perspective.
Q25: Why was Griffin unable to stay hidden forever?
A: His overconfidence and mistakes revealed his presence.
Q26: Mention one conflict in the story.
A: Griffin’s desire for domination versus Dr. Kemp’s moral resistance.
Q27: How does the author create suspense?
A: Through mysterious attacks and unknown identity of the invisible man.
Q28: What is the importance of Dr. Kemp’s quick thinking?
A: It helps to eventually stop Griffin and protect the village.
Q29: How is Griffin described physically before invisibility?
A: He was a scientific-looking man, focused on experiments.
Q30: What does the story suggest about human greed?
A: Greed and desire for power lead to destruction and downfall.
