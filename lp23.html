Class 10 Biology Previous Year Questions (2023) - Life Processes

  1. Explain the process of photosynthesis in plants with a balanced chemical equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Photosynthesis converts CO₂ and H₂O into glucose using sunlight and chlorophyll. Equation: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ (in presence of sunlight and chlorophyll).

  2. What is the role of chlorophyll in photosynthesis? (3 marks)

    Answer: Chlorophyll absorbs sunlight (blue and red wavelengths), converting light energy into chemical energy for glucose synthesis.

  3. Describe the structure of a chloroplast and its role in photosynthesis. (4 marks)

    Answer: Chloroplast has a double membrane, stroma, and grana (thylakoids with chlorophyll). Role: Site of light-dependent (grana) and light-independent (stroma) reactions.

  4. Differentiate between autotrophic and heterotrophic nutrition with examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Autotrophic: Organisms make own food (e.g., plants via photosynthesis). Heterotrophic: Depend on others (e.g., humans eating plants/animals).

  5. Explain the role of stomata in plants. (3 marks)

    Answer: Stomata regulate gas exchange (CO₂ in, O₂ out) and transpiration (water vapor loss) for photosynthesis and cooling.

  6. What is transpiration? Describe its significance in plants. (4 marks)

    Answer: Transpiration is water vapor loss through stomata. Significance: Drives nutrient transport, cools plant, maintains turgor pressure.

  7. Explain the digestion of carbohydrates in the human digestive system. (3 marks)

    Answer: Starch broken down in mouth by salivary amylase to maltose, then in small intestine by pancreatic amylase to glucose for absorption.

  8. Describe the role of the small intestine in digestion and absorption. (4 marks)

    Answer: Small intestine: Bile emulsifies fats, pancreatic enzymes (amylase, lipase, trypsin) digest carbs, fats, proteins; villi absorb nutrients into blood.

  9. What is the function of villi in the small intestine? (3 marks)

    Answer: Villi increase surface area for efficient nutrient absorption into bloodstream.

  10. Explain the role of hydrochloric acid in the stomach. (3 marks)

    Answer: HCl kills bacteria, activates pepsin, and provides acidic medium for protein digestion.

  11. Differentiate between aerobic and anaerobic respiration with examples. (4 marks)

    Answer: Aerobic: Uses O₂, produces CO₂, H₂O, 38 ATP (e.g., human muscle cells). Anaerobic: No O₂, produces lactic acid/ethanol, 2 ATP (e.g., yeast fermentation).

  12. Describe the process of aerobic respiration in humans. (3 marks)

    Answer: Glucose oxidized in mitochondria using O₂, producing CO₂, H₂O, and energy (C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + energy).

  13. What is the role of mitochondria in respiration? (3 marks)

    Answer: Mitochondria are sites of aerobic respiration, producing ATP via Krebs cycle and electron transport chain.

  14. Explain the mechanism of breathing in humans. (4 marks)

    Answer: Inhalation: Diaphragm contracts, chest expands, air enters lungs. Exhalation: Diaphragm relaxes, air expelled. Driven by pressure changes.

  15. What is the role of alveoli in gas exchange? (3 marks)

    Answer: Alveoli provide large surface area for O₂ diffusion into blood and CO₂ removal from blood.

  16. Describe the transport of oxygen in human blood. (3 marks)

    Answer: O₂ binds to hemoglobin in RBCs forming oxyhemoglobin, transported to tissues, and released where needed.

  17. Explain the structure of the human heart and its function. (4 marks)

    Answer: Heart: Four chambers (two atria, two ventricles), valves prevent backflow. Function: Pumps oxygenated blood to body, deoxygenated to lungs via double circulation.

  18. What is double circulation? Explain its importance. (3 marks)

    Answer: Blood passes heart twice per cycle (pulmonary, systemic). Importance: Separates oxygenated/deoxygenated blood, efficient delivery.

  19. Differentiate between arteries and veins in terms of structure and function. (3 marks)

    Answer: Arteries: Thick walls, carry oxygenated blood from heart. Veins: Thin walls, valves, carry deoxygenated blood to heart.

  20. What is the lymphatic system? Describe its role in transport. (4 marks)

    Answer: Lymphatic system: Vessels and nodes carrying lymph. Role: Returns tissue fluid to blood, transports fats, aids immunity.

  21. Explain the transport of food in plants through phloem. (3 marks)

    Answer: Phloem translocates glucose from leaves to other parts via sieve tubes using active transport and pressure flow.

  22. What is the role of xylem in plants? (3 marks)

    Answer: Xylem transports water and minerals from roots to leaves via transpiration pull and capillary action.

  23. Describe the mechanism of urine formation in the human kidney. (4 marks)

    Answer: Urine formation: Filtration (glomerulus removes wastes), reabsorption (tubules reclaim water/nutrients), secretion (adds wastes). Forms urine.

  24. What is a nephron? Explain its role in excretion. (3 marks)

    Answer: Nephron: Functional unit of kidney. Role: Filters blood, reabsorbs essentials, secretes wastes to form urine.

  25. Explain the structure of a nephron with a diagram description. (4 marks)

    Answer: Nephron: Bowman’s capsule, glomerulus, proximal tubule, loop of Henle, distal tubule, collecting duct. Description: Filters blood, reabsorbs nutrients.

  26. What is osmoregulation? Why is it important? (3 marks)

    Answer: Osmoregulation: Maintains water/salt balance. Importance: Prevents cell dehydration/swelling, ensures homeostasis.

  27. Explain the role of kidneys in excretion. (3 marks)

    Answer: Kidneys filter blood, remove urea, excess water, and salts, forming urine to maintain body balance.

  28. What is the significance of dialysis in kidney failure? (3 marks)

    Answer: Dialysis filters blood artificially, removing wastes and excess fluids when kidneys fail.

  29. Describe the process of anaerobic respiration in yeast. (4 marks)

    Answer: Yeast converts glucose to ethanol and CO₂ without O₂ (fermentation): C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2C₂H₅OH + 2CO₂ + energy.

  30. Explain the role of ATP in life processes. (3 marks)

    Answer: ATP (adenosine triphosphate) is energy currency, providing energy for cellular processes like muscle contraction, transport.

  31. What is peristalsis? Explain its role in digestion. (3 marks)

    Answer: Peristalsis: Rhythmic muscle contractions in alimentary canal. Role: Moves food through esophagus, stomach, intestines.

  32. Describe the role of bile in fat digestion. (3 marks)

    Answer: Bile emulsifies fats, breaking them into smaller droplets for lipase enzyme action in small intestine.

  33. Explain the significance of hemoglobin in oxygen transport. (3 marks)

    Answer: Hemoglobin binds O₂ in lungs, forms oxyhemoglobin, and releases O₂ to tissues for respiration.

  34. What are the differences between pulmonary and systemic circulation? (4 marks)

    Answer: Pulmonary: Deoxygenated blood from heart to lungs for oxygenation. Systemic: Oxygenated blood from heart to body tissues.

  35. Explain the process of blood clotting. (3 marks)

    Answer: Platelets release clotting factors, convert fibrinogen to fibrin, forming a mesh to seal wound.

  36. What is the role of guard cells in regulating stomata? (3 marks)

    Answer: Guard cells control stomatal opening/closing by changing turgidity, regulating gas exchange and transpiration.

  37. Describe the digestion of proteins in humans. (4 marks)

    Answer: Proteins digested in stomach by pepsin (into peptides) and in small intestine by trypsin, chymotrypsin into amino acids for absorption.

  38. What is the role of the large intestine in digestion? (3 marks)

    Answer: Large intestine absorbs water from undigested food, forms feces, and eliminates waste.

  39. Explain how plants excrete waste products. (3 marks)

    Answer: Plants excrete via stomata (O₂, CO₂), shedding leaves, or storing wastes in vacuoles/resins.

  40. What is the significance of the respiratory quotient? (4 marks)

    Answer: Respiratory quotient (RQ) is ratio of CO₂ produced to O₂ consumed. Indicates substrate used (e.g., RQ = 1 for glucose).
