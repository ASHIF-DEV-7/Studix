Class 10 English – Poem 2023 PYQ

Previous Year Board Questions & Answers

Q1. From “Dust of Snow”, how does the poet’s mood change?
Ans: Initially gloomy, the poet’s mood becomes cheerful when the crow shakes snow on him, making him realise small events can brighten life.
Q2. What moral is conveyed in “Fire and Ice”?
Ans: Human emotions like intense desire (fire) and hatred (ice) are equally destructive and can end the world.
Q3. Why is the tiger described as “sliding through long grass” in “A Tiger in the Zoo”?
Ans: It highlights the tiger’s natural grace and silent movement in the wild.
Q4. What does “fog comes on little cat feet” mean?
Ans: Fog arrives silently and softly like a cat walking on padded feet.
Q5. Contrast the tiger in the cage and in the wild.
Ans: The caged tiger is restless and helpless; the wild tiger is free, powerful and majestic.
Q6. Theme of “The Ball Poem”?
Ans: Loss is part of growing up; we must accept it and move on without expecting compensation.
Q7. How does “Amanda!” show conflict between child and parent?
Ans: Amanda longs for freedom and imagines escapes while her mother constantly instructs and scolds her.
Q8. Why does the poet admire animals in “Animals”?
Ans: They are calm, content, free from greed, jealousy and hypocrisy, unlike humans.
Q9. Central idea of “Fog”?
Ans: Nature’s quiet but inevitable power is compared to a cat, showing beauty and strength.
Q10. Why does the poet use the word “bricks” in “The Trees”?
Ans: Bricks symbolise man-made barriers that imprison nature inside houses.
Q11. How does “The Trees” depict a struggle of nature against humans?
Ans: Plants try to break walls and reach open air, showing nature’s fight for freedom.
Q12. Lesson of “Fire and Ice” about emotions?
Ans: Both uncontrolled passion and hatred can destroy relationships and society.
Q13. Significance of the ball in “The Ball Poem”?
Ans: The ball symbolises childhood and innocence which once lost cannot be regained.
Q14. Why does Amanda imagine herself as a mermaid?
Ans: She yearns for a peaceful life away from worldly commands, free in the calm sea.
Q15. How is the mood of “Dust of Snow” uplifting?
Ans: A simple natural event removes gloom and brings optimism to the poet.
Q16. What does the poet convey about animals’ lifestyle in “Animals”?
Ans: Animals live a simple, honest life without sins of civilisation and materialism.
Q17. Imagery of fog as a cat?
Ans: The fog’s silent arrival and departure resemble a cat’s stealthy movement and quiet watchfulness.
Q18. How does “A Tiger in the Zoo” highlight human cruelty?
Ans: Captivity of a wild tiger shows man’s dominance and disregard for animal freedom.
Q19. Feelings of the boy in “The Ball Poem”?
Ans: He feels grief and realises he must accept the loss without expecting replacement.
Q20. How does “The Trees” celebrate freedom?
Ans: The escape of trees into open air represents liberation of nature from human control.
Q21. Symbolic meaning of fire in “Fire and Ice”?
Ans: Fire symbolises intense desires and passions of humans.
Q22. What does ice symbolise in “Fire and Ice”?
Ans: Ice stands for hatred and cold indifference that can also destroy the world.
Q23. Message of “Amanda!”?
Ans: Children need space and understanding; over-discipline suppresses imagination.
Q24. Equality between man and animal in “Animals”?
Ans: Animals are content and morally superior; humans should learn equality and honesty from them.
Q25. Character sketch of the tiger in “A Tiger in the Zoo”.
Ans: Majestic and powerful but helpless and angry in captivity.
Q26. Change in poet’s heart after snowfall in “Dust of Snow”?
Ans: His depressed thoughts vanish and he feels optimistic and joyful.
Q27. How does “Fog” present nature’s quiet power?
Ans: Fog silently envelops everything and departs quietly, yet controls the scene completely.
Q28. Why does Amanda imagine herself as Rapunzel?
Ans: She dreams of a quiet life in a tower, free from orders and restrictions.
Q29. Meaning of “I think I could turn and live with animals”?
Ans: Poet wishes to adopt the simple, peaceful life of animals free from human vices.
Q30. Lesson from “The Trees”?
Ans: Nature cannot be confined; it will reclaim its freedom sooner or later.
