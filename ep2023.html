Class 10 Science – Electricity 2023 PYQ

Previous Year Board Questions & Answers

1 Mark Questions

Q1. Define electric current and mention its SI unit.
Ans: Rate of flow of charge; SI unit – Ampere (A).
Q2. Write the formula of resistance in terms of resistivity, length and area.
Ans: R = ρ L/A.
Q3. What is the SI unit of potential difference?
Ans: Volt (V).
Q4. State Ohm’s law.
Ans: V ∝ I at constant temperature; V = IR.
Q5. Name the physical quantity whose unit is coulomb/second.
Ans: Electric current.

2 Mark Questions

Q6. Why is tungsten used almost exclusively for filament of electric lamps?
Ans: High melting point and high resistivity.
Q7. Distinguish between a series and a parallel circuit (any two points).
Ans: Series – same current; Parallel – same voltage across branches etc.
Q8. Draw the circuit diagram of a cell, ammeter, voltmeter and resistor in series.
Ans: [Labelled diagram]
Q9. Calculate the current if 10 C of charge flows in 2 s.
Ans: I = Q/t = 10/2 = 5 A.
Q10. State two safety measures to avoid electric shocks.
Ans: Proper earthing, insulation of wires, fuse etc.

3 Mark Questions

Q11. Three resistors of 5 Ω, 10 Ω, 15 Ω are connected in series. Find total resistance and current if 6 V is applied.
Ans: R = 30 Ω; I = 6/30 = 0.2 A.
Q12. Derive the formula for equivalent resistance of two resistors in parallel.
Ans: 1/R = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ …
Q13. Explain Joule’s law of heating and its practical applications.
Ans: Heat ∝ I²Rt; applications – electric heater, fuse, etc.
Q14. A 60 W bulb is used for 5 h daily. Find total energy consumed in a month (30 days).
Ans: 60×5×30 Wh = 9000 Wh = 9 kWh.
Q15. Two wires of same material, lengths 1 m and 2 m and areas 1 mm² and 2 mm² have resistance R₁ and R₂. Compare R₁ and R₂.
Ans: R ∝ L/A → ratio 2:1.
