The Sermon at Benares — Class 10 Notes

Introduction (10 Lines)

1. “The Sermon at Benares” presents the first sermon of Gautama Buddha.
2. It tells about Buddha’s search for truth and an end to suffering.
3. Born as Prince Siddhartha, he left his palace after seeing sickness, old age and death.
4. He meditated deeply and attained enlightenment under the Bodhi tree.
5. After enlightenment he became the Buddha, “The Awakened One.”
6. His first sermon was delivered at Benares (Varanasi).
7. In it, he explained that life is full of sorrow and suffering.
8. He taught that suffering is caused by desire and attachment.
9. By giving up craving and following the Middle Path, one can achieve peace.
10. The chapter also tells the story of Kisa Gotami to show Buddha’s message.

Chapter Explanation (20 Points)

  • 1. Gautama Buddha was born as Prince Siddhartha in a royal family.

  • 2. He lived a sheltered life but once saw sickness, old age and death.

  • 3. These sights made him think about human suffering.

  • 4. At the age of 25 he left his palace, wife and son in search of truth.

  • 5. He wandered, met holy men and practiced meditation.

  • 6. After years of deep meditation he attained enlightenment at Bodh Gaya.

  • 7. He became Buddha — the Enlightened One.

  • 8. His first sermon was given at Benares, an important holy city.

  • 9. Buddha explained that life is full of sorrow and suffering (dukkha).

  • 10. The cause of suffering is craving, desire and attachment.

  • 11. Suffering ends when desire is removed.

  • 12. To end desire one must follow the Middle Path (Noble Eightfold Path).

  • 13. Right belief, thought, speech, action, livelihood, effort, mindfulness and meditation are its steps.

  • 14. The story of Kisa Gotami illustrates this teaching.

  • 15. Kisa Gotami’s only child died, and she was full of grief.

  • 16. She begged Buddha to bring her child back to life.

  • 17. Buddha asked her to bring a handful of mustard seeds from a house where no one had died.

  • 18. She went door to door but every house had faced death.

  • 19. She realised that death is universal and unavoidable.

  • 20. She understood Buddha’s message: acceptance of death brings peace.

Conclusion (7–8 Lines)

The Sermon at Benares teaches that death is a natural law of life.

Every living being must face old age, sickness and death.

Running away from sorrow or begging for miracles cannot stop this truth.

Real peace comes from accepting reality and ending desires.

Buddha’s Middle Path shows a way to live with balance and compassion.

The story of Kisa Gotami proves that suffering is shared by all humans.

By understanding this, we become calm, wise and free from unnecessary pain.

Moral / Theme

  • Accept Death: Death is universal and cannot be avoided.

  • End Desire: Craving and attachment cause suffering.

  • Middle Path: Balance in life leads to peace and enlightenment.

  • Compassion: Understanding others’ sorrow creates kindness and wisdom.
