Class 10 Physics Previous Year Questions (2022) with Answers

  1. What is the SI unit of force? (1 mark)

    Answer: Newton (N)

  2. Define one newton. (1 mark)

    Answer: One newton is the force that produces an acceleration of 1 m/s² on a mass of 1 kg.

  3. What is the effect of balanced forces on a stationary object? (1 mark)

    Answer: The object remains stationary.

  4. Which law of motion defines inertia? (1 mark)

    Answer: Newton’s first law of motion.

  5. Calculate the momentum of a 5 kg object moving at 2 m/s. (2 marks)

    Answer: Momentum = mass × velocity = 5 kg × 2 m/s = 10 kg·m/s.

  6. What is the difference between distance and displacement? (2 marks)

    Answer: Distance is the total path length covered, a scalar quantity. Displacement is the shortest path between initial and final positions, a vector quantity.

  7. A car accelerates uniformly from rest to 20 m/s in 5 seconds. Calculate its acceleration. (2 marks)

    Answer: Acceleration = (final velocity - initial velocity) / time = (20 m/s - 0 m/s) / 5 s = 4 m/s².

  8. State Newton’s second law of motion. (2 marks)

    Answer: The rate of change of momentum of an object is directly proportional to the applied force and occurs in the direction of the force. F = ma.

  9. What is the role of friction in walking? (2 marks)

    Answer: Friction between the foot and ground prevents slipping, allowing us to push forward and walk.

  10. Define work done in physics. (1 mark)

    Answer: Work is done when a force causes displacement in its direction. Work = force × displacement × cosθ.

  11. Calculate the work done when a force of 10 N moves an object 5 m in the direction of the force. (2 marks)

    Answer: Work = force × displacement = 10 N × 5 m = 50 J.

  12. What is the commercial unit of energy? (1 mark)

    Answer: Kilowatt-hour (kWh).

  13. Convert 1 kWh to joules. (2 marks)

    Answer: 1 kWh = 1000 W × 3600 s = 3.6 × 10⁶ J.

  14. What is the power of a machine that does 1000 J of work in 10 seconds? (2 marks)

    Answer: Power = work / time = 1000 J / 10 s = 100 W.

  15. Define kinetic energy and give its formula. (2 marks)

    Answer: Kinetic energy is the energy of an object due to its motion. Formula: KE = ½ mv².

  16. What is the law of conservation of energy? (2 marks)

    Answer: Energy cannot be created or destroyed; it can only be transformed from one form to another.

  17. What is the SI unit of electric current? (1 mark)

    Answer: Ampere (A).

  18. Define electric potential difference. (1 mark)

    Answer: Electric potential difference is the work done per unit charge to move a charge between two points.

  19. State Ohm’s law. (1 mark)

    Answer: The current through a conductor is directly proportional to the potential difference across it, provided temperature remains constant. V = IR.

  20. Calculate the resistance of a conductor when 2 A current flows through it with a potential difference of 4 V. (2 marks)

    Answer: Resistance = V / I = 4 V / 2 A = 2 Ω.

  21. What is the difference between series and parallel circuits? (2 marks)

    Answer: In a series circuit, components are connected end-to-end, with the same current flowing through all. In a parallel circuit, components are connected across the same voltage, with current dividing among branches.

  22. Why do electric bulbs glow when current passes through them? (2 marks)

    Answer: The filament in the bulb heats up due to the heating effect of current, becoming incandescent and emitting light.

  23. What is the heating effect of electric current? (1 mark)

    Answer: When current flows through a conductor, it produces heat due to resistance. Heat = I²Rt.

  24. Calculate the heat produced in a 5 Ω resistor when 3 A current flows for 10 seconds. (2 marks)

    Answer: Heat = I²Rt = (3 A)² × 5 Ω × 10 s = 9 × 5 × 10 = 450 J.

  25. What is the function of a fuse in an electric circuit? (1 mark)

    Answer: A fuse prevents excessive current by melting and breaking the circuit, protecting devices from damage.

  26. Define magnetic field. (1 mark)

    Answer: A magnetic field is the region around a magnet where magnetic forces can be detected.

  27. What is the direction of magnetic field lines around a current-carrying conductor? (2 marks)

    Answer: The magnetic field lines form concentric circles around the conductor, with direction given by the right-hand thumb rule.

  28. State Fleming’s left-hand rule. (2 marks)

    Answer: When the thumb, forefinger, and middle finger of the left hand are held perpendicular to each other, the forefinger points in the direction of the magnetic field, the middle finger in the direction of current, and the thumb shows the direction of force on the conductor.

  29. What is electromagnetic induction? (2 marks)

    Answer: Electromagnetic induction is the production of an electric current in a conductor due to a changing magnetic field.

  30. Name the device that converts mechanical energy to electrical energy. (1 mark)

    Answer: Electric generator.

  31. What is the difference between a bar magnet and an electromagnet? (2 marks)

    Answer: A bar magnet has a permanent magnetic field, while an electromagnet’s field is created by electric current and can be turned off.

  32. What are the properties of light? (2 marks)

    Answer: Light travels in straight lines, can be reflected, refracted, and undergoes dispersion.

  33. Define reflection of light. (1 mark)

    Answer: Reflection is the bouncing back of light when it strikes a surface.

  34. State the laws of reflection. (2 marks)

    Answer: 1. The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection. 2. The incident ray, reflected ray, and normal at the point of incidence lie in the same plane.

  35. What is the focal length of a plane mirror? (1 mark)

    Answer: The focal length of a plane mirror is infinity.

  36. Define refraction of light. (1 mark)

    Answer: Refraction is the bending of light as it passes from one medium to another with a different refractive index.

  37. What is the refractive index of a medium? (1 mark)

    Answer: Refractive index is the ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum to the speed of light in the medium.

  38. Why does a pencil appear bent in water? (2 marks)

    Answer: The pencil appears bent due to refraction of light as it passes from water to air, changing its speed and direction.

  39. What is the power of a lens with a focal length of 50 cm? (2 marks)

    Answer: Power = 1 / focal length (in meters) = 1 / 0.5 m = 2 dioptres.

  40. What is dispersion of light? Give an example. (2 marks)

    Answer: Dispersion is the splitting of white light into its constituent colors due to different refractive indices. Example: Formation of a rainbow.
