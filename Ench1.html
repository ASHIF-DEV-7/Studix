A Letter to God - Class 10 Notes

Introduction

1. "A Letter to God" is a story written by Gregorio López y Fuentes.
2. The story is set in a poor village where farming is the main source of income.
3. Lencho is the protagonist, a hardworking farmer with strong faith in God.
4. He has a family of six members, all living in a small house.
5. The story begins during a drought, when Lencho hopes for rain.
6. One day, heavy hail destroys his entire crop.
7. Lencho becomes worried as the crops are his only source of income.
8. Despite the misfortune, he decides to write a letter to God asking for help.
9. The story highlights human faith, hope, and irony.
10. It also shows kindness and generosity of people who help him indirectly.

Chapter Explanation

  • 1. Lencho is a simple, poor farmer.
  • 2. He owns a small house and some land for farming.
  • 3. The family depends entirely on their crops for livelihood.
  • 4. The story is set in Mexico.
  • 5. The story starts during a drought when the crops need rain.
  • 6. One Sunday, Lencho hopes for a good rainfall.
  • 7. Unexpectedly, a heavy hailstorm destroys the crops.
  • 8. Lencho becomes sad and worried about the future.
  • 9. He writes a letter to God requesting 100 pesos to re-sow the field.
  • 10. The letter is addressed to God and left in the mailbox.
  • 11. The post office employees find the letter.
  • 12. They are impressed by Lencho’s faith in God.
  • 13. The employees decide to help him by collecting money.
  • 14. They manage to collect 70 pesos instead of 100.
  • 15. The money is sent to Lencho without revealing the source.
  • 16. Lencho receives the money and counts it carefully.
  • 17. He notices only 70 pesos instead of 100.
  • 18. Lencho is convinced that the post office employees kept the rest.
  • 19. He trusts God completely and never doubts divine help.
  • 20. The story ends with irony showing Lencho’s faith vs human effort.

Conclusion

The story “A Letter to God” teaches us about faith, hope, and human nature.
Despite losing everything, Lencho never loses his faith in God.
The story shows that even in hard times, hope and belief can keep a person strong.
It also highlights the kindness of humans who help others without expecting anything in return.
The irony in the story adds humor and depth, making it more engaging.
The story reminds us that faith can give strength in adversity.
It teaches us to trust both God and the goodness in people around us.
Overall, it is a story of resilience, faith, and human generosity.

Moral / Theme

  • Faith in God and hope during difficult times can keep us strong.
  • Human kindness and generosity can make a big difference in someone’s life.
  • Irony in human behavior adds humor but also teaches important lessons.
  • Trust and resilience are essential traits during adversity.
