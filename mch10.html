CIRCLE

Chapter 10 - Class 10 CBSE Mathematics

1. Introduction to Circle

Circle: A circle is the collection of all points in a plane which are at a constant distance (radius) from a fixed point (center).

Important Terms:

  • Radius: The constant distance from center to any point on circle
  • Chord: A line segment joining two points on the circle
  • Diameter: A chord passing through the center (longest chord)
  • Arc: A continuous piece of a circle
  • Segment: Region between a chord and arc
  • Sector: Region between two radii and an arc
  • Secant: A line intersecting the circle at two distinct points

2. Tangent to a Circle

Tangent: A tangent to a circle is a line that touches the circle at exactly one point. This point is called the point of contact or point of tangency.

Key Points about Tangents:

  • A tangent touches the circle at only one point
  • A secant intersects the circle at two points
  • From an external point, exactly two tangents can be drawn to a circle
  • No tangent can be drawn from a point inside the circle
  • Only one tangent can be drawn at any point on a circle

3. Important Theorems

Theorem 10.1: The tangent at any point of a circle is perpendicular to the radius through the point of contact.

Proof Concept: If a tangent is not perpendicular to radius, then it would intersect the circle at another point, contradicting the definition of tangent.
Theorem 10.2 (Converse): If a line is perpendicular to a radius at its outer endpoint, then it is a tangent to the circle.
Theorem 10.3: The lengths of tangents drawn from an external point to a circle are equal.

Given: Two tangents PA and PB are drawn from external point P to circle with center O.
To Prove: PA = PB
Proof: In triangles OAP and OBP:
  • OA = OB (radii)
  • OP = OP (common)
  • ∠OAP = ∠OBP = 90° (tangent ⊥ radius)
  • Therefore, △OAP ≅ △OBP (RHS congruency)
  • Hence, PA = PB (CPCT)

4. Properties of Tangents from External Point

If two tangents are drawn from an external point P to a circle with center O, touching at points A and B:

  • PA = PB (tangent lengths are equal)
  • ∠OPA = ∠OPB (angle subtended by tangents are equal)
  • ∠APB + ∠AOB = 180° (opposite angles are supplementary)
  • OP is the angle bisector of ∠APB
  • OP is perpendicular bisector of chord AB

5. Number of Tangents from a Point

  • Point inside the circle: No tangent can be drawn
  • Point on the circle: Exactly one tangent can be drawn (perpendicular to radius at that point)
  • Point outside the circle: Exactly two tangents can be drawn

6. Important Formulas

Length of tangent from external point = √(d² - r²)
where d = distance from center to external point, r = radius
If two tangents PA and PB are drawn from P, and ∠APB = θ, then:
∠AOB = 180° - θ

7. Common Tangent

Common Tangent: A line that is tangent to two or more circles.

Types of Common Tangents:

  • Direct (External) Common Tangent: Does not cross the line joining centers
  • Transverse (Internal) Common Tangent: Crosses the line joining centers

Number of Common Tangents:

  • Two circles don't touch or intersect: 4 common tangents (2 direct + 2 transverse)
  • Two circles touch externally: 3 common tangents (2 direct + 1 transverse)
  • Two circles intersect at two points: 2 common tangents (2 direct only)
  • Two circles touch internally: 1 common tangent (at point of contact)
  • One circle inside another: 0 common tangents

8. Solved Examples

Example 1: A tangent PQ at point P of a circle of radius 5 cm meets a line through center O at Q such that OQ = 13 cm. Find length of PQ.

Solution:
OP ⊥ PQ (tangent perpendicular to radius)
In right triangle OPQ:
OQ² = OP² + PQ² (Pythagoras theorem)
13² = 5² + PQ²
169 = 25 + PQ²
PQ² = 144
PQ = 12 cm
Example 2: Two tangents PA and PB are drawn from external point P. If ∠APB = 60°, find ∠AOB.

Solution:
∠APB + ∠AOB = 180° (opposite angles supplementary)
60° + ∠AOB = 180°
∠AOB = 120°
Example 3: Prove that the parallelogram circumscribing a circle is a rhombus.

Solution:
Let ABCD be a parallelogram circumscribing circle with center O.
Tangents from external point are equal:
From A: AP = AS
From B: BP = BQ
From C: CQ = CR
From D: DR = DS

AB + CD = AP + BP + CR + DR = AS + BQ + CQ + DS
Also, AB + CD = BC + DA (opposite sides of parallelogram)
Therefore, AB = BC = CD = DA
Hence, ABCD is a rhombus.

9. Important Results to Remember

  • The perpendicular from center to a chord bisects the chord
  • Equal chords are equidistant from center
  • The angle subtended by an arc at center is double the angle subtended at any point on remaining circle
  • Angles in the same segment are equal
  • Angle in a semicircle is a right angle
  • Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral are supplementary (sum = 180°)

10. Quick Tips for Exam

  • Always draw a neat diagram showing center, radius, tangent, and point of contact
  • Mark right angles between radius and tangent
  • Use Pythagoras theorem in problems involving tangent length
  • Remember: tangent ⊥ radius at point of contact
  • Tangents from external point are equal - use this for congruency proofs
  • For construction problems, use perpendicular at point of contact method
  • Check if cyclic quadrilateral properties can be applied

11. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't assume a line touching circle is tangent - verify it touches at only one point
  • Radius is perpendicular to tangent at point of contact, not anywhere else
  • Don't confuse chord with tangent - chord has both endpoints on circle
  • Equal tangent lengths only apply when drawn from same external point
  • Remember to mention all given conditions and theorems used in proofs

Study these concepts thoroughly and practice questions from NCERT and exemplar books for better understanding.
