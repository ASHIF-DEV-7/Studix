Nelson Mandela: Long Walk to Freedom – Class 10 Notes

Introduction (10 Lines)

1. This extract is from Nelson Mandela’s autobiography *Long Walk to Freedom*.
2. It describes the historic inauguration of South Africa’s first democratic government in 1994.
3. Nelson Mandela became the country’s first black President after years of apartheid.
4. Leaders and dignitaries from around the world attended the grand ceremony in Pretoria.
5. Mandela reflects on decades of racial segregation and the struggles of freedom fighters.
6. He acknowledges the sacrifice of countless men and women who fought against injustice.
7. Mandela emphasizes the ideals of equality, liberty, and human dignity.
8. He highlights that courage is not the absence of fear but the triumph over it.
9. The chapter inspires readers to value democracy and human rights.
10. It stands as a powerful lesson on forgiveness and rebuilding a divided nation.

Chapter Explanation (20 Points)

  • 1. The chapter opens with the grand ceremony on 10 May 1994 in Pretoria.
  • 2. Nelson Mandela takes the oath as the first black President of South Africa.
  • 3. World leaders from different countries witness the historic moment.
  • 4. It symbolizes the victory of justice over decades of racial oppression.
  • 5. Mandela remembers all freedom fighters, many of whom did not live to see the day.
  • 6. He stresses that true liberty means freedom from both oppression and hatred.
  • 7. He recalls the oppressive apartheid system that divided people by colour.
  • 8. Mandela explains how racial discrimination robbed both blacks and whites of humanity.
  • 9. He praises the courage and discipline of the African National Congress and other activists.
  • 10. Mandela shares that courage is not absence of fear but the ability to conquer it.
  • 11. He learnt that a brave man is one who can face fear and still move forward.
  • 12. Mandela expresses gratitude to his colleagues and fellow South Africans.
  • 13. He highlights the importance of unity among different races and communities.
  • 14. The ceremony included military displays and singing of the national anthem.
  • 15. Mandela believes every man is born free but prejudice creates bondage.
  • 16. He admits that even oppressors are not truly free; hatred imprisons everyone.
  • 17. Forgiveness and reconciliation, not revenge, are the keys to a better future.
  • 18. Mandela dreams of a society where all people live with equal opportunities.
  • 19. He acknowledges the long struggle that demanded immense sacrifices.
  • 20. The event marks the dawn of a new, democratic and peaceful South Africa.

Conclusion (7–8 Lines)

Nelson Mandela’s journey teaches us that real freedom is more than the end of political oppression. It is freedom from fear, hatred and prejudice. The inauguration of a democratic South Africa shows the strength of unity and the power of forgiveness. Mandela’s leadership proves that patience and courage can break the strongest chains of injustice. His decision to reconcile rather than seek revenge set a global example of humanity. The chapter inspires us to value our rights and stand against any form of discrimination. It reminds us that the road to freedom is long, but determination makes it possible. Mandela’s life remains a guiding light for equality and peace.

Moral / Theme

  • Freedom and equality require sacrifice and perseverance.
  • Courage means overcoming fear, not the absence of it.
  • Forgiveness and reconciliation heal a nation more than revenge.
  • Unity among diverse people is the foundation of real democracy.
