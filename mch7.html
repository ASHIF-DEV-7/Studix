1. Introduction to Coordinate Geometry

Coordinate geometry, also known as Analytical Geometry, is a branch of mathematics that describes the position of points on a plane using an ordered pair of numbers called coordinates.

The Cartesian Coordinate System

The Cartesian coordinate system was introduced by the French mathematician Ren√© Descartes in the 17th century. It consists of two perpendicular lines called axes:

  • X-axis: The horizontal line
  • Y-axis: The vertical line
  • Origin (O): The point of intersection of both axes, denoted as (0, 0)

Components of the Coordinate System:

  • Quadrants: The axes divide the plane into four parts called quadrants
  • Coordinates: Any point P in the plane is represented as P(x, y) where x is the abscissa and y is the ordinate

The Four Quadrants

Quadrant Position x-coordinate y-coordinate Example
I Top Right Positive (+) Positive (+) (3, 4)
II Top Left Negative (‚àí) Positive (+) (‚àí2, 5)
III Bottom Left Negative (‚àí) Negative (‚àí) (‚àí3, ‚àí4)
IV Bottom Right Positive (+) Negative (‚àí) (5, ‚àí2)
Important Note: Points lying on the x-axis have y-coordinate = 0, and points lying on the y-axis have x-coordinate = 0.

Applications of Coordinate Geometry

Coordinate geometry is widely used in:

  • Physics and Engineering for motion analysis
  • Computer Graphics and Game Development
  • Navigation and GPS systems
  • Architecture and Design
  • Astronomy and Space Science

2. Distance Formula

The distance formula calculates the distance between two points in a coordinate plane. It is derived using the Pythagorean theorem.

Step-by-Step Process

  2. Find the difference in y-coordinates: (y‚ÇÇ ‚àí y‚ÇÅ)
  3. Square both differences
  4. Add the squared values
  5. Take the square root of the sum

3. Section Formula

The section formula is used to find the coordinates of a point that divides a line segment joining two given points in a given ratio.

Internal Division

When a point P divides the line segment joining points A(x‚ÇÅ, y‚ÇÅ) and B(x‚ÇÇ, y‚ÇÇ) internally in the ratio m:n:

P(x, y) = ((mx‚ÇÇ + nx‚ÇÅ)/(m + n), (my‚ÇÇ + ny‚ÇÅ)/(m + n))

When a point P divides the line segment externally in the ratio m:n:

P(x, y) = ((mx‚ÇÇ ‚àí nx‚ÇÅ)/(m ‚àí n), (my‚ÇÇ ‚àí ny‚ÇÅ)/(m ‚àí n))
Important: For external division, m ‚â† n. If m = n, the point lies at infinity.
Example 4: Internal Division

Problem: Find the coordinates of the point P that divides the line segment joining A(2, 3) and B(8, 9) in the ratio 2:1 internally.

Solution:

Example 5: Finding Ratio

Problem: Point P(4, 5) divides the line segment joining A(2, 3) and B(7, 8) internally. Find the ratio in which it divides.

Solution:

Let the ratio be m:n

Using x-coordinate: 4 = (7m + 2n)/(m + n)

4(m + n) = 7m + 2n

4m + 4n = 7m + 2n

2n = 3m

m/n = 2/3

Therefore, the ratio is 2:3

4. Midpoint Formula

The midpoint formula is a special case of the section formula where the ratio is 1:1. It finds the exact middle point of a line segment.

Midpoint M = ((x‚ÇÅ + x‚ÇÇ)/2, (y‚ÇÅ + y‚ÇÇ)/2)

This formula simply takes the average of the x-coordinates and y-coordinates of the two endpoints.

Solution:

Problem: The midpoint of a line segment is M(5, 3). If one endpoint is A(2, 1), find the other endpoint B.

Solution:

Problem: Find the centroid of a triangle with vertices A(0, 0), B(6, 0), and C(3, 6).

Solution:

Properties of Midpoint:

  • The midpoint is equidistant from both endpoints
  • It divides the line segment in the ratio 1:1
  • Midpoint always lies on the line segment
  • Used to find the center of a line segment

6. Collinearity of Points

Three or more points are said to be collinear if they lie on the same straight line.

Condition for Collinearity

Three points A(x‚ÇÅ, y‚ÇÅ), B(x‚ÇÇ, y‚ÇÇ), and C(x‚ÇÉ, y‚ÇÉ) are collinear if and only if:

Alternative Methods to Check Collinearity

Method 1: Using Area Formula

If area of triangle = 0, points are collinear

Method 2: Using Distance

If AB + BC = AC (or any similar combination), points are collinear

Method 3: Using Slope

If slope of AB = slope of BC, points are collinear
