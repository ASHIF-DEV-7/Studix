Class 10 Biology Previous Year Questions (2023) - Control and Coordination

  1. Explain the role of the nervous system in control and coordination in animals. (3 marks)

    Answer: Nervous system coordinates body functions by transmitting signals via neurons, controlling responses to stimuli (e.g., muscle movement, reflexes).

  2. Describe the structure of a neuron with a diagram description. (4 marks)

    Answer: Neuron: Dendrites (receive signals), cell body (nucleus), axon (transmits signals), synaptic terminals. Diagram: Branched dendrites, long axon, terminal knobs.

  3. What is a reflex action? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Reflex action is a rapid, involuntary response to a stimulus. Example: Withdrawing hand from a hot surface.

  4. Explain the reflex arc with its components. (4 marks)

    Answer: Reflex arc: Pathway of reflex action. Components: Receptor (detects stimulus), sensory neuron, spinal cord (interneuron), motor neuron, effector (muscle/gland).

  5. What is the role of the spinal cord in the nervous system? (3 marks)

    Answer: Spinal cord relays signals between brain and body, mediates reflex actions, and controls involuntary responses.

  6. Describe the functions of the human brain. (4 marks)

    Answer: Brain: Cerebrum (thinking, memory), cerebellum (balance, coordination), medulla (heart rate, breathing), controls voluntary/involuntary actions.

  7. Differentiate between voluntary and involuntary actions with examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Voluntary: Controlled consciously (e.g., writing). Involuntary: Automatic (e.g., heartbeat).

  8. What is the role of the synapse in nerve impulse transmission? (3 marks)

    Answer: Synapse transfers impulses between neurons via neurotransmitters (e.g., acetylcholine) released across synaptic cleft.

  9. Explain the role of the peripheral nervous system. (3 marks)

    Answer: Peripheral nervous system connects CNS to body, relaying sensory and motor signals for coordination.

  10. What is the autonomic nervous system? Describe its functions. (4 marks)

    Answer: Autonomic nervous system controls involuntary actions (e.g., heart rate, digestion). Functions: Sympathetic (fight/flight), parasympathetic (rest/digest).

  11. Explain how nerve impulses are transmitted across neurons. (3 marks)

    Answer: Impulse travels as electrical signal along axon, triggers neurotransmitter release at synapse, stimulating next neuron.

  12. What is the role of the cerebrum in the human brain? (3 marks)

    Answer: Cerebrum controls voluntary actions, memory, reasoning, and sensory processing.

  13. Describe the function of the cerebellum. (3 marks)

    Answer: Cerebellum coordinates muscle movements, maintains balance, and fine-tunes motor skills.

  14. Explain the role of the medulla oblongata in vital functions. (3 marks)

    Answer: Medulla controls involuntary functions like heart rate, breathing, and swallowing.

  15. What are hormones? Explain their role in coordination. (4 marks)

    Answer: Hormones are chemical messengers from endocrine glands. Role: Regulate growth, metabolism, reproduction (e.g., insulin controls blood sugar).

  16. Name four endocrine glands and their hormones. (3 marks)

    Answer: Thyroid (thyroxine), pancreas (insulin), adrenal (adrenaline), pituitary (growth hormone).

  17. Explain the role of insulin in the human body. (3 marks)

    Answer: Insulin regulates blood glucose by facilitating glucose uptake into cells, reducing blood sugar levels.

  18. What is the feedback mechanism in hormone regulation? Give an example. (4 marks)

    Answer: Feedback mechanism maintains hormone levels. Example: Negative feedbackâ€”high glucose triggers insulin release, lowering glucose.

  19. Explain the role of adrenaline in the body. (3 marks)

    Answer: Adrenaline increases heart rate, blood flow to muscles, and energy availability during stress (fight-or-flight).

  20. What is the function of the thyroid gland? Describe its hormone. (3 marks)

    Answer: Thyroid secretes thyroxine, regulating metabolism, growth, and energy use.

  21. Describe the role of the pituitary gland in coordination. (4 marks)

    Answer: Pituitary, the master gland, secretes hormones (e.g., growth hormone, TSH) controlling other glands and body functions.

  22. What is diabetes mellitus? Explain its cause. (3 marks)

    Answer: Diabetes mellitus: High blood sugar due to insufficient insulin or insulin resistance.

  23. Explain the role of growth hormone in humans. (3 marks)

    Answer: Growth hormone promotes cell division, bone growth, and tissue development in children and adults.

  24. What are tropic movements in plants? Give two examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Tropic movements are directional responses to stimuli. Examples: Phototropism (growth towards light), geotropism (roots towards gravity).

  25. Explain phototropism with an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Phototropism: Plant growth towards light due to auxin distribution. Example: Sunflower stem bending towards sunlight.

  26. Describe geotropism and its significance in plants. (4 marks)

    Answer: Geotropism: Growth in response to gravity (roots downward, shoots upward). Significance: Anchors roots, optimizes light capture for shoots.

  27. What is hydrotropism? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Hydrotropism: Growth towards water. Example: Roots growing towards a water source in soil.

  28. Explain chemotropism in plants with an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Chemotropism: Growth towards chemicals. Example: Pollen tube grows towards ovule chemicals during fertilization.

  29. What are nastic movements? Differentiate from tropic movements. (4 marks)

    Answer: Nastic movements: Non-directional responses to stimuli (e.g., touch-me-not folding). Tropic: Directional (e.g., phototropism).

  30. Explain the role of auxins in plant growth. (3 marks)

    Answer: Auxins promote cell elongation, root growth, and tropic responses (e.g., bending in phototropism).

  31. What is the role of gibberellins in plants? (3 marks)

    Answer: Gibberellins promote stem elongation, seed germination, and fruit development.

  32. Explain the function of cytokinins in plant growth. (3 marks)

    Answer: Cytokinins promote cell division, delay leaf aging, and enhance growth in tissues.

  33. Describe the role of abscisic acid in plants. (3 marks)

    Answer: Abscisic acid induces dormancy, closes stomata during water stress, and inhibits growth.

  34. What is the significance of plant hormones in agriculture? (4 marks)

    Answer: Plant hormones enhance crop yield, promote rooting (auxins), fruit ripening (ethylene), and stress resistance (abscisic acid).

  35. Explain the mechanism of touch-me-not plant movement. (3 marks)

    Answer: Touch triggers turgor pressure changes in pulvinus, causing leaves to fold (seismonastic movement).

  36. What is the role of the hypothalamus in hormone regulation? (3 marks)

    Answer: Hypothalamus regulates pituitary gland by releasing hormones, controlling body functions like hunger, temperature.

  37. Explain the coordination between nervous and endocrine systems. (4 marks)

    Answer: Nervous system provides rapid control via impulses; endocrine system uses hormones for slower, sustained responses. Example: Adrenaline release during stress.

  38. What is the role of the adrenal medulla in stress response? (3 marks)

    Answer: Adrenal medulla secretes adrenaline, increasing heart rate and energy for fight-or-flight response.

  39. Describe the importance of feedback mechanisms in maintaining homeostasis. (4 marks)

    Answer: Feedback mechanisms regulate body functions (e.g., negative feedback: insulin lowers blood sugar; positive feedback: oxytocin during childbirth).

  40. Explain the role of plant movements in survival. (3 marks)

    Answer: Plant movements optimize light capture (phototropism), water access (hydrotropism), and protection (nastic movements).
