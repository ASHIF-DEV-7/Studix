Class 10 English –
“A Tiger in the Zoo” by Leslie Norris

Introduction (10 Lines)

1. “A Tiger in the Zoo” is a powerful poem by Leslie Norris.

2. It contrasts the life of a tiger in a cage with its life in the wild.

3. The poem shows the beauty and strength of the tiger.

4. It also highlights the pain of captivity.

5. The poet uses simple language but deep emotions.

6. He describes the tiger’s majestic movements in the forest.

7. Then he shows the restricted life inside a concrete cell.

8. The contrast creates sympathy for the animal.

9. The poem is both descriptive and thought-provoking.

10. It is a plea for freedom and respect for wildlife.

Explanation – 20 Points

  • 1. The poem opens with the tiger pacing in a cage.
  • 2. His “velvet quiet” pads show grace and suppressed power.
  • 3. He is angry but silent, symbolizing helpless strength.
  • 4. The cage limits his natural instinct to roam freely.
  • 5. The poet imagines the tiger in the wild forest.
  • 6. There, he would lurk in shadows to stalk deer.
  • 7. The tiger would slide through long grass with deadly skill.
  • 8. His freedom would reflect the true spirit of nature.
  • 9. Back in the zoo, he moves on concrete, not soft earth.
  • 10. Visitors stare at him, but he ignores them.
  • 11. His world is small, dull, and lifeless.
  • 12. At night he hears the sounds of the forest far away.
  • 13. He hears the patrolling cars and city noises.
  • 14. The sounds remind him of freedom lost.
  • 15. He longs to hunt and live naturally.
  • 16. The poet criticizes humans for caging wild animals.
  • 17. The tiger’s silent rage reflects suppressed power of nature.
  • 18. The poem creates empathy for all animals in captivity.
  • 19. It also warns humans about disturbing natural balance.
  • 20. The ending leaves readers thoughtful and sad.

Conclusion (7–8 Lines)

1. Leslie Norris shows the sharp difference between freedom and captivity.

2. The caged tiger’s silent movements speak louder than words.

3. The poem reminds us that wild animals belong in their natural homes.

4. Human interference destroys their dignity and strength.

5. The tiger’s quiet anger symbolizes nature’s suppressed power.

6. It is a call to treat animals with compassion and respect.

7. Freedom is essential for all living beings, not just humans.

8. The message stays relevant in every age of wildlife conservation.

Moral of the Poem

Moral: Wild animals should live free in their natural habitats. Captivity causes pain, destroys dignity, and disturbs the balance of nature. Humans must protect forests and allow animals the life they deserve.
