Class X English Language & Literature - First Flight (2023)

Prose Questions
1. What was Lencho's faith in God? How did it get justified?
Lencho had unwavering faith in God. He believed God would provide him with help in his time of need. His faith was justified when he received money in the mail, though he mistakenly thought the post office employees had stolen some of it.
2. How did the post office employees help Lencho?
The post office employees collected money from their own pockets to help Lencho after seeing his letter to God asking for financial assistance.
3. What does Nelson Mandela remember about the first decade of his life?
Nelson Mandela remembers that he was free to do as he pleased during the first decade of his life. He felt free to run in the fields, swim in the streams, and roast mealies under the stars.
4. What did being free mean to Mandela as a boy?
As a boy, being free to Mandela meant being able to run in the fields, swim in the clear streams, roast mealies under the stars, and ride the broad backs of slow-moving bulls.
5. Why does Anne Frank think that 'paper has more patience than people'?
Anne Frank believes paper has more patience than people because she can confide in her diary without fear of judgment or interruption, unlike sharing with people who might grow impatient.
6. Why did Anne decide to write a diary?
Anne decided to write a diary because she felt she didn't have a true friend she could confide in. She believed paper had more patience than people.
7. How does Wanda Petronski differ from other children?
Wanda Petronski differs from other children in her quiet nature, Polish background, poverty evident from her worn blue dress, and unusual name that sets her apart.
8. Why does Maddie stand by and not do anything?
Maddie stands by and does nothing because she doesn't want to lose her popularity and become a target herself. She feels uncomfortable but is too afraid to speak up.
9. What does the Buddha say about death and suffering?
The Buddha says that death and suffering are inevitable parts of life. He teaches that everything that is born will die and suffering comes from attachments.
10. What lesson does Kisa Gotami learn from the Buddha?
Kisa Gotami learns that death is inevitable and common to all humanity. She understands grieving cannot bring back the dead and must accept reality.
Poetry Questions
11. How does the poet describe the tiger in the zoo?
The poet describes the tiger in the zoo as a powerful but imprisoned animal. It moves quietly in its cage with quiet rage, ignoring visitors.
12. What does the tiger do in the cage?
In the cage, the tiger stalks the length of its cage, ignores visitors, hears patrolling cars at night, and stares at the stars.
13. What is the central idea of the poem "Fire and Ice"?
The central idea is that human emotions like desire and hatred are powerful enough to destroy the world. Fire represents burning desires while ice represents cold hatred.
14. What does the poet compare fire with?
The poet compares fire with desire, passion, and emotional intensity that can lead to destruction.
15. Why does the poet want to know where the teachers go?
The child poet wants to know where teachers go after school because he sees them as mysterious figures and wonders if they have normal lives outside school.
16. What does the poet imagine about his teacher's home life?
The poet imagines his teacher might live in a typical house, wear casual clothes, watch TV, wash socks, and have parents rather than being a strict teacher always.
17. What is Amanda's mother constantly nagging her about?
Amanda's mother constantly nags her about sitting properly, cleaning her room, finishing homework, biting nails, and slouching instead of sitting straight.
18. How does Amanda escape from reality?
Amanda escapes from reality by imagining herself as a mermaid in a calm sea, an orphan roaming freely, and Rapunzel in a peaceful tower.
19. What does the poet say about the snake in "The Snake Trying"?
The poet describes the snake as beautiful and harmless, trying to escape from being pursued. He suggests the snake should not be killed as it is graceful.
20. Why does the poet defend the snake?
The poet defends the snake because he believes it is harmless and beautiful. He thinks all creatures have a right to live and the snake is merely trying to escape.
Long Answer Questions
21. What does the poet want to convey through "The Tale of Custard the Dragon"?
The poet wants to convey that appearances can be deceptive and true courage isn't about boasting but acting bravely when needed. Custard appears cowardly but proves bravest when real danger appears.
22. How did everyone perceive Custard the Dragon?
Everyone perceived Custard as a coward because he always cried for a nice safe cage while other pets boasted about their bravery.
23. What happened when the pirate arrived?
When the pirate arrived, all the pets who had boasted about their bravery ran away and hid in fear. Only Custard, perceived as cowardly, attacked the pirate and saved everyone.
24. What is the Fog compared to in the poem?
The fog is compared to a cat that moves silently on its little feet, sits looking over the city and harbor, and then moves on silently.
25. How does the fog come according to the poet?
According to the poet, the fog comes silently and stealthily like a cat, on its little cat feet.
26. What does the fog do over the city and harbor?
The fog sits looking over the city and harbor like a cat sitting on its haunches, watching everything silently before moving on.
27. How does Anne Frank's diary help her cope with her situation?
Anne Frank's diary serves as her confidante and emotional outlet. It helps her process her thoughts, fears, and hopes while in hiding, providing comfort during difficult times.
28. What values does Nelson Mandela highlight in his autobiography?
Nelson Mandela highlights values like courage, resilience, forgiveness, equality, and the importance of fighting for justice and freedom in his autobiography.
29. How does the story of Lencho highlight the power of faith?
Lencho's story highlights the power of faith through his unwavering belief that God would help him, which ultimately leads to him receiving assistance, though not in the way he expected.
30. What message does the poem "Amanda" convey about parenting?
The poem "Amanda" conveys that excessive nagging and constant correction can make children withdraw into fantasy worlds. It suggests parents should balance guidance with allowing freedom.
