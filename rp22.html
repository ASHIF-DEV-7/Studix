Class 10 Biology Previous Year Questions (2022) - How Do Organisms Reproduce?

  1. Define reproduction and explain its importance in living organisms. (3 marks)

    Answer: Reproduction is the process by which organisms produce offspring. Importance: Ensures species survival, maintains genetic continuity.

  2. Differentiate between asexual and sexual reproduction with examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Asexual: Single parent, identical offspring (e.g., binary fission in Amoeba). Sexual: Two parents, genetically diverse offspring (e.g., humans).

  3. Explain binary fission in Amoeba with a diagram description. (4 marks)

    Answer: Binary fission: Amoeba divides into two equal daughter cells. Description: Nucleus divides, cytoplasm splits, forming two Amoebae.

  4. Describe budding as a mode of reproduction with an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Budding: Outgrowth develops into new organism. Example: Yeast forms a bud that detaches as a new cell.

  5. What is regeneration? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Regeneration: Organism regrows lost parts or forms new individual. Example: Planaria regrows from cut fragments.

  6. Explain spore formation in fungi with an example. (4 marks)

    Answer: Spores develop in sporangia, disperse, and grow into new fungi. Example: Rhizopus spores form new mycelium.

  7. What is vegetative propagation? Name two methods with examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Vegetative propagation: Reproduction via plant parts. Methods: Cutting (rose), layering (jasmine).

  8. Explain the advantages of vegetative propagation in plants. (3 marks)

    Answer: Advantages: Retains parental traits, faster growth, no seed requirement, useful for seedless plants.

  9. Describe the process of fragmentation in Spirogyra. (3 marks)

    Answer: Fragmentation: Spirogyra filament breaks into fragments, each growing into a new filament.

  10. What is the significance of sexual reproduction in organisms? (4 marks)

    Answer: Sexual reproduction produces genetic variation, enhances adaptability, promotes evolution, and increases survival chances.

  11. Describe the male reproductive system in humans. (4 marks)

    Answer: Male system: Testes (produce sperm/testosterone), epididymis, vas deferens, prostate, penis. Function: Sperm production and delivery.

  12. Explain the structure of the human female reproductive system. (4 marks)

    Answer: Female system: Ovaries (produce eggs/estrogen), fallopian tubes, uterus, vagina. Function: Egg production, fertilization, pregnancy.

  13. What is the role of testes in male reproduction? (3 marks)

    Answer: Testes produce sperm and testosterone, essential for reproduction and male characteristics.

  14. Explain the role of ovaries in female reproduction. (3 marks)

    Answer: Ovaries produce eggs and hormones (estrogen, progesterone), supporting reproduction and pregnancy.

  15. Describe the process of fertilization in humans. (3 marks)

    Answer: Sperm fuses with egg in fallopian tube, forming a zygote that develops into an embryo.

  16. What is the placenta? Explain its functions. (4 marks)

    Answer: Placenta: Organ connecting fetus to uterus. Functions: Supplies nutrients/oxygen, removes wastes, produces hormones.

  17. Explain the menstrual cycle and its phases. (4 marks)

    Answer: Menstrual cycle (28 days): Menstrual (shedding uterus lining), follicular (egg maturation), ovulation (egg release), luteal (prepares for pregnancy).

  18. What is ovulation? Explain its significance. (3 marks)

    Answer: Ovulation: Release of mature egg from ovary. Significance: Enables fertilization, key for reproduction.

  19. Differentiate between monozygotic and dizygotic twins. (3 marks)

    Answer: Monozygotic: Identical, from one zygote splitting. Dizygotic: Fraternal, from two eggs fertilized separately.

  20. What is implantation in human reproduction? (3 marks)

    Answer: Implantation: Embryo attaches to uterine wall for development, initiating pregnancy.

  21. Explain the role of the umbilical cord in fetal development. (3 marks)

    Answer: Umbilical cord connects fetus to placenta, transporting nutrients, oxygen, and removing wastes.

  22. What is contraception? Name three methods. (3 marks)

    Answer: Contraception prevents pregnancy. Methods: Barrier (condom), hormonal (pills), surgical (vasectomy).

  23. Explain the working of a barrier method of contraception. (4 marks)

    Answer: Barrier methods (e.g., condoms) prevent sperm from reaching egg by creating a physical barrier, reducing fertilization risk.

  24. What are hormonal contraceptives? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Hormonal contraceptives alter hormone levels to prevent ovulation. Example: Oral contraceptive pills.

  25. Describe the surgical method of contraception in males. (3 marks)

    Answer: Vasectomy: Vas deferens cut and sealed, preventing sperm release during ejaculation.

  26. Explain the significance of tubectomy in females. (3 marks)

    Answer: Tubectomy: Fallopian tubes cut/sealed, preventing egg-sperm meeting, ensuring permanent contraception.

  27. What are sexually transmitted diseases? Name two examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: STDs: Infections spread via sexual contact. Examples: HIV/AIDS, gonorrhea.

  28. Explain how HIV is transmitted and its effects. (4 marks)

    Answer: HIV transmitted via blood, sexual contact, or mother-to-child. Effects: Weakens immune system, leading to AIDS.

  29. What measures can prevent sexually transmitted diseases? (3 marks)

    Answer: Use condoms, avoid multiple partners, regular testing, and safe blood transfusions.

  30. Describe the structure of a flower and its role in reproduction. (4 marks)

    Answer: Flower: Sepals, petals, stamens (male), pistil (female). Role: Stamens produce pollen, pistil receives pollen for fertilization.

  31. Explain the process of pollination in plants. (3 marks)

    Answer: Pollination: Transfer of pollen from anther to stigma, enabling fertilization. Types: Self and cross-pollination.

  32. Differentiate between self-pollination and cross-pollination. (3 marks)

    Answer: Self-pollination: Pollen transfers within same flower/plant. Cross-pollination: Pollen transfers between different plants, increasing diversity.

  33. What is double fertilization in plants? (4 marks)

    Answer: Double fertilization: One sperm fertilizes egg (forms zygote), another fuses with polar nuclei (forms endosperm) in angiosperms.

  34. Explain the role of the endosperm in seed development. (3 marks)

    Answer: Endosperm provides nutrients to developing embryo, supporting seed growth.

  35. What is germination? Describe its conditions. (3 marks)

    Answer: Germination: Seed develops into seedling. Conditions: Water, oxygen, suitable temperature.

  36. Explain the structure of a seed with a diagram description. (4 marks)

    Answer: Seed: Seed coat, embryo (cotyledons, plumule, radicle), endosperm. Description: Seed coat protects, embryo grows into plant.

  37. What is the role of pollen tube in plant reproduction? (3 marks)

    Answer: Pollen tube delivers sperm to ovule for fertilization, enabling seed formation.

  38. Explain the advantages of seed dispersal in plants. (3 marks)

    Answer: Seed dispersal reduces competition, colonizes new areas, and enhances species survival.

  39. Describe two methods of seed dispersal with examples. (4 marks)

    Answer: Wind dispersal (e.g., dandelion seeds). Animal dispersal (e.g., burrs sticking to fur).

  40. What is the significance of genetic variation in sexual reproduction? (3 marks)

    Answer: Genetic variation enhances adaptability, survival, and evolution by combining parental genes.
