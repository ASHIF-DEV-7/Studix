Class 10 Maths Previous Year Questions (2023) - Introduction to Trigonometry

  1. Define trigonometric ratios for a right triangle. Name the six ratios. (3 marks)

    Answer: Trigonometric ratios relate angles to sides in a right triangle. Ratios: sin θ = opp/hyp, cos θ = adj/hyp, tan θ = opp/adj, cosec θ = hyp/opp, sec θ = hyp/adj, cot θ = adj/opp.

  2. If sin θ = 3/5, find cos θ and tan θ. (3 marks)

    Answer: Hypotenuse = 5, opposite = 3, adjacent = √(5² - 3²) = 4. cos θ = 4/5, tan θ = 3/4.

  3. Prove that sin² θ + cos² θ = 1. (4 marks)

    Answer: In right triangle, sin θ = opp/hyp, cos θ = adj/hyp. By Pythagoras, opp² + adj² = hyp². Divide by hyp²: (opp/hyp)² + (adj/hyp)² = 1, so sin² θ + cos² θ = 1.

  4. If tan θ = 12/5, find cosec θ. (3 marks)

    Answer: tan θ = opp/adj = 12/5. Hyp = √(12² + 5²) = 13. cosec θ = hyp/opp = 13/12.

  5. Evaluate: sin 30° + cos 60°. (3 marks)

    Answer: sin 30° = 1/2, cos 60° = 1/2. So, 1/2 + 1/2 = 1.

  6. Prove that tan θ = sin θ / cos θ. (3 marks)

    Answer: tan θ = opp/adj, sin θ = opp/hyp, cos θ = adj/hyp. sin θ / cos θ = (opp/hyp) / (adj/hyp) = opp/adj = tan θ.

  7. If cos θ = 5/13, find the other five trigonometric ratios. (4 marks)

    Answer: Hyp = 13, adj = 5, opp = √(13² - 5²) = 12. sin θ = 12/13, tan θ = 12/5, cosec θ = 13/12, sec θ = 13/5, cot θ = 5/12.

  8. Find the value of cot 45° + cosec 45°. (3 marks)

    Answer: cot 45° = 1, cosec 45° = √2. So, 1 + √2.

  9. Prove that 1 + cot² θ = cosec² θ. (4 marks)

    Answer: cot θ = adj/opp, cosec θ = hyp/opp. 1 + cot² θ = 1 + (adj/opp)² = (opp² + adj²)/opp² = hyp²/opp² = cosec² θ.

  10. If sin θ = cos θ, find θ. (3 marks)

    Answer: sin θ = cos θ implies tan θ = 1. So, θ = 45°.

  11. A pole 6 m high casts a shadow 2√3 m long. Find the sun’s elevation angle. (4 marks)

    Answer: tan θ = pole/shadow = 6/(2√3) = √3. So, θ = 60°.

  12. If sec θ = 2, find sin θ and tan θ. (3 marks)

    Answer: sec θ = hyp/adj = 2. Hyp = 2, adj = 1, opp = √(2² - 1²) = √3. sin θ = √3/2, tan θ = √3.

  13. Evaluate: 2 tan 45° + cos 30°. (3 marks)

    Answer: tan 45° = 1, cos 30° = √3/2. So, 2(1) + √3/2 = 2 + √3/2.

  14. Prove that sec² θ - tan² θ = 1. (4 marks)

    Answer: sec θ = hyp/adj, tan θ = opp/adj. sec² θ - tan² θ = (hyp/adj)² - (opp/adj)² = (hyp² - opp²)/adj² = adj²/adj² = 1.

  15. If cot θ = 7/8, find cos θ. (3 marks)

    Answer: cot θ = adj/opp = 7/8. Hyp = √(7² + 8²) = √113. cos θ = adj/hyp = 7/√113.

  16. Find the angle of depression from a 10 m tower to a point 10√3 m away. (4 marks)

    Answer: tan θ = height/distance = 10/(10√3) = 1/√3. So, θ = 30°.

  17. If sin θ = 1/2, find cosec θ and cot θ. (3 marks)

    Answer: sin θ = 1/2, so cosec θ = 1/sin θ = 2. cos θ = √(1 - (1/2)²) = √3/2, cot θ = cos θ/sin θ = √3.

  18. Evaluate: sin 60° / cos 30°. (3 marks)

    Answer: sin 60° = √3/2, cos 30° = √3/2. So, (√3/2) / (√3/2) = 1.

  19. Prove that (sin θ + cos θ)² = 1 + 2 sin θ cos θ. (4 marks)

    Answer: Expand: (sin θ + cos θ)² = sin² θ + cos² θ + 2 sin θ cos θ = 1 + 2 sin θ cos θ (using sin² θ + cos² θ = 1).

  20. If tan θ = 3/4, find sec θ. (3 marks)

    Answer: tan θ = opp/adj = 3/4. Hyp = √(3² + 4²) = 5. sec θ = hyp/adj = 5/4.

  21. A ladder 15 m long reaches a window 9 m high. Find angle with ground. (4 marks)

    Answer: cos θ = adj/hyp = 9/15 = 3/5. θ = cos⁻¹(3/5) ≈ 53°.

  22. If cosec θ = 5/3, find cos θ. (3 marks)

    Answer: cosec θ = hyp/opp = 5/3. Adj = √(5² - 3²) = 4. cos θ = adj/hyp = 4/5.

  23. Evaluate: sec 60° + cot 45°. (3 marks)

    Answer: sec 60° = 2, cot 45° = 1. So, 2 + 1 = 3.

  24. Prove that (1 - sin² θ) / cos² θ = 1. (4 marks)

    Answer: 1 - sin² θ = cos² θ. So, (cos² θ) / cos² θ = 1.

  25. If sin θ = 4/5, find cot θ. (3 marks)

    Answer: sin θ = 4/5, cos θ = √(1 - (4/5)²) = 3/5. cot θ = cos θ/sin θ = (3/5)/(4/5) = 3/4.

  26. Find height of a tree if its shadow is 20 m, sun’s angle is 30°. (4 marks)

    Answer: tan 30° = height/shadow = h/20 = 1/√3. h = 20/√3 ≈ 11.55 m.

  27. If sec θ = 13/12, find sin θ. (3 marks)

    Answer: sec θ = hyp/adj = 13/12. Opp = √(13² - 12²) = 5. sin θ = opp/hyp = 5/13.

  28. Evaluate: tan 30° × cos 60°. (3 marks)

    Answer: tan 30° = 1/√3, cos 60° = 1/2. So, (1/√3) × (1/2) = 1/(2√3).

  29. Prove that cosec θ - cot θ = sin θ / (1 + cos θ). (4 marks)

    Answer: LHS: cosec θ = 1/sin θ, cot θ = cos θ/sin θ. cosec θ - cot θ = (1 - cos θ)/sin θ. RHS: sin θ/(1 + cos θ). Multiply numerator/denominator by (1 - cos θ): sin θ(1 - cos θ)/(1 - cos² θ) = (1 - cos θ)/sin θ = LHS.

  30. If tan θ = 1, find cos θ. (3 marks)

    Answer: tan θ = 1, so θ = 45°. cos 45° = 1/√2.

  31. Find angle of elevation if a 12 m pole casts 12√3 m shadow. (4 marks)

    Answer: tan θ = 12/(12√3) = 1/√3. So, θ = 30°.

  32. If cos θ = 1/√2, find other ratios. (3 marks)

    Answer: cos θ = 1/√2, θ = 45°. sin θ = 1/√2, tan θ = 1, cosec θ = √2, sec θ = √2, cot θ = 1.

  33. Evaluate: sin 45° + cos 45°. (3 marks)

    Answer: sin 45° = 1/√2, cos 45° = 1/√2. So, 1/√2 + 1/√2 = √2.

  34. Prove that tan² θ + 1 = sec² θ. (4 marks)

    Answer: tan² θ = (opp/adj)², sec² θ = (hyp/adj)². tan² θ + 1 = (opp² + adj²)/adj² = hyp²/adj² = sec² θ.

  35. If cot θ = 3/4, find cosec θ. (3 marks)

    Answer: cot θ = adj/opp = 3/4. Hyp = √(3² + 4²) = 5. cosec θ = hyp/opp = 5/4.

  36. A kite at 50 m height has string at 60° angle. Find string length. (4 marks)

    Answer: sin 60° = height/string = 50/l = √3/2. l = 50 × 2/√3 ≈ 57.74 m.

  37. If sin θ = 5/13, find sec θ. (3 marks)

    Answer: sin θ = opp/hyp = 5/13. Adj = √(13² - 5²) = 12. sec θ = hyp/adj = 13/12.

  38. Evaluate: cosec 30° - cot 60°. (3 marks)

    Answer: cosec 30° = 2, cot 60° = 1/√3. So, 2 - 1/√3.

  39. Prove that (1 + tan² θ) cos² θ = 1. (4 marks)

    Answer: 1 + tan² θ = sec² θ. So, sec² θ × cos² θ = (1/cos² θ) × cos² θ = 1.

  40. Find distance of a point 20 m from a 20√3 m pole, angle 60°. (4 marks)

    Answer: tan 60° = 20√3/d = √3. d = 20√3/√3 = 20 m.
