Class 10 English (Poem) 2024 Previous Year Questions

1. What is the central theme of the poem "Dust of Snow" by Robert Frost?
The poem highlights how a small natural incident can bring a positive change in our mood and outlook on life.
2. Explain the symbolism of the crow and the hemlock tree in "Dust of Snow".
The crow and hemlock symbolize negativity or sorrow, but they bring a surprising positive impact, showing that even unpleasant things can bring happiness.
3. What message does the poem "Fire and Ice" convey?
The poem warns that the world may end either in fire (desire) or ice (hatred), emphasizing the destructive power of human emotions.
4. How does the poet use fire and ice as metaphors?
Fire represents desire, greed, and passion; ice represents hatred, coldness, and indifference.
5. What is the significance of the tiger in the poem "A Tiger in the Zoo"?
The tiger represents the contrast between captivity and freedom, showing the cruelty of confining wild animals.
6. Describe the mood of the poem "A Tiger in the Zoo".
The poem is filled with sadness and anger at the tiger's imprisonment, highlighting the loss of natural freedom.
7. What lesson does the poem "The Ball Poem" teach?
It teaches the inevitability of loss and the importance of accepting it as a part of life.
8. How does the boy react to the loss of his ball?
The boy is initially shocked and saddened but gradually learns to accept the loss as a life lesson.
9. What is the theme of the poem "Animals" by Walt Whitman?
The poem praises the simplicity, honesty, and contentment of animals compared to humans' greed and hypocrisy.
10. How does the poet express admiration for animals in the poem "Animals"?
The poet appreciates their calmness, lack of complaints, and self-sufficiency, presenting them as a model for humans.
11. What is the message of "Amanda!"?
The poem conveys the importance of giving children freedom and respecting their individuality.
12. How does Amanda escape from her reality?
Amanda daydreams of being a mermaid, an orphan, or Rapunzel to escape the constant nagging of adults.
13. Describe the poetic device used in the line "I am Rapunzel" in "Amanda!".
The poet uses allusion, referring to the fairy-tale character Rapunzel to represent freedom and isolation.
14. What does the poem "The Trees" symbolize?
It symbolizes the human desire for freedom and the importance of reconnecting with nature.
15. Explain how the poet describes the movement of trees in "The Trees".
The poet uses vivid imagery to show trees moving out of the house into the forest, reclaiming their natural habitat.
16. What is the main idea of "Fog" by Carl Sandburg?
The poem presents fog as a living creature, like a cat, moving silently and mysteriously.
17. How does the poet personify fog in the poem "Fog"?
The fog is described as a cat that comes on little feet, sits looking, and then moves on quietly.
