Class 10 Chemistry Previous Year Questions (2023) - Chemical Reactions and Equations

  1. Explain the law of conservation of mass with an example of a chemical reaction. (3 marks)

    Answer: The law states that mass is neither created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction. Example: CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O; mass of reactants equals mass of products.

  2. Classify the reaction: 2Na + Cl₂ → 2NaCl. Explain why it fits this category. (3 marks)

    Answer: Combination reaction; two substances (Na and Cl₂) combine to form a single product (NaCl).

  3. Balance the equation: Fe + H₂O → Fe₃O₄ + H₂, and identify its type. (3 marks)

    Answer: 3Fe + 4H₂O → Fe₃O₄ + 4H₂. Redox and combination reaction.

  4. What is a decomposition reaction? Give two examples with balanced equations. (4 marks)

    Answer: A single compound breaks into simpler substances. Examples: 2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂ (electrolysis); 2HgO → 2Hg + O₂ (thermal).

  5. Explain why calcium carbonate decomposes when heated. Write the balanced equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: CaCO₃ decomposes into CaO and CO₂ due to heat breaking bonds. CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂.

  6. Identify the type of reaction: Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu. Justify your answer. (3 marks)

    Answer: Displacement reaction; Zn displaces Cu from CuSO₄ due to higher reactivity.

  7. What is a double displacement reaction? Provide an example with a balanced equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Ions of two compounds exchange places. Example: Na₂SO₄ + BaCl₂ → BaSO₄ + 2NaCl.

  8. Explain the concept of redox reactions with an example. (4 marks)

    Answer: Redox involves oxidation (loss of electrons) and reduction (gain of electrons). Example: CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O; CuO reduced, H₂ oxidized.

  9. Identify the substance oxidized and reduced in: 2Fe + O₂ → 2FeO. (3 marks)

    Answer: Fe oxidized (gains oxygen), O₂ reduced (loses oxygen).

  10. Why is respiration considered an exothermic reaction? Explain with the reaction. (3 marks)

    Answer: Respiration releases energy. C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + energy.

  11. Balance the equation: Al + HCl → AlCl₃ + H₂, and state its type. (3 marks)

    Answer: 2Al + 6HCl → 2AlCl₃ + 3H₂. Displacement reaction.

  12. What is corrosion? Describe the chemical process of rusting of iron. (4 marks)

    Answer: Corrosion is metal deterioration by chemical reaction. Rusting: 4Fe + 3O₂ + 6H₂O → 4Fe(OH)₃ (rust).

  13. Suggest three methods to prevent corrosion of iron. (3 marks)

    Answer: Galvanization (zinc coating), painting, applying oil/grease.

  14. What is rancidity? Explain two methods to prevent it. (3 marks)

    Answer: Rancidity is oxidation of fats/oils causing bad smell/taste. Prevention: Nitrogen flushing, adding antioxidants like BHA.

  15. Write the balanced equation for the combustion of methane and classify it. (3 marks)

    Answer: CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O. Exothermic and redox reaction.

  16. Explain why some reactions are exothermic while others are endothermic. (4 marks)

    Answer: Exothermic reactions release energy (bonds formed stronger); endothermic absorb energy (bonds broken require more energy). Examples: Combustion (exothermic), CaCO₃ decomposition (endothermic).

  17. Balance the equation: KClO₃ → KCl + O₂, and state its type. (3 marks)

    Answer: 2KClO₃ → 2KCl + 3O₂. Decomposition reaction.

  18. What is a precipitation reaction? Give an example with a balanced equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Forms an insoluble product. Example: AgNO₃ + NaCl → AgCl↓ + NaNO₃.

  19. Explain the role of a catalyst in a chemical reaction with an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Catalyst lowers activation energy, speeding up reaction without being consumed. Example: MnO₂ in 2H₂O₂ → 2H₂O + O₂.

  20. Write the balanced equation for the reaction of zinc with dilute H₂SO₄. Identify the type. (3 marks)

    Answer: Zn + H₂SO₄ → ZnSO₄ + H₂. Displacement reaction.

  21. Describe the electrolysis of water and write the electrode reactions. (4 marks)

    Answer: Water decomposes to H₂ and O₂ via electric current. Cathode: 2H₂O + 2e⁻ → H₂ + 2OH⁻; Anode: 4OH⁻ → O₂ + 2H₂O + 4e⁻.

  22. Why do some reactions produce a pop sound with a burning splinter? (3 marks)

    Answer: Hydrogen gas, produced in reactions (e.g., Zn + HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂), burns rapidly with a pop sound.

  23. Balance the equation: Pb(NO₃)₂ → PbO + NO₂ + O₂, and state its type. (3 marks)

    Answer: 2Pb(NO₃)₂ → 2PbO + 4NO₂ + O₂. Decomposition reaction.

  24. Explain the term 'chemical equation' and its significance. (3 marks)

    Answer: A chemical equation represents reactants and products using symbols/formulae. Significance: Shows reaction stoichiometry, conservation of mass.

  25. What is an oxidation reaction? Give two examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Loss of electrons or gain of oxygen. Examples: 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO; S + O₂ → SO₂.

  26. Explain the reaction of calcium with water with a balanced equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Ca + 2H₂O → Ca(OH)₂ + H₂. Calcium displaces hydrogen, forming calcium hydroxide.

  27. Why is the reaction of sodium with water vigorous? (3 marks)

    Answer: Sodium is highly reactive, releases H₂ rapidly: 2Na + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂, exothermic.

  28. Balance the equation: NH₃ + O₂ → NO + H₂O, and identify its type. (4 marks)

    Answer: 4NH₃ + 5O₂ → 4NO + 6H₂O. Redox reaction.

  29. What is a neutralization reaction? Provide an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Acid reacts with base to form salt and water. Example: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O.

  30. Explain why food items turn rancid and suggest prevention methods. (4 marks)

    Answer: Oxidation of fats/oils causes rancidity. Prevention: Store in air-tight containers, use antioxidants, refrigerate.

  31. Write the balanced equation for burning of sulphur in air. (3 marks)

    Answer: S + O₂ → SO₂.

  32. Explain the effect of temperature on reaction rate with an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Higher temperature increases reaction rate by providing energy. Example: CaCO₃ decomposes faster when heated.

  33. What is photochemical decomposition? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Decomposition by light. Example: 2AgCl → 2Ag + Cl₂ (on exposure to sunlight).

  34. Balance the equation: C₂H₄ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O, and state its type. (3 marks)

    Answer: C₂H₄ + 3O₂ → 2CO₂ + 2H₂O. Combustion reaction.

  35. Explain why some metals corrode faster in humid conditions. (3 marks)

    Answer: Moisture facilitates electron transfer, accelerating oxidation (e.g., Fe forms Fe(OH)₃ faster with water).

  36. What is thermal decomposition? Give an example with a balanced equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Decomposition by heat. Example: 2Pb(NO₃)₂ → 2PbO + 4NO₂ + O₂.

  37. Explain the role of oxygen in combustion reactions. (3 marks)

    Answer: Oxygen acts as an oxidizing agent, supporting burning by accepting electrons. Example: C + O₂ → CO₂.

  38. Balance the equation: Mg + N₂ → Mg₃N₂, and classify it. (3 marks)

    Answer: 3Mg + N₂ → Mg₃N₂. Combination reaction.
