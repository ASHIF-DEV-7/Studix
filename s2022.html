Science 2022 Previous Year Questions

1. What is the difference between a physical change and a chemical change?
A physical change alters the state or appearance without forming a new substance, while a chemical change produces one or more new substances.
2. Define acids and bases with examples.
Acids: substances that release H+ ions (e.g., HCl). Bases: substances that release OH- ions (e.g., NaOH).
3. What is the law of conservation of mass?
In a chemical reaction, mass of reactants equals mass of products; mass is neither created nor destroyed.
4. Explain the structure of an atom.
An atom consists of a nucleus containing protons and neutrons, surrounded by electrons in shells.
5. What are metals and non-metals?
Metals are shiny, malleable, ductile, and good conductors; non-metals are dull, brittle, and poor conductors.
6. What is the difference between an element and a compound?
An element has only one type of atom; a compound contains two or more elements chemically combined.
7. Define reproduction in plants.
Reproduction is the biological process by which plants produce new individuals, either sexually or asexually.
8. What is heredity?
Heredity is the passing of traits from parents to offspring through genes.
9. Explain control and coordination in humans.
Control and coordination involve the nervous and endocrine systems regulating body functions and responses to stimuli.
10. What are carbohydrates, proteins, and fats?
Carbohydrates provide energy, proteins are for growth and repair, fats store energy and insulate the body.
11. What is photosynthesis?
Process by which green plants produce food from carbon dioxide and water using sunlight and chlorophyll.
12. Define respiration in humans.
Respiration is the process of releasing energy from food, can be aerobic (with O2) or anaerobic (without O2).
13. What is the role of xylem and phloem in plants?
Xylem transports water and minerals; phloem transports food from leaves to other parts.
14. What is the difference between aerobic and anaerobic respiration?
Aerobic uses oxygen to release energy; anaerobic occurs without oxygen, producing less energy and lactic acid or ethanol.
15. Define alloy and give examples.
An alloy is a mixture of metals or metal with non-metal to improve properties. Examples: steel, brass, bronze.
16. Explain the water cycle.
Water evaporates, condenses to form clouds, and precipitates as rain, repeating continuously.
17. What is the importance of soil in agriculture?
Soil provides nutrients, water, and support to plants for growth.
18. Define biodiversity.
Biodiversity is the variety of life forms in a particular habitat or ecosystem.
19. What are enzymes?
Enzymes are biological catalysts that speed up chemical reactions in the body without being consumed.
20. Explain the principle of inheritance.
Traits are inherited from parents through genes and alleles, determining the characteristics of offspring.
21. What is a mixture and its types?
A mixture is a physical combination of two or more substances. Types: homogeneous and heterogeneous.
22. What is neutralization reaction?
Reaction between an acid and a base to form salt and water.
23. Explain the periodic table.
The periodic table arranges elements by increasing atomic number, showing patterns in properties.
24. What is chemical reaction?
A process in which substances react to form new substances with different properties.
25. Explain decomposition reaction with example.
A reaction where a compound breaks into simpler substances. Example: 2H2O â†’ 2H2 + O2.
26. What is combustion?
Combustion is a reaction of a substance with oxygen producing heat and light.
27. Define metals and their properties.
Metals are solid, shiny, malleable, ductile, and conduct heat and electricity.
28. Define non-metals and their properties.
Non-metals are brittle, dull, poor conductors, and may exist in solid, liquid, or gas state.
29. What is the significance of control and coordination in plants?
Hormones in plants coordinate growth, response to stimuli, and adaptation to environment.
30. Explain reproductive strategies in plants.
Plants reproduce sexually via seeds and asexually via budding, cutting, or vegetative propagation.
31. What are carbohydrates?
Organic compounds made of carbon, hydrogen, oxygen; main energy source for body.
32. What is the difference between physical and chemical properties?
Physical properties can be observed without changing substance; chemical properties describe reactivity.
33. Define acid-base indicators.
Substances that change color in acids or bases to indicate pH level, e.g., litmus, phenolphthalein.
34. What is a chemical formula?
Representation of a compound showing types and numbers of atoms present.
35. Explain the role of chlorophyll in photosynthesis.
Chlorophyll absorbs sunlight and converts it into chemical energy for making food.
36. What is the role of digestive enzymes?
Enzymes help break down food into absorbable nutrients efficiently.
37. What is the role of excretory system?
Removes metabolic wastes and maintains chemical balance in body fluids.
38. Define nitrogen cycle.
Process of nitrogen conversion between atmosphere, soil, plants, and animals, maintaining ecosystem balance.
39. Explain the process of osmosis.
Osmosis is the movement of water molecules from low to high solute concentration through a semi-permeable membrane.
40. Define biodiversity conservation.
Protection and management of species and habitats to maintain ecological balance.
