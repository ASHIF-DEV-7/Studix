Madam Rides the Bus — Class 10 Notes

Introduction (10 Lines)

1. “Madam Rides the Bus” is a heart-warming story written by Vallikannan.
2. It is about an eight-year-old curious girl named Valli.
3. Valli longs to experience the world outside her village.
4. She is fascinated by the bus that passes daily through her street.
5. Secretly, she plans to ride the bus to the nearby town.
6. She carefully saves money for her adventure.
7. The story captures her excitement, courage and innocence.
8. Valli’s solo bus ride shows her independent spirit.
9. She also learns about life and death during the trip.
10. The chapter highlights childhood curiosity and growing understanding of the world.

Chapter Explanation (20 Points)

  • 1. Valli is an eight-year-old girl living in a small village.

  • 2. She is very curious and loves observing things around her.

  • 3. A bus passes her house every day, which fascinates her.

  • 4. She hears people talk about the nearby town and longs to visit it.

  • 5. Valli secretly plans a trip to the town without informing anyone.

  • 6. She carefully saves money for the bus fare of thirty paise one way.

  • 7. On a sunny afternoon when her mother is asleep, she boards the bus.

  • 8. The conductor jokingly calls her “Madam” because of her confident manner.

  • 9. Valli enjoys watching fields, mountains and other scenes during the ride.

  • 10. She observes passengers and listens to their conversations.

  • 11. A young cow runs in front of the bus, making everyone laugh.

  • 12. On reaching the town, she refuses to get off because she only wants to ride the bus.

  • 13. She pays the fare again for the return journey.

  • 14. On the way back, she sees a shocking sight — the same cow lying dead on the road.

  • 15. This shows her the reality of life and death.

  • 16. Valli quietly reflects on the scene, feeling a sudden seriousness.

  • 17. When she reaches home, her mother is still unaware of her adventure.

  • 18. Valli listens calmly as her mother talks about life’s many mysteries.

  • 19. The trip makes Valli more mature and thoughtful.

  • 20. The story ends showing her innocence but also new understanding of life’s truths.

Conclusion (7–8 Lines)

Madam Rides the Bus beautifully captures a child’s spirit of adventure.

Valli’s independent bus journey shows her courage and careful planning.

The dead cow incident teaches her the reality of death and the fragility of life.

By the end, she is wiser and more aware of the world beyond her village.

The story reflects the curiosity and growth of childhood.

It reminds us that small experiences can give big lessons about life and maturity.

Valli’s day out becomes a turning point in her understanding of life.

Moral / Theme

  • Childhood Curiosity: Children have a natural desire to explore the world.

  • Independence: Planning and responsibility help achieve small dreams.

  • Life & Death: Experiences teach that joy and sorrow are part of life.

  • Growth: Real learning often happens outside books and classrooms.
