The Triumph of Surgery

By James Herriot | Class 10 English - Footprints Without Feet

About the Story

"The Triumph of Surgery" is a heartwarming story from James Herriot's collection about a veterinary surgeon's experience with an overfed dog named Tricki and his wealthy owner Mrs. Pumphrey.

Key Message:

Sometimes the best medicine is not medication but proper care, discipline, and healthy lifestyle.

Main Characters

  • Mr. Herriot: The veterinary surgeon and narrator
  • Tricki: A small dog who is excessively pampered
  • Mrs. Pumphrey: Tricki's wealthy and overindulgent owner
  • Hodgkin: The gardener who drives Mrs. Pumphrey's car

Summary

Mrs. Pumphrey's dog Tricki falls ill due to overfeeding and lack of exercise. Despite being given various medications and special treatments, Tricki's condition worsens.

Veterinary surgeon Mr. Herriot realizes that Tricki's problem is not medical but lifestyle-related. He decides to hospitalize Tricki away from Mrs. Pumphrey's pampering. At the surgery, Tricki is put on a strict diet and gets proper exercise with other dogs.

Within days, Tricki transforms into a healthy, active dog. Mrs. Pumphrey believes it's a "triumph of surgery," though the real cure was simple discipline and healthy habits.

Central Theme:

Overindulgence and lack of discipline can cause harm, while proper care and healthy habits lead to well-being.

Important Points

  • Tricki was fed excessively with cream cakes, chocolates, and cod-liver oil
  • Mrs. Pumphrey's overaffection was actually harming Tricki's health
  • The real treatment was simple: balanced diet and regular exercise
  • Sometimes love needs to be expressed through discipline, not indulgence
  • The story shows the importance of professional advice over emotional decisions

Character Analysis:

Mrs. Pumphrey represents well-intentioned but misguided love, while Mr. Herriot represents practical, professional care.

About the Author

James Herriot (1916-1995) was a British veterinary surgeon and writer famous for his books about animals and their owners. His real name was James Alfred Wight, and he wrote under the pen name James Herriot.

Vocabulary

  • Surgery: Medical treatment of injuries or diseases
  • Pampered: Treated with excessive care and attention
  • Convalescing: Recovering from illness
  • Lumbago: Pain in the muscles and joints of the lower back
  • Cod-liver oil: A nutritional supplement rich in vitamins

Moral of the Story

True care sometimes requires making difficult decisions rather than giving in to immediate desires. Proper discipline and healthy habits are more effective than excessive pampering.
