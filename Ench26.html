The Making of a Scientist

By Robert W. Peterson | Class 10 English - Footprints Without Feet

About the Story

"The Making of a Scientist" is the inspiring story of Richard H. Ebright, who became a renowned scientist through his curiosity, hard work, and dedication from a young age.

Key Message:

Curiosity, perseverance, and proper guidance can help anyone achieve great success in science.

Main Character

  • Richard H. Ebright: The protagonist who becomes a successful scientist
  • Mrs. Ebright: Richard's supportive mother
  • Dr. Urquhart: The scientist who guided Richard
  • Richard A. Weiherer: Richard's social studies teacher

Summary

Richard Ebright showed scientific curiosity from childhood. He started collecting butterflies and rocks, but his interest deepened when his mother bought him a book called "The Travels of Monarch X."

He began researching monarch butterflies and discovered the purpose of gold spots on their pupae. His project won first prize in the county science fair.

In high school, he discovered an unknown insect hormone and later found how cells can read the blueprint of DNA. His work earned him many prestigious awards, including the Searle Scholar Award.

Turning Point:

Dr. Urquhart's book and subsequent guidance helped channel Richard's curiosity into meaningful scientific research.

Important Points

  • Richard collected butterflies, rocks, coins, and fossils as a child
  • His mother encouraged his curiosity and supported his interests
  • He participated in science fairs from a young age
  • His discovery about gold spots on monarch pupae was his first major achievement
  • He proved that DNA is the blueprint for life
  • Besides science, he was also a debater, canoeist, and photographer

Qualities of a Scientist:

Curiosity, perseverance, observation skills, competitive spirit, and willingness to work hard.

Scientific Discoveries

  • Purpose of gold spots on monarch pupae
  • Discovery of an unknown insect hormone
  • How cells read the blueprint of DNA
  • Theory on the life of cells

Awards and Achievements

  • First prize in county science fair
  • First prize in International Science and Engineering Fair
  • Searle Scholar Award
  • Schering Plough Award for Biochemistry

Vocabulary

  • Monarch butterfly: A large migratory butterfly with orange-and-black wings
  • Pupa: The stage between larva and adult in insect metamorphosis
  • DNA: Deoxyribonucleic acid, carrier of genetic information
  • Blueprint: A design plan or prototype
  • Hormone: A regulatory substance in living organisms

Inspiration from the Story

Richard Ebright's journey shows that scientific greatness can begin with simple childhood curiosity when nurtured with proper guidance, hard work, and determination.
