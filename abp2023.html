Class 10 Chemistry Previous Year Questions (2023) - Acids, Bases, and Salts

  1. Define acids and bases according to Arrhenius theory. Give one example each. (3 marks)

    Answer: Acids release H⁺ ions in water (e.g., HCl). Bases release OH⁻ ions in water (e.g., NaOH).

  2. Explain why acids conduct electricity in aqueous solution. (3 marks)

    Answer: Acids dissociate into ions (H⁺ and anion) in water, allowing ion movement to conduct electricity.

  3. Write the chemical equation for the reaction of hydrochloric acid with zinc. Identify the gas evolved. (3 marks)

    Answer: Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂. Gas: Hydrogen.

  4. What is the pH scale? Explain its significance in daily life. (4 marks)

    Answer: pH scale (0-14) measures acidity/alkalinity. Significance: Monitors soil pH for agriculture, blood pH for health.

  5. How does litmus paper help identify acids and bases? Describe with examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Blue litmus turns red in acids (e.g., HCl); red litmus turns blue in bases (e.g., NaOH).

  6. What is a neutralization reaction? Write a balanced equation for one. (3 marks)

    Answer: Acid reacts with base to form salt and water. Example: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O.

  7. Explain the reaction of a metal carbonate with an acid. Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Produces salt, water, and CO₂. Example: CaCO₃ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + H₂O + CO₂.

  8. Describe the preparation of sodium hydroxide in the lab with a balanced equation. (4 marks)

    Answer: Electrolysis of NaCl solution: 2NaCl + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + Cl₂ + H₂. NaOH forms at cathode.

  9. Why is dilute acid used instead of concentrated acid in reactions with metals? (3 marks)

    Answer: Dilute acid reacts controllably, preventing violent reactions or excessive gas production.

  10. What are strong and weak acids? Give one example each. (3 marks)

    Answer: Strong acids fully ionize (e.g., HCl). Weak acids partially ionize (e.g., CH₃COOH).

  11. Explain the role of pH in tooth decay and its prevention. (4 marks)

    Answer: Bacteria produce acids (pH < 5.5), eroding enamel. Prevention: Use basic toothpaste, limit sugary foods.

  12. Write the reaction of sodium bicarbonate with an acid. State its use. (3 marks)

    Answer: NaHCO₃ + HCl → NaCl + H₂O + CO₂. Use: Antacid to neutralize stomach acid.

  13. What is the universal indicator? How does it indicate pH? (3 marks)

    Answer: Mixture of dyes showing pH by color change (red: acidic, green: neutral, purple: basic).

  14. Explain the reaction of a base with a non-metal oxide. Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Forms salt and water. Example: Ca(OH)₂ + CO₂ → CaCO₃ + H₂O.

  15. Describe the properties of salts with two examples. (4 marks)

    Answer: Ionic, high melting points, soluble in water. Examples: NaCl (table salt), CuSO₄ (used in fungicides).

  16. What is the chemical formula of washing soda? Explain its preparation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Na₂CO₃. Prepared via Solvay process: NaCl + NH₃ + CO₂ + H₂O → NaHCO₃, then 2NaHCO₃ → Na₂CO₃ + H₂O + CO₂.

  17. Why is plaster of Paris used in construction? Write its setting reaction. (3 marks)

    Answer: Hardens quickly for casts. CaSO₄·½H₂O + 1½H₂O → CaSO₄·2H₂O (gypsum).

  18. Explain the difference between acidic and basic salts with examples. (4 marks)

    Answer: Acidic salts from strong acid, weak base (e.g., NH₄Cl). Basic salts from strong base, weak acid (e.g., Na₂CO₃).

  19. What is the role of baking soda in baking? Write the reaction involved. (3 marks)

    Answer: Releases CO₂ for rising dough. 2NaHCO₃ → Na₂CO₃ + H₂O + CO₂.

  20. Why do acids taste sour? Give two examples of edible acids. (3 marks)

    Answer: H⁺ ions stimulate sour taste. Examples: Citric acid (lemon), acetic acid (vinegar).

  21. Explain the reaction of acids with metal oxides. Provide an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Forms salt and water. Example: CuO + 2HCl → CuCl₂ + H₂O.

  22. What is the chemical name and formula of bleaching powder? Describe its preparation. (4 marks)

    Answer: Calcium hypochlorite, Ca(OCl)Cl. Prepared by passing Cl₂ over slaked lime: Ca(OH)₂ + Cl₂ → Ca(OCl)Cl + H₂O.

  23. Why is pH important in soil for agriculture? (3 marks)

    Answer: pH affects nutrient availability; most crops need pH 6-7. Lime (Ca(OH)₂) used to neutralize acidic soil.

  24. Write the reaction of sodium hydroxide with ammonium chloride. (3 marks)

    Answer: NaOH + NH₄Cl → NaCl + H₂O + NH₃.

  25. Explain why bases are slippery to touch. (3 marks)

    Answer: Bases react with skin oils to form soap-like substances, causing slipperiness.

  26. What is the common ion effect in salts? Give an example. (4 marks)

    Answer: Presence of common ion reduces salt solubility. Example: NaCl solubility decreases in presence of HCl due to Cl⁻ ions.

  27. Describe the use of sodium chloride in daily life. (3 marks)

    Answer: Used as table salt, food preservative, and in chemical industries (e.g., NaOH production).

  28. Explain the formation of hydrated salts with an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Salts bind water molecules in crystal structure. Example: CuSO₄·5H₂O (blue vitriol).

  29. What is the reaction of acids with metal hydrogen carbonates? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Produces salt, water, CO₂. Example: NaHCO₃ + HCl → NaCl + H₂O + CO₂.

  30. Explain the preparation of hydrochloric acid in the lab. (4 marks)

    Answer: React NaCl with conc. H₂SO₄: NaCl + H₂SO₄ → NaHSO₄ + HCl. Collect HCl gas by upward displacement of air.

  31. Why do some salts form neutral solutions while others are acidic or basic? (3 marks)

    Answer: Depends on parent acid/base strength. NaCl (strong acid, strong base) neutral; NH₄Cl (strong acid, weak base) acidic.

  32. What is the role of gypsum in cement? Write its formula. (3 marks)

    Answer: CaSO₄·2H₂O; slows cement setting for workability.

  33. Explain the reaction of a base with an acid anhydride. Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Forms salt and water. Example: 2NaOH + SO₂ → Na₂SO₃ + H₂O.

  34. What is the water of crystallization? Give two examples of salts. (3 marks)

    Answer: Water molecules in salt crystals. Examples: CuSO₄·5H₂O, Na₂CO₃·10H₂O.

  35. Describe the test for carbon dioxide gas in a reaction. (3 marks)

    Answer: Pass gas through lime water (Ca(OH)₂); turns milky due to CaCO₃ formation: Ca(OH)₂ + CO₂ → CaCO₃ + H₂O.

  36. Explain why antacids are used to treat acidity. Give an example. (4 marks)

    Answer: Antacids neutralize excess stomach acid (HCl). Example: Mg(OH)₂ + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + 2H₂O.

  37. What is the role of phenolphthalein in acid-base titrations? (3 marks)

    Answer: Changes color (pink in base, colorless in acid) to indicate endpoint of titration.

  38. Write the reaction of magnesium with dilute sulphuric acid. Identify products. (3 marks)

    Answer: Mg + H₂SO₄ → MgSO₄ + H₂. Products: Magnesium sulphate, hydrogen gas.

  39. Explain the use of calcium hydroxide in agriculture. (3 marks)

    Answer: Neutralizes acidic soil, improving nutrient availability. Ca(OH)₂ + H₂SO₄ → CaSO₄ + 2H₂O.

  40. Describe the preparation of bleaching powder and its use. (4 marks)

    Answer: Ca(OH)₂ + Cl₂ → Ca(OCl)Cl + H₂O. Use: Disinfectant, bleaching agent in textiles.
