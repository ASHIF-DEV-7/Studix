Reading Comprehension

1. What does the author suggest about the importance of reading habits in the passage?
The author suggests that developing strong reading habits from an early age enhances vocabulary, improves concentration, and expands knowledge across various subjects. Regular reading also develops critical thinking skills and empathy by exposing readers to diverse perspectives.
2. How does the author distinguish between active and passive reading?
The author distinguishes active reading as an engaged process where readers ask questions, make connections, and reflect on the content, while passive reading involves simply scanning words without deeper comprehension or retention of information.
3. According to the passage, what are the benefits of reading fiction?
According to the passage, reading fiction enhances empathy by allowing readers to experience different perspectives, improves emotional intelligence, reduces stress, and develops creativity and imagination more effectively than other forms of media.

[Story would include elements of narrative writing: setting/atmosphere, characters, conflict (world where books are disappearing), climax (discovery of the last book), and resolution. The story would emphasize the value of preserving knowledge and literature.]

Grammar

7. Fill in the blanks with appropriate determiners: ____ books you ordered have arrived, but ____ few are out of stock.
The books you ordered have arrived, but a few are out of stock.
8. Rearrange the words to form meaningful sentences: reading / not only / enhances / but also / vocabulary / concentration / improves
Reading not only enhances vocabulary but also improves concentration.
9. Identify the error and correct it: Neither of the books are available in the library.
Error: subject-verb agreement. Correction: Neither of the books is available in the library.
10. Change the narration: The teacher said, "Reading regularly will improve your writing skills."
The teacher said that reading regularly would improve our writing skills.

Literature: First Flight

11. How does the postmaster respond to Lencho's letter in "A Letter to God"? What does this reveal about his character?
The postmaster responds to Lencho's letter with empathy and generosity, collecting money from his employees to help Lencho. This reveals his compassionate nature, his respect for faith, and his willingness to go out of his way to maintain someone's belief in divine intervention.
12. What does Nelson Mandela mean when he says that courage is not the absence of fear but the triumph over it?
Mandela means that brave people are not those who never feel fear, but those who acknowledge their fear and still choose to act according to their principles. True courage involves overcoming fear rather than never experiencing it.
13. How does Anne Frank view the conflicting sides of her nature? Explain with examples from the diary.
Anne Frank recognizes two Annes: the cheerful, flippant exterior that others see, and the deeper, more serious interior self that she reveals only in her diary. She describes how her lively behavior often leads people to misunderstand her true thoughtful and sensitive nature.
14. What is the central theme of the poem "Amanda"? How does the poet convey this theme?
The central theme of "Amanda" is the conflict between a child's desire for freedom and imagination versus parental control and expectations. The poet conveys this through the contrasting voices: the nagging mother and Amanda's dreamy responses where she imagines herself in different free-spirited roles.
15. How does the tiger in the poem "A Tiger in the Zoo" contrast with a tiger in its natural habitat?
The zoo tiger is confined, frustrated, and powerless, pacing in his limited cage, while a natural habitat tiger would be free, powerful, and fearsome, lurking in shadows, sliding through long grass, and snarling near villages.

Literature: Footprints Without Feet

16. How does Griffin's invisibility contribute to his antisocial behavior?
Griffin's invisibility gives him a sense of power and detachment from social norms, allowing him to satisfy his selfish desires without fear of recognition or consequences. This isolation and power gradually erode his morality and connection to humanity.
17. What role does the mirror play in the lesson "The Hack Driver"?
The mirror serves as a symbolic element that reveals the true nature of the hack driver Bill. When the lawyer looks in the mirror, he sees Bill's reflection instead of his own, symbolically representing how Bill has manipulated and reflected what the lawyer wanted to see throughout their interactions.
18. How does Bholi's teacher help her overcome her insecurities?
Bholi's teacher helps her by providing patient encouragement, speaking to her kindly, giving her a book to inspire dreams of a better future, and most importantly, teaching her to read and speak clearly, which builds her confidence and self-worth.
19. What is the significance of the title "The Making of a Scientist"?
The title signifies the process and factors that contribute to Richard Ebright's development as a scientist: his curiosity, his mother's support, the role of mentors, his participation in science fairs, his competitive spirit, and his ability to learn from failures and successes.
20. How does the lesson "The Necklace" portray the consequences of vanity and desire?
The story portrays how Mathilde's vanity and desire for a wealthier lifestyle lead her to borrow a necklace she can't afford to replace, resulting in ten years of hardship and poverty to repay the debt, ultimately revealing the irony that the original necklace was fake and her sacrifice was unnecessary.
