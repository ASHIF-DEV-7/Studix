First Flight 2022 Previous Year Questions

1. What is the central theme of "A Letter to God"?
The story emphasizes faith and hope. Lencho, a poor farmer, trusts God to help him after a hailstorm destroys his crops, showing human reliance on faith.
2. Describe the poem "Dust of Snow" in brief.
The poem describes how a small incident of snow falling from a tree changes the poet's mood, symbolizing nature's positive impact on human life.
3. How does the story "Nelson Mandela: Long Walk to Freedom" inspire readers?
It inspires readers through Mandela's courage, determination, and fight for equality, showing that persistence and morality can overcome oppression.
4. Discuss the personality of the young seagull in "His First Flight".
The young seagull is initially fearful but gains courage and confidence through the encouragement of its parents, symbolizing growth and learning to face fears.
5. What lesson does the poem "Fire and Ice" convey?
It warns of the destructive power of human emotions such as desire and hatred, suggesting that both can lead to catastrophic consequences.
6. What is the moral of the story "The Necklace"?
The story teaches that vanity and greed can lead to suffering, as Mme. Loisel loses years of her life due to her desire to appear wealthy.
7. Explain the main idea of "Two Stories about Flying".
The stories highlight courage, faith, and dealing with fear. The pilots overcome danger and uncertainty, emphasizing human resilience.
8. How does the poem "A Tiger in the Zoo" depict captivity?
It contrasts the tiger's natural strength and grace with the restrictions of a zoo, emphasizing the loss of freedom and natural instincts.
9. Summarize the story "Bholi" and its significance.
Bholi, a neglected and timid girl, becomes confident through education and courageously rejects an unfair marriage proposal, showing empowerment through learning.
10. What is the role of imagination in "Amanda!"?
Amanda's imagination allows her to escape boredom and strict parental control, highlighting children's need for creativity and freedom.
11. Describe the ending of "The Hundred Dresses" and its lesson.
Wanda forgives her classmates for mocking her, teaching the importance of empathy, understanding, and remorse.
12. How does the poem "Fog" use imagery?
The poet compares fog to a silent cat, using vivid imagery to convey its quiet and mysterious movement.
13. Discuss the theme of courage in "His First Flight".
The young seagull learns to face fear and take risks, demonstrating that courage is necessary for growth and independence.
14. What is the significance of Tricki's treatment in "A Triumph of Surgery"?
The story emphasizes the importance of proper care and nutrition, as Tricki becomes healthy after simple corrective measures.
15. Explain the humor in "The Proposal".
The humor arises from trivial quarrels and exaggerated personalities, critiquing human behavior while entertaining readers.
16. How does the poem "Animals" contrast human and animal life?
It highlights the simplicity, honesty, and contentment of animals versus human greed, envy, and dishonesty.
17. How does Anne Frank describe her diary in the beginning?
Anne describes her diary as a friend that listens to her thoughts and feelings, providing comfort and a sense of companionship.
18. What lesson is taught in "The Ball Poem"?
The poem teaches acceptance of loss as a part of life and the importance of learning responsibility from early experiences.
19. What does the poem "For Anne Gregory" convey?
It conveys that true love values inner beauty and character over external appearance.
20. Describe the message of "The Trees".
The poem emphasizes the natural freedom of trees and human respect for nature's cycles, encouraging environmental awareness.
21. What moral does "A Letter to God" teach?
It teaches the power of faith and hope, showing that belief in higher powers can sustain humans during adversity.
22. How does the poem "Amanda!" show children's struggle?
It portrays the tension between children's desire for freedom and adults' imposition of rules, highlighting the need for empathy.
23. Explain the theme of empathy in "The Hundred Dresses".
The story stresses understanding others' feelings and the importance of compassion to prevent regret and guilt.
24. How does the young seagull gain confidence?
Through parental encouragement and repeated attempts, the seagull overcomes fear, symbolizing growth through experience.
25. Discuss the significance of humor in "The Proposal".
Humor exposes human follies and absurdities in social situations, making the story both entertaining and thought-provoking.
26. How does education transform Bholi?
Education empowers Bholi, enhancing her self-confidence and enabling her to make independent decisions.
27. What is the main lesson of "A Triumph of Surgery"?
Responsible care and attention to health can correct problems and improve well-being, even with simple measures.
28. How does the poem "Fire and Ice" reflect human emotions?
It uses the elements fire and ice to symbolize desire and hatred, illustrating their destructive potential.
29. What is the moral of "The Necklace"?
Vanity and obsession with appearances can lead to unnecessary suffering and life-long consequences.
30. Explain the central idea of "Two Stories about Flying".
The stories highlight bravery, faith, and human perseverance in the face of danger and uncertainty.
