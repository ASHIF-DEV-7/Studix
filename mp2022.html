Class 10 Chemistry Previous Year Questions (2022) - Metals and Non-Metals

  1. Differentiate between metals and non-metals based on physical properties. (3 marks)

    Answer: Metals are lustrous, malleable, ductile, and good conductors (e.g., Cu). Non-metals are non-lustrous, brittle, and poor conductors (e.g., S).

  2. Explain why metals are good conductors of electricity. (3 marks)

    Answer: Metals have free electrons in their lattice, allowing electric current to flow.

  3. What is the reactivity series? List four metals in decreasing order of reactivity. (3 marks)

    Answer: Reactivity series ranks metals by reactivity. Order: K, Na, Ca, Mg.

  4. Describe the reaction of magnesium with oxygen. Write the balanced equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Mg burns in O₂ to form MgO. 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO.

  5. Explain why sodium is stored under kerosene. (3 marks)

    Answer: Sodium reacts vigorously with air and water, forming NaOH and H₂; kerosene prevents contact.

  6. What is corrosion? Describe the chemical process of rusting of iron. (4 marks)

    Answer: Corrosion is metal deterioration by chemical reaction. Rusting: 4Fe + 3O₂ + 6H₂O → 4Fe(OH)₃.

  7. Suggest three methods to prevent corrosion of iron. (3 marks)

    Answer: Galvanization, painting, applying oil/grease.

  8. Explain the formation of ionic bonds in sodium chloride. (4 marks)

    Answer: Na donates one electron to Cl, forming Na⁺ and Cl⁻, which are attracted electrostatically to form NaCl lattice.

  9. Why do non-metals not conduct electricity? Give an exception. (3 marks)

    Answer: Non-metals lack free electrons. Exception: Graphite conducts due to delocalized electrons.

  10. What is an alloy? Give two examples with their compositions. (3 marks)

    Answer: Alloy is a mixture of metals or metal with non-metal. Examples: Brass (Cu, Zn), Steel (Fe, C).

  11. Explain the extraction of iron from haematite ore. (4 marks)

    Answer: Haematite (Fe₂O₃) reduced in blast furnace with C and CO: Fe₂O₃ + 3CO → 2Fe + 3CO₂.

  12. Why is aluminium used in aircraft manufacturing? (3 marks)

    Answer: Aluminium is lightweight, strong, and corrosion-resistant due to Al₂O₃ layer.

  13. Write the reaction of zinc with dilute HCl. Identify the gas evolved. (3 marks)

    Answer: Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂. Gas: Hydrogen.

  14. Explain the term 'metallurgy' and its importance. (3 marks)

    Answer: Metallurgy is extracting and purifying metals from ores. Importance: Provides metals for industries, tools.

  15. What is the role of cryolite in aluminium extraction? (3 marks)

    Answer: Cryolite lowers the melting point of Al₂O₃, making electrolysis energy-efficient.

  16. Describe the reaction of copper with concentrated nitric acid. Write the equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Cu + 4HNO₃ → Cu(NO₃)₂ + 2NO₂ + 2H₂O. Produces nitrogen dioxide.

  17. Explain why gold is used in jewelry. (3 marks)

    Answer: Gold is lustrous, corrosion-resistant, and malleable, ideal for intricate designs.

  18. What is an ore? Name two ores of iron. (3 marks)

    Answer: Ore is a mineral from which metal is extracted. Iron ores: Haematite (Fe₂O₃), Magnetite (Fe₃O₄).

  19. Explain the roasting process in metallurgy with an example. (4 marks)

    Answer: Roasting heats sulphide ore in air to form oxide. Example: 2ZnS + 3O₂ → 2ZnO + 2SO₂.

  20. Why do metals form basic oxides? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Metals react with O₂ to form oxides that react with water to form bases. Example: Na₂O + H₂O → 2NaOH.

  21. Describe the reaction of non-metals with oxygen. Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Non-metals form acidic or neutral oxides. Example: S + O₂ → SO₂ (acidic).

  22. What is the difference between calcination and roasting? (4 marks)

    Answer: Calcination heats carbonate ore in absence of air (e.g., CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂). Roasting heats sulphide ore in air (e.g., 2ZnS + 3O₂ → 2ZnO + 2SO₂).

  23. Explain why copper does not react with water. (3 marks)

    Answer: Copper is low in reactivity series, lacks sufficient reactivity to displace H₂ from H₂O.

  24. What is galvanization? Explain its significance. (3 marks)

    Answer: Galvanization coats iron with zinc to prevent rusting. Zn sacrifices itself, protecting iron.

  25. Write the reaction of aluminium with sodium hydroxide. (3 marks)

    Answer: 2Al + 2NaOH + 6H₂O → 2NaAl(OH)₄ + 3H₂.

  26. Explain the formation of covalent bonds in non-metals with an example. (4 marks)

    Answer: Non-metals share electrons to form covalent bonds. Example: Cl₂ shares one pair of electrons per Cl atom.

  27. Why is stainless steel corrosion-resistant? (3 marks)

    Answer: Contains Cr and Ni, forming a protective oxide layer preventing rust.

  28. What is froth flotation? Explain its use in metallurgy. (3 marks)

    Answer: Froth flotation separates sulphide ores using oil and water; ore floats, impurities sink.

  29. Explain the reaction of iron with steam. Write the equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: 3Fe + 4H₂O → Fe₃O₄ + 4H₂. Forms magnetite and hydrogen.

  30. What are amphoteric oxides? Give two examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Oxides reacting with both acids and bases. Examples: Al₂O₃, ZnO.

  31. Describe the electrolytic refining of copper. (4 marks)

    Answer: Impure Cu anode, pure Cu cathode in CuSO₄ solution; Cu²⁺ ions deposit on cathode, purifying copper.

  32. Why do non-metals form acidic oxides? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Non-metal oxides react with water to form acids. Example: CO₂ + H₂O → H₂CO₃.

  33. Explain the use of mercury in thermometers. (3 marks)

    Answer: Mercury expands uniformly with temperature, is liquid at room temperature, and visible.

  34. What is the thermit reaction? Write its equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Al reduces Fe₂O₃ to Fe, releasing heat. Fe₂O₃ + 2Al → 2Fe + Al₂O₃.

  35. Explain why metals are sonorous. (3 marks)

    Answer: Metals produce sound when struck due to their crystalline structure and elasticity.

  36. What is the role of carbon in the reduction of metal oxides? (3 marks)

    Answer: Carbon acts as a reducing agent, removing oxygen. Example: ZnO + C → Zn + CO.

  37. Explain the extraction of mercury from cinnabar. (4 marks)

    Answer: Cinnabar (HgS) is roasted: 2HgS + 3O₂ → 2HgO + 2SO₂, then HgO → 2Hg + O₂.

  38. Why are alloys stronger than pure metals? (3 marks)

    Answer: Alloy atoms disrupt metal lattice, reducing slippage, increasing strength.

  39. What is anodizing? Explain its importance for aluminium. (3 marks)

    Answer: Anodizing forms a thick Al₂O₃ layer on Al, enhancing corrosion resistance.

  40. Explain the reaction of metals with dilute acids. Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Metals above H in reactivity series displace H₂. Example: Mg + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂.

  41. Describe the properties of ionic compounds with an example. (4 marks)

    Answer: High melting points, soluble in water, conduct electricity in molten state. Example: NaCl.
