Class 10 English –
“Fog” by Carl Sandburg

Introduction (10 Lines)

1. “Fog” is a short poem written by Carl Sandburg.

2. It describes the quiet arrival of fog over a city.

3. The poem is only a few lines but full of imagery.

4. Sandburg compares fog to a cat silently moving on rooftops.

5. The fog appears, rests, and moves away without notice.

6. The poet uses simple language to create vivid images.

7. The poem shows nature’s quiet yet mysterious power.

8. Fog symbolizes calm, secrecy, and stillness in the city.

9. Sandburg captures a natural phenomenon with elegance.

10. It teaches observation of simple natural beauty around us.

Explanation – 20 Points

  • 1. The poem opens by describing fog settling over a city.
  • 2. Fog is portrayed as soft, quiet, and gentle.
  • 3. Sandburg compares fog to a cat moving silently.
  • 4. The fog “rubs its back” on buildings and streets.
  • 5. This gives a sense of smooth, graceful movement.
  • 6. It comes unnoticed and leaves quietly.
  • 7. Fog covers the city without disturbing life.
  • 8. It appears suddenly in the morning or evening.
  • 9. Fog softens the harshness of the urban environment.
  • 10. Sandburg uses imagery to make the city lively and poetic.
  • 11. The cat comparison makes fog seem alive.
  • 12. The poem shows nature’s presence even in cities.
  • 13. The simplicity of the poem reflects the calmness of fog.
  • 14. The fog moves gracefully, just like a silent cat.
  • 15. It can symbolize mystery or unpredictability in life.
  • 16. The poem encourages careful observation of surroundings.
  • 17. It teaches appreciation of subtle, quiet natural phenomena.
  • 18. The poem uses few words but evokes strong imagery.
  • 19. It demonstrates the poet’s ability to see beauty in ordinary events.
  • 20. Overall, it reflects harmony between nature and human life.

Conclusion (7–8 Lines)

1. “Fog” is a short but powerful poem using vivid imagery.

2. Carl Sandburg compares fog to a cat to show its silent movement.

3. The poem teaches observation and appreciation of simple things.

4. Fog symbolizes calm, mystery, and quiet strength in nature.

5. Despite being brief, the poem creates a visual and emotional impact.

6. It highlights the poet’s skill in portraying ordinary phenomena poetically.

7. Readers learn to see beauty in the small and unnoticed.

8. Overall, it reflects harmony between nature and human life.

Moral / Theme

Moral: The poem teaches that beauty and wonder exist in small, quiet natural events. By observing them carefully, we can learn to appreciate nature’s subtle power and elegance.
