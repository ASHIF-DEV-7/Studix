A Question of Trust

By Victor Canning | Class 10 English - Footprints Without Feet

About the Story

"A Question of Trust" is an intriguing story about a professional thief named Horace Danby who gets tricked by a clever woman while attempting a robbery.

Key Message:

Even experienced criminals can fall victim to deception, and trust can be easily manipulated.

Main Characters

  • Horace Danby: A 50-year-old thief who loves books
  • The Young Woman: A clever thief who poses as the house owner's wife
  • Police Officer: The officer who arrests Horace

Summary

Horace Danby was a successful thief who robbed one safe every year to buy rare books. He planned to rob Shotover Grange, studying the house for two weeks.

While breaking in, he encountered a young woman who claimed to be the owner's wife. She convinced Horace to open the safe for her, pretending she forgot her glasses. After taking the jewels, she disappeared.

Three days later, Horace was arrested for the robbery. The real owner's wife was a sixty-year-old woman, and Horace realized he had been tricked by another thief.

Irony:

A professional thief who studied his targets carefully was outsmarted by another criminal using his own methods against him.

Important Points

  • Horace was considered a good, honest citizen except for one robbery each year
  • He suffered from hay fever, which became his weakness
  • The young woman used psychological manipulation to trick Horace
  • Horace's love for books was his motivation for stealing
  • The story shows that overconfidence can lead to downfall

Plot Twist:

The woman Horace trusted as the house owner's wife turned out to be another thief who used his skills to steal the jewels.

About the Author

Victor Canning (1911-1986) was a British writer of adventure novels and thrillers. He wrote over 60 books, many of which were adapted for television and film.

Vocabulary

  • Shotover Grange: The name of the house Horace planned to rob
  • Hay fever: An allergy to pollen that causes sneezing
  • Safe: A strong fireproof cabinet for valuables
  • Grange: A country house with farm buildings
  • Conscientious: Wishing to do what is right

Moral of the Story

Crime never pays, and even the most careful criminals can be caught. Trust should be earned, not given blindly based on appearances.
