Class 10 Chemistry Previous Year Questions (2022) - Carbon and Its Compounds

  1. Explain why carbon forms covalent bonds. (3 marks)

    Answer: Carbon has 4 valence electrons, neither losing nor gaining electrons easily, so it shares electrons to form covalent bonds.

  2. What is catenation? Give an example with carbon. (3 marks)

    Answer: Catenation is carbon’s ability to form chains by bonding with itself. Example: C₂H₆ (ethane).

  3. Describe the structure of diamond and its properties. (4 marks)

    Answer: Diamond has a tetrahedral covalent structure. Properties: Hardest substance, high melting point, non-conductor of electricity.

  4. Explain the structure of graphite and why it conducts electricity. (4 marks)

    Answer: Graphite has hexagonal layers of carbon atoms; delocalized electrons in layers allow electrical conduction.

  5. What are allotropes? Name three allotropes of carbon. (3 marks)

    Answer: Allotropes are different forms of an element. Carbon allotropes: Diamond, graphite, fullerene.

  6. Why is fullerene used in nanotechnology? (3 marks)

    Answer: Fullerene (C₆₀) has a cage-like structure, offering unique strength and electrical properties for nanotechnology.

  7. Differentiate between saturated and unsaturated hydrocarbons with examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Saturated hydrocarbons have single bonds (e.g., CH₄). Unsaturated have double/triple bonds (e.g., C₂H₄).

  8. Write the general formula for alkanes, alkenes, and alkynes. (3 marks)

    Answer: Alkanes: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂; Alkenes: CₙH₂ₙ; Alkynes: CₙH₂ₙ₋₂.

  9. Name the first three members of the alkane series and their molecular formulas. (3 marks)

    Answer: Methane (CH₄), Ethane (C₂H₆), Propane (C₃H₈).

  10. Explain the combustion reaction of methane with a balanced equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Methane burns in oxygen to form CO₂ and H₂O. CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O.

  11. What is a functional group? Give two examples with their structures. (4 marks)

    Answer: Functional group determines chemical properties. Examples: Alcohol (-OH, CH₃OH); Aldehyde (-CHO, CH₃CHO).

  12. Write the IUPAC name for CH₃CH₂OH and explain the naming process. (3 marks)

    Answer: Ethanol. Naming: Parent alkane (ethane), functional group (-OH, alcohol suffix -ol).

  13. Explain the addition reaction of ethene with hydrogen. Write the equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Ethene reacts with H₂ to form ethane (Ni catalyst). C₂H₄ + H₂ → C₂H₆.

  14. What is a homologous series? Give an example with two compounds. (3 marks)

    Answer: Series with same functional group, differing by CH₂. Example: CH₃OH, C₂H₅OH (alcohols).

  15. Describe the substitution reaction of methane with chlorine. (4 marks)

    Answer: Methane reacts with Cl₂ under UV light, replacing H with Cl. CH₄ + Cl₂ → CH₃Cl + HCl.

  16. Why is ethanol used as a solvent? (3 marks)

    Answer: Ethanol dissolves both polar and non-polar substances due to its -OH group and hydrocarbon chain.

  17. Explain the formation of ethanoic acid from ethanol. Write the equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Ethanol oxidizes to ethanoic acid using KMnO₄. C₂H₅OH → CH₃COOH (oxidation).

  18. What is esterification? Write the reaction of ethanoic acid with ethanol. (4 marks)

    Answer: Acid reacts with alcohol to form ester. CH₃COOH + C₂H₅OH → CH₃COOC₂H₅ + H₂O.

  19. Explain the cleansing action of soap. (4 marks)

    Answer: Soap has hydrophilic head and hydrophobic tail, forming micelles that trap grease and rinse in water.

  20. What is saponification? Write the reaction for soap preparation. (3 marks)

    Answer: Ester hydrolysis with NaOH forms soap. Example: (C₁₇H₃₅COO)₃C₃H₅ + 3NaOH → 3C₁₇H₃₅COONa + C₃H₈O₃.

  21. Why do carbon compounds have low melting points? (3 marks)

    Answer: Carbon compounds are covalent, with weak intermolecular forces, requiring less energy to break.

  22. Explain the structure of benzene and its stability. (4 marks)

    Answer: Benzene (C₆H₆) has a ring structure with alternating double bonds; delocalized electrons enhance stability.

  23. What is polymerization? Give an example with a reaction. (3 marks)

    Answer: Monomers combine to form polymers. Example: nC₂H₄ → (C₂H₄)ₙ (polyethene).

  24. Write the IUPAC name for CH₃COCH₃ and explain the naming. (3 marks)

    Answer: Propan-2-one. Naming: Parent alkane (propane), ketone group (-one, on 2nd carbon).

  25. Explain why graphite is used as a lubricant. (3 marks)

    Answer: Graphite’s layered structure allows layers to slide over each other, reducing friction.

  26. What is the difference between alkanes and alkenes in terms of reactivity? (3 marks)

    Answer: Alkanes are less reactive (single bonds); alkenes are more reactive due to double bonds.

  27. Describe the reaction of ethyne with bromine water. (3 marks)

    Answer: Ethyne decolorizes bromine water due to addition. C₂H₂ + 2Br₂ → C₂H₂Br₄.

  28. Explain the use of ethanol in alcoholic beverages. (3 marks)

    Answer: Ethanol (C₂H₅OH) is fermented from sugars, providing the intoxicating effect in beverages.

  29. What is denatured alcohol? Why is it used? (3 marks)

    Answer: Ethanol mixed with toxic substances to prevent consumption. Used as industrial solvent or fuel.

  30. Explain the role of concentrated H₂SO₄ in esterification. (4 marks)

    Answer: H₂SO₄ acts as a catalyst and dehydrating agent, removing water to favor ester formation.

  31. Write the structural formula of propanoic acid and its IUPAC name. (3 marks)

    Answer: CH₃CH₂COOH. IUPAC name: Propanoic acid.

  32. Why are carbon compounds versatile in nature? (3 marks)

    Answer: Due to catenation, tetravalency, and ability to form multiple bonds and functional groups.

  33. Explain the oxidation of propanol to propanoic acid. (4 marks)

    Answer: Propanol (CH₃CH₂CH₂OH) oxidizes using KMnO₄ to CH₃CH₂COOH (propanoic acid).

  34. What is the role of micelles in soap action? (3 marks)

    Answer: Micelles trap grease in their hydrophobic core, allowing water to wash it away.

  35. Explain the difference between soaps and detergents. (3 marks)

    Answer: Soaps are sodium salts of fatty acids, form scum in hard water; detergents are synthetic, don’t form scum.

  36. Write the reaction of ethanoic acid with sodium carbonate. (3 marks)

    Answer: 2CH₃COOH + Na₂CO₃ → 2CH₃COONa + H₂O + CO₂.

  37. What is isomerism? Give an example for butane. (4 marks)

    Answer: Isomerism is different structures for same molecular formula. Butane: n-butane (CH₃CH₂CH₂CH₃), isobutane (CH₃CH(CH₃)CH₃).

  38. Explain why diamond is an insulator. (3 marks)

    Answer: Diamond has all electrons tightly bound in covalent bonds, no free electrons for conduction.

  39. What is the significance of carbon’s tetravalency? (3 marks)

    Answer: Allows carbon to form four bonds, creating diverse and stable compounds.

  40. Describe the test for unsaturation in hydrocarbons. (3 marks)

    Answer: Unsaturated hydrocarbons decolorize bromine water or KMnO₄ solution due to addition reactions.
