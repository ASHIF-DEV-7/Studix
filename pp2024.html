Physics 2024 Previous Year Questions

1. State the laws of reflection of light.
1) Angle of incidence equals angle of reflection. 2) Incident ray, reflected ray, and normal lie in the same plane.
2. What is the speed of light in vacuum?
The speed of light in vacuum is approximately 3 × 10⁸ m/s.
3. Define refraction of light.
Refraction is the bending of light when it passes from one medium to another due to change in speed.
4. What is the focal length of a lens?
The focal length is the distance between the optical center of a lens and its principal focus.
5. Explain the phenomenon of dispersion of light.
Dispersion is the splitting of white light into its constituent colors when it passes through a prism.
6. Define power of a lens.
The power of a lens is the reciprocal of its focal length in meters. P = 1/f.
7. What is magnification?
Magnification is the ratio of the height of the image to the height of the object. M = v/u for lenses.
8. State Snell's law.
Snell's law: n₁ sin i = n₂ sin r, where n₁ and n₂ are refractive indices, i is angle of incidence, r is angle of refraction.
9. What is the difference between real and virtual image?
A real image is formed when rays actually meet, can be projected; a virtual image is formed when rays appear to meet, cannot be projected.
10. Define critical angle and total internal reflection.
Critical angle is the angle of incidence in a denser medium for which the angle of refraction in rarer medium is 90°. Total internal reflection occurs when incidence angle > critical angle.
11. What is refraction through a prism?
It is the bending of light passing through a prism, causing deviation and dispersion of colors.
12. Define lateral inversion in mirrors.
Lateral inversion is the reversal of an image in a mirror from left to right.
13. What is a concave mirror used for?
Concave mirrors are used in headlights, shaving mirrors, and telescopes to focus light.
14. What is a convex mirror used for?
Convex mirrors are used in vehicles as rear-view mirrors for a wider field of view.
15. Define spherical aberration.
Spherical aberration is the failure of rays to meet at a single point after reflection/refraction due to spherical surface shape.
16. What is dispersion in optics?
Dispersion is the separation of white light into its constituent colors.
17. Explain the principle of a microscope.
A microscope uses two convex lenses to magnify small objects in steps: objective lens forms an image, eyepiece magnifies it further.
18. What is the role of a telescope?
A telescope gathers more light than the eye and magnifies distant objects using lenses or mirrors.
19. Define reflection of sound.
Reflection of sound is the bouncing back of sound waves from a surface without absorption.
20. What is an echo?
An echo is the reflected sound heard after a time delay from the original sound.
21. What is reverberation?
Reverberation is the persistence of sound in an enclosed space due to multiple reflections.
22. Define pitch and loudness of sound.
Pitch depends on frequency, loudness depends on amplitude of sound waves.
23. Explain the Doppler effect.
Change in frequency of sound due to relative motion between source and observer.
24. What is the velocity of sound in air?
Approximately 343 m/s at 20°C.
25. What is refraction of sound?
Bending of sound waves when they pass through regions of different air density.
26. Define frequency and wavelength.
Frequency is number of oscillations per second; wavelength is distance between two consecutive crests.
27. What is resonance?
Resonance occurs when the frequency of forced vibration matches the natural frequency of an object, producing large amplitude.
28. Explain pitch of musical notes.
Higher frequency gives higher pitch, lower frequency gives lower pitch.
29. Define intensity of sound.
Sound intensity is power per unit area carried by the wave.
30. What is the relationship between wavelength, frequency, and speed?
v = f × λ, where v is speed, f is frequency, λ is wavelength.
31. Define amplitude of wave.
Amplitude is the maximum displacement of particles from their mean position.
32. What is constructive and destructive interference?
Constructive interference: waves add up; destructive interference: waves cancel each other.
33. Explain longitudinal and transverse waves.
Longitudinal waves: particles vibrate parallel to direction of wave; Transverse: perpendicular.
34. What is the effect of temperature on speed of sound?
Speed of sound increases with temperature.
35. Define ultra sound and its applications.
Ultrasound: sound waves above 20 kHz, used in medical imaging, cleaning, and detection.
36. Define shock wave.
A shock wave is a sudden change in pressure caused by objects moving faster than the speed of sound.
37. What is the principle of hearing in humans?
Sound waves enter ear, cause vibration of eardrum, transmitted via ossicles to cochlea, stimulating nerves.
