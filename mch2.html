Key Points about Polynomials

  • A polynomial is an expression consisting of variables and coefficients with non-negative integer exponents
  • The degree of a polynomial is the highest power of the variable in the polynomial
  • A constant polynomial has degree 0 (except for the zero polynomial)
  • A linear polynomial has degree 1 and is of the form ax + b
  • A quadratic polynomial has degree 2 and is of the form ax¬≤ + bx + c
  • A cubic polynomial has degree 3 and is of the form ax¬≥ + bx¬≤ + cx + d
  • The zero polynomial has all coefficients equal to zero and degree is undefined
  • A monomial is a polynomial with only one term
  • A binomial is a polynomial with exactly two terms
  • A trinomial is a polynomial with exactly three terms
  • The leading coefficient is the coefficient of the term with the highest degree
  • A polynomial is said to be in standard form when terms are arranged in descending order of degree
  • If p(a) = 0, then a is called a zero or root of the polynomial p(x)
  • Every linear polynomial has exactly one zero
  • A quadratic polynomial can have at most 2 zeros
  • A cubic polynomial can have at most 3 zeros
  • In general, a polynomial of degree n can have at most n zeros
  • The value of a polynomial p(x) at x = a is denoted by p(a)
  • If p(x) and g(x) are two polynomials, then p(x) + g(x) and p(x) √ó g(x) are also polynomials
  • The sum of polynomials of degrees m and n has degree max(m,n)
  • The product of polynomials of degrees m and n has degree m + n
  • Division algorithm for polynomials: p(x) = g(x) √ó q(x) + r(x)
  • The remainder theorem states that when p(x) is divided by (x-a), the remainder is p(a)
  • The factor theorem states that (x-a) is a factor of p(x) if and only if p(a) = 0
  • Polynomials are continuous and differentiable functions
