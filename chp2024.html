Class 10 Chemistry Previous Year Questions (2024) with Answers

  1. Explain the law of conservation of mass with an example of a balanced chemical equation. (3 marks)

    Answer: The law of conservation of mass states that mass can neither be created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction; it remains constant. Example: In the decomposition of calcium carbonate, CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂, the mass of CaCO₃ equals the mass of CaO + CO₂.

  2. Classify the following reaction as combination, decomposition, displacement, or double displacement, and explain why: 2Pb(NO₃)₂ → 2PbO + 4NO₂ + O₂. (3 marks)

    Answer: This is a decomposition reaction because a single compound breaks down into two or more simpler substances upon heating.

  3. Write the balanced chemical equation for the reaction between zinc and dilute sulphuric acid, and state the type of reaction. (3 marks)

    Answer: Zn + H₂SO₄ → ZnSO₄ + H₂. This is a displacement reaction as zinc displaces hydrogen from the acid.

  4. What is rancidity? Suggest two methods to prevent it in food items. (3 marks)

    Answer: Rancidity is the oxidation of fats and oils in food leading to bad smell and taste. Prevention: Adding antioxidants like BHT; storing food in air-tight containers or nitrogen gas flushing.

  5. Identify the substance oxidized and reduced in the reaction: CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O. Justify your answer. (3 marks)

    Answer: H₂ is oxidized (loses electrons, gain of oxygen), CuO is reduced (gains electrons, loss of oxygen). It's a redox reaction.

  6. Describe the process of electrolysis of water with a labelled diagram in words, and write the electrode reactions. (4 marks)

    Answer: Electrolysis of water uses electric current to decompose water into H₂ and O₂. At cathode: 2H₂O + 2e⁻ → H₂ + 2OH⁻; At anode: 4OH⁻ → O₂ + 2H₂O + 4e⁻. (Diagram: Electrolytic cell with electrodes in acidified water.)

  7. Explain why sodium chloride solution conducts electricity but solid sodium chloride does not. (3 marks)

    Answer: In solution, NaCl dissociates into Na⁺ and Cl⁻ ions, which are mobile and carry charge. In solid, ions are fixed in lattice, no mobility.

  8. Write the chemical formula of plaster of Paris and explain its setting reaction with water. (3 marks)

    Answer: CaSO₄·½H₂O. Setting: CaSO₄·½H₂O + 1½H₂O → CaSO₄·2H₂O (gypsum), which hardens.

  9. Differentiate between exothermic and endothermic reactions with one example each. (3 marks)

    Answer: Exothermic releases heat (e.g., combustion of methane: CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O + heat). Endothermic absorbs heat (e.g., decomposition of CaCO₃: CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂ - heat).

  10. Balance the following equation and identify the type: Fe + H₂O → Fe₃O₄ + H₂. (3 marks)

    Answer: 3Fe + 4H₂O → Fe₃O₄ + 4H₂. It's a redox reaction (displacement and combination).

  11. What is a corrosion? Explain the chemical process involved in the rusting of iron. (4 marks)

    Answer: Corrosion is the gradual destruction of metal by chemical attack. Rusting: 4Fe + 3O₂ + 6H₂O → 4Fe(OH)₃ (rust), an oxidation process.

  12. Explain the formation of sodium chloride crystal with reference to ionic bonding. (3 marks)

    Answer: Na loses one electron to Cl, forming Na⁺ and Cl⁻. Electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions forms the lattice structure.

  13. Write the balanced equation for the reaction of quicklime with water and state its nature. (3 marks)

    Answer: CaO + H₂O → Ca(OH)₂. It's an exothermic combination reaction.

  14. Why is baking soda used to neutralize acidic soil? Write the reaction involved. (3 marks)

    Answer: Baking soda (NaHCO₃) reacts with acid to form CO₂, water, and salt, neutralizing it. NaHCO₃ + HCl → NaCl + H₂O + CO₂.

  15. Describe the laboratory preparation of hydrogen gas from acids. (4 marks)

    Answer: React dilute acid (e.g., HCl) with metal (Zn) in W-shaped tube: Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂. Collect by downward displacement of air.

  16. What are the properties of acids? Give two examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Sour taste, turn blue litmus red, react with metals to produce H₂. Examples: HCl, H₂SO₄.

  17. Explain the pH scale and its importance in daily life. (3 marks)

    Answer: pH scale measures acidity/alkalinity from 0-14 (7 neutral). Importance: Controls soil pH for crops, monitors blood pH.

  18. Write the reaction of calcium oxide with water and classify it. (3 marks)

    Answer: CaO + H₂O → Ca(OH)₂. Combination and exothermic.

  19. How does tooth decay occur? How can it be prevented? (3 marks)

    Answer: Bacteria produce acids from sugars, eroding enamel (pH < 5.5). Prevent: Use fluoride toothpaste, limit sweets.

  20. Balance and explain the type: AgNO₃ + Cu → Cu(NO₃)₂ + Ag. (3 marks)

    Answer: 2AgNO₃ + Cu → Cu(NO₃)₂ + 2Ag. Displacement reaction (Cu displaces Ag).

  21. Draw the structure of an atom with atomic number 11 and mass number 23. (4 marks)

    Answer: Sodium (Na): 11 protons, 12 neutrons, 11 electrons (2,8,1 configuration). (Diagram: Nucleus with 11p+12n, shells with electrons.)

  22. Explain Rutherford's alpha particle scattering experiment and its conclusions. (4 marks)

    Answer: Alpha particles fired at gold foil; most passed through, few deflected, some bounced back. Conclusions: Atom mostly empty, positive nucleus, electrons orbit.

  23. What is an isotope? Give examples for hydrogen. (3 marks)

    Answer: Isotopes are atoms of same element with same Z but different A. Hydrogen: Protium (¹H), Deuterium (²H), Tritium (³H).

  24. Calculate the number of moles in 46 g of sodium. (3 marks)

    Answer: Atomic mass 23 g/mol, moles = mass/molar mass = 46/23 = 2 moles.

  25. State Dalton's atomic theory and its limitations. (4 marks)

    Answer: Atoms indivisible, indestructible; same element identical. Limitations: Atoms divisible (subatomic particles), isotopes exist.

  26. What is valency? Explain for elements with atomic numbers 8 and 17. (3 marks)

    Answer: Valency is combining capacity. O (8): 2 (gains 2e). Cl (17): 1 (gains 1e).

  27. Differentiate between atomic number and mass number with examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Atomic number (Z): protons/electrons (e.g., C=6). Mass number (A): protons+neutrons (e.g., C-12=12).

  28. Write the electron distribution for magnesium (Z=12). (3 marks)

    Answer: 2,8,2.

  29. Explain why atoms are electrically neutral. (3 marks)

    Answer: Number of protons equals number of electrons, balancing positive and negative charges.

  30. What is the modern periodic table? Mention two merits. (4 marks)

    Answer: Arrangement of elements by increasing atomic number. Merits: Predicts properties, accommodates isotopes.

  31. Explain the trend in atomic size down a group in the periodic table. (3 marks)

    Answer: Atomic size increases down a group due to addition of new shells, shielding effect.

  32. Why do elements in the same group have similar properties? (3 marks)

    Answer: Same number of valence electrons, similar valence shell configuration.

  33. Classify the following as metal/non-metal/metalloid: Na, Si, I. (3 marks)

    Answer: Na-metal, Si-metalloid, I-non-metal.

  34. Explain the position of hydrogen in the periodic table. (3 marks)

    Answer: Hydrogen has properties of both alkali metals (loses e) and halogens (gains e), placed separately.

  35. What are metalloids? Give two examples and their uses. (4 marks)

    Answer: Elements with properties of both metals and non-metals. Examples: Si (semiconductors), Ge (electronics).

  36. Write the balanced equation for the reaction of carbon with oxygen. (3 marks)

    Answer: C + O₂ → CO₂.

  37. Explain the structure of diamond and graphite. (3 marks)

    Answer: Diamond: 3D tetrahedral, hard. Graphite: Layers of hexagonal rings, soft, conductor.

  38. What is an allotrope? Give examples for phosphorus. (3 marks)

    Answer: Different structural forms of same element. Phosphorus: White (reactive), red (stable).

  39. Why is diamond used in cutting tools? (3 marks)

    Answer: Hardest substance due to strong covalent bonds in tetrahedral structure.

  40. Differentiate between saturated and unsaturated hydrocarbons with examples. (3 marks)

    Answer: Saturated: Single bonds (e.g., ethane C₂H₆). Unsaturated: Double/triple bonds (e.g., ethene C₂H₄).

  41. Write the general formula for alkenes and give one reaction. (4 marks)

    Answer: CₙH₂ₙ. Reaction: Addition with H₂: C₂H₄ + H₂ → C₂H₆ (Ni catalyst).

  42. What is polymerization? Give an example. (3 marks)

    Answer: Process of monomers forming polymers. Example: Ethene → Polyethene.

  43. Explain the cleansing action of soap. (4 marks)

    Answer: Soap has hydrophilic head (ionic) and hydrophobic tail (hydrocarbon). Tail attaches to grease, head to water, forming micelles that rinse away dirt.
