SST 2023 Previous Year Questions

1. Explain the factors responsible for the formation of alluvial soil in India.
Alluvial soil is formed by the deposition of silt, sand and clay brought by rivers from the Himalayas and the Peninsular plateau, mainly in the Indo-Gangetic plain and deltas.
2. Describe the Salt March and its significance in the Indian freedom struggle.
The Salt March began on 12 March 1930 when Mahatma Gandhi marched from Sabarmati to Dandi to break the salt law. It united Indians against British salt monopoly and became a symbol of civil disobedience.
3. Define sustainable development and mention two measures to achieve it.
Sustainable development means using resources in a way that meets present needs without harming future generations. Measures include afforestation and use of renewable energy.
4. What were the main demands of the Civil Rights Movement in the USA?
It demanded an end to racial segregation, equal rights in education, voting, and employment for African-Americans.
5. Explain the role of tertiary sector in Indian economy.
The tertiary sector provides services such as transport, communication, trade, banking, education, and health. It supports primary and secondary sectors and contributes the largest share to GDP.
6. Name the two types of resources on the basis of ownership and give examples.
(a) Individual resources like private land and houses. (b) Community resources like village ponds, public parks.
7. What is the importance of the Indian Monsoon?

8. Discuss the major challenges to democracy in India.
Challenges include communalism, corruption, money and muscle power in elections, and unequal socio-economic conditions.
9. Explain the concept of federalism with reference to India.
Federalism is a system where power is divided between a central authority and states. India follows a federal structure as per the Constitution with Union and State lists.
10. Why is the conservation of minerals necessary?
Minerals are non-renewable resources. Conservation ensures future supply through recycling, efficient mining, and substitution.
11. What were the reasons for the rise of nationalism in Europe during the 19th century?
Industrialization, liberal ideas, common language and culture, and resistance to monarchies encouraged nationalism.
12. Define globalization and its impact on Indian economy.
Globalization is the integration of economies through trade, investment and technology. It increased foreign investment and modern industries in India.
13. Give two merits of democracy.
Ensures equality and freedom of speech, allows peaceful change of government through elections.
14. Describe the power sharing arrangement in Belgium.
Belgium shared power between Dutch and French communities through community government, regional councils and central government representation.
15. Mention any three steps to solve unemployment problem in India.
Promote small-scale industries, develop rural infrastructure, improve vocational education and training.
16. What is the importance of biodiversity?
It maintains ecological balance, provides food, medicine, raw materials, and genetic resources.
17. Explain the difference between formal and informal sector of employment.
Formal sector jobs have fixed wages, job security and benefits, while informal sector lacks these protections.
18. What is the role of the Election Commission of India?
It conducts free and fair elections, prepares electoral rolls, and monitors election campaigns and results.
19. Discuss the importance of public distribution system (PDS) in India.
PDS ensures availability of essential food grains at subsidized rates to the poor and maintains food security.
20. What are the main features of India's foreign trade?
India trades in agricultural products, minerals, petroleum and manufactured goods with both developed and developing countries.
21. Define secularism as per the Indian Constitution.
Secularism means the state has no official religion and treats all religions equally.
22. Explain the significance of the Chipko movement.
It was a forest conservation movement in 1970s where villagers hugged trees to prevent deforestation.
23. Why is the manufacturing sector considered the backbone of economic development?
It provides jobs, adds value to raw materials, increases exports, and supports other sectors.
24. Mention two consequences of deforestation.
Loss of biodiversity and increase in global warming due to more carbon dioxide.
25. What are the major features of the Indian Constitution?
It is written, lengthy, federal in nature, provides fundamental rights and directive principles.
26. Define the term Gross Domestic Product (GDP).
GDP is the total value of goods and services produced in a country in a year.
27. What is the significance of the Right to Information Act?
It empowers citizens to access information from government bodies to promote transparency and reduce corruption.
28. How does population pressure affect environment?
It leads to overuse of resources, deforestation, pollution, and loss of biodiversity.
29. Explain the difference between thermal and hydel power.
Thermal power is generated using coal, oil or gas; hydel power uses moving water to generate electricity.
30. Name two major types of farming in India.
Subsistence farming and commercial farming.
31. What is the significance of self-help groups (SHGs)?
They provide small loans to poor people, especially women, to start small businesses and promote savings.
32. Why is it important to conserve water resources?
To ensure availability for agriculture, drinking, and industrial use and to prevent droughts.
33. Discuss the impact of British economic policies on Indian handicrafts.
Heavy taxes and imports of British goods ruined Indian handicrafts, leading to unemployment and decline of traditional industries.
34. Explain the concept of equality in a democracy.
Equality means equal political, social and economic rights for all citizens without discrimination.
35. What is the importance of the National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (NREGA)?
It provides 100 days of guaranteed wage employment to rural households to reduce poverty and unemployment.
