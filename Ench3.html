Two Stories about Flying – Class 10 Notes

Introduction (10 Lines)

1. “Two Stories about Flying” includes two inspirational stories of courage and trust.
2. The first part, *His First Flight* by Liam O’Flaherty, describes a young seagull’s fear of flying.
3. It shows how instinct and family support help him overcome hesitation.
4. The second part, *Black Aeroplane* by Frederick Forsyth, tells of a pilot’s mysterious night journey.
5. Both stories highlight bravery and self-belief during challenging moments.
6. They explore themes of fear, faith, and unexpected help.
7. The flying metaphor represents facing life’s uncertainties.
8. Nature and mystery play key roles in both narratives.
9. Each story ends with a positive and inspiring message.
10. Together, they encourage readers to trust themselves and the unseen forces of life.

Chapter Explanation (20 Points)

  • His First Flight
  • 1. A young seagull is afraid to fly despite his siblings already soaring.
  • 2. His parents encourage and scold him, but he refuses to leave the ledge.
  • 3. Hunger forces him to watch his family eating fish.
  • 4. His mother tempts him with a piece of fish in her beak.
  • 5. Driven by hunger, he jumps to get the food.
  • 6. At first he falls but soon realizes he can flap his wings and fly.
  • 7. Joy fills him as he glides over the sea.
  • 8. His family proudly joins him in the air.
  • 9. He finally dives to catch his first meal from the sea.
  • 10. The story shows that courage grows when we face our fears.
  • Black Aeroplane
  • 11. The narrator-pilot flies a Dakota plane from Paris to England at night.
  • 12. The sky is clear and he dreams of a nice English breakfast.
  • 13. Suddenly, dark storm clouds appear in his path.
  • 14. Instead of turning back, he enters the storm to save time.
  • 15. The compass and radio stop working inside the storm.
  • 16. Out of nowhere, a mysterious black aeroplane appears.
  • 17. Its pilot gestures him to follow and guides him through the clouds.
  • 18. Soon they safely reach the runway, but the black plane disappears.
  • 19. Air traffic control tells him no other aircraft was in the sky that night.
  • 20. The story ends with a mystery—was it a ghost plane or divine help?

Conclusion (7–8 Lines)

Both stories teach that fear is natural but can be conquered with courage and faith. The young seagull learns to fly only when he dares to take the first leap. The pilot in *Black Aeroplane* trusts an unknown guide and survives a deadly storm. These experiences highlight the power of self-belief and unseen help in critical moments. They remind us that life often demands bold decisions despite uncertainty. Facing challenges head-on reveals inner strength we never knew we had. Together, these tales inspire confidence and trust in both ourselves and mysterious forces of nature. Ultimately, they celebrate the triumph of courage over fear.

Moral / Theme

  • Real growth happens when we face our fears.
  • Faith—in oneself or a higher power—guides us through danger.
  • Courage and determination open the door to new opportunities.
  • Some events in life remain unexplained yet life-changing.
