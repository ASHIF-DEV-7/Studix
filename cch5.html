Definition

A political party is an organized group of people who have similar political views and work together to contest elections and hold power in the government.

Three Components of Political Party

  • Leaders: Top leaders who make major political decisions
  • Active Members: Members who participate in meetings and activities
  • Followers: People who believe in party ideology and support it

üéØ Functions of Political Parties

‚ñº

1. Contest Elections

Parties put up candidates for elections and campaign for them.

2. Put Forward Policies

Parties present different policies and programs to voters.

3. Make Laws

Parties play a decisive role in making laws for the country.

4. Form and Run Government

The winning party forms the government and executes policies.

5. Role of Opposition

Parties that lose elections form opposition and keep check on ruling party.

6. Shape Public Opinion

Parties raise and highlight issues to influence public opinion.

üåç Types of Party Systems

‚ñº

One-Party System

Only one party is allowed to rule the country. Example: China, North Korea

  • No choice for voters
  • Not democratic

Two-Party System

Power usually alternates between two main parties. Example: USA, UK

  • Two main parties dominate
  • Others may exist but have little chance of winning

Multi-Party System

Several parties compete for power. Example: India, France

  • More than two parties have real chance of winning
  • Often leads to coalition governments
  • Represents diverse interests

üáÆüá≥ Types of Parties in India

‚ñº

National Parties

Parties recognized at national level by Election Commission

  • Presence in multiple states
  • Get 6% votes in Lok Sabha or Assembly elections in 4 states
  • Win at least 4 seats in Lok Sabha

State/Regional Parties

Parties recognized at state level

  • Strong in one or few states
  • Get 6% votes in state assembly elections
  • Win at least 2 seats in state assembly
üí° Did You Know? India has a multi-party system with several national and regional parties. Coalition governments are common!

‚ö†Ô∏è Challenges to Political Parties

‚ñº

1. Lack of Internal Democracy

Power concentrated with top leaders, ordinary members have little say

2. Dynastic Succession

Top positions controlled by family members

3. Money and Muscle Power

Rich candidates have advantage, use of money and force in elections

4. Lack of Choice

Similar policies offered by different parties, ideological differences fading

‚úÖ Reforms for Political Parties

‚ñº

Legal Reforms (By Law)

  • Anti-defection law to prevent party switching
  • Declaration of assets and criminal cases by candidates
  • Limit on election expenses
  • State funding of elections proposed

Internal Reforms (By Parties)

  • Maintain register of members
  • Hold open organizational elections
  • File income tax returns
  • Give minimum quota to women

People's Participation

  • Join political parties and pressure them from within
  • Support movements and pressure groups
  • Vote wisely and responsibly
