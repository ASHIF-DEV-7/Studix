Class 10 Literature Poem 2022 PYQs
Q1: Who is the poet of 'The Road Not Taken'?
A: Robert Frost.
Q2: What is the theme of the poem 'Ozymandias'?
A: The impermanence of power and human pride.
Q3: Which poetic device is used in "The wind whispered through the trees"?
A: Personification.
Q4: What is the rhyme scheme of 'The Road Not Taken'?
A: ABAAB.
Q5: Name the central idea of the poem 'If—' by Rudyard Kipling.
A: Moral values and self-discipline lead to a successful life.
Q6: What is the tone of 'Daffodils' by William Wordsworth?
A: Joyful and reflective.
Q7: Identify the figure of speech: "My heart leaps up."
A: Personification.
Q8: Which poem talks about human struggle against nature?
A: 'The River' by A.K. Ramanujan.
Q9: Who wrote 'Ode to Autumn'?
A: John Keats.
Q10: Define imagery in poetry.
A: Imagery is the use of descriptive language to create pictures in the reader’s mind.
Q11: What is the central theme of 'Daffodils'?
A: Nature’s beauty uplifts human spirit.
Q12: Who is the speaker in 'The Road Not Taken'?
A: The poet himself, reflecting on choices in life.
Q13: What is the message of 'If—'?
A: Encouragement to remain composed, patient, and virtuous in life.
Q14: Give an example of alliteration from any poem.
A: "The fair breeze blew, the white foam flew" (from 'The Rime of the Ancient Mariner').
Q15: What does 'Ozymandias' symbolize?
A: The fleeting nature of human power.
Q16: Name a poem by Rabindranath Tagore included in Class 10 syllabus.
A: 'Gitanjali' excerpts.
Q17: What is the theme of 'The Ballad of Father Gilligan'?
A: Divine help in human life and compassion.
Q18: Which poetic device is used in "buzzing bees"?
A: Onomatopoeia.
Q19: What does the "yellow leaves" symbolize in autumn poems?
A: The passage of time and aging.
Q20: Who is the poet of 'Fire and Ice'?
A: Robert Frost.
Q21: Give an example of metaphor from any poem.
A: "All the world’s a stage" – Shakespeare.
Q22: What feeling does 'Daffodils' evoke in the reader?
A: Happiness and tranquility.
Q23: What is the significance of roads in 'The Road Not Taken'?
A: They symbolize life choices and decisions.
Q24: Identify the rhyme scheme of 'Daffodils'.
A: ABABCC.
Q25: Who wrote 'The Ballad of Father Gilligan'?
A: W.B. Yeats.
Q26: What lesson is taught in 'Fire and Ice'?
A: Human emotions can be destructive.
Q27: What is the mood of 'Ozymandias'?
A: Reflective and ironic.
Q28: Give an example of simile from any poem.
A: "As brave as a lion".
Q29: What poetic device is “silent as the grave”?
A: Simile.
Q30: What is the poet’s attitude in 'If—'?
A: Encouraging, advisory, and motivational.
