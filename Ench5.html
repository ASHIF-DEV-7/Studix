Glimpses of India

Class 10 English Notes - First Flight

Chapter Overview

"Glimpses of India" is a chapter from the Class 10 English textbook 'First Flight' that consists of three parts, each offering a unique perspective on different regions of India.

Key Highlights:

  • Explores the cultural diversity of India through three distinct regions
  • Highlights traditional occupations and their significance
  • Showcases the natural beauty and resources of different parts of India
  • Provides insights into local traditions, customs, and lifestyles

The Three Parts:

1. A Baker from Goa - A nostalgic look at the Portuguese influence on Goan culture, particularly through the tradition of bread-making.

2. Coorg - A description of the natural beauty, coffee plantations, and brave people of Coorg (Kodagu) in Karnataka.

3. Tea from Assam - A journey through the tea gardens of Assam, exploring the history and process of tea cultivation.

A Baker from Goa

This part is a nostalgic account by Lucio Rodrigues about the traditional bread-making culture in Goa, which has its roots in Portuguese influence.

Summary

The narrator reminisces about the importance of bakers (paders) in Goan society. The baker was an essential part of daily life, arriving early each morning with his bamboo staff jingling, signaling his arrival. Different types of bread were baked for various occasions - bol for marriage, kabai for festivals, and sweet bread for special events.

Key Points:

  • Goan bakers are known as 'paders'
  • Baking was a profitable profession in Goa
  • Traditional breads include bol, kabai, and sweet bread
  • The baker's arrival was announced by the jingling sound of his bamboo staff
  • Bread-making continues to be an important part of Goan culture despite Portuguese departure

Important Vocabulary:

Pader: Goan term for baker
Furnace: A very hot oven used for baking
Bol: A sweet bread usually eaten at marriages
Kabai: A bread baked in a special shape for festivals

Think About:

How does the author use nostalgia to describe the importance of bakers in Goan society?

Coorg

Written by Lokesh Abrol, this part describes the beautiful region of Coorg (Kodagu) in Karnataka, known for its coffee plantations, brave people, and natural beauty.

Summary

Coorg is located between Mysore and the coastal town of Mangalore. It is famous for its coffee plantations, evergreen rainforests, and spices. The people of Coorg are known for their bravery and are possibly of Greek or Arabic descent. The region is also home to various wildlife including elephants, kingfishers, and langurs.

Key Points:

  • Coorg is the smallest district of Karnataka
  • Known as the 'Scotland of India' due to its similar landscape
  • Famous for coffee plantations and spices
  • Coorgi people are known for their bravery and hospitality
  • Possible descendants of Greeks or Arabs
  • Rich in biodiversity with many animal species

Important Vocabulary:

Plantations: Large farms where crops like coffee, tea, or rubber are grown
Evergreen: Having green leaves throughout the year
Biodiversity: Variety of plant and animal life in a particular habitat
Descent: Origin or background of a person in terms of family or nationality

Tea from Assam

This part by Arup Kumar Datta follows two friends, Rajvir and Pranjol, as they travel to Assam, where Pranjol's father manages a tea garden.

Summary

During their journey, Rajvir shares his knowledge about tea with Pranjol. He talks about the legend of how tea was discovered by a Chinese emperor and how it reached Europe and eventually India. When they arrive at the tea garden, they see women plucking tea leaves and learn about the process of tea cultivation.

Key Points:

  • Assam has the largest concentration of tea plantations in the world
  • Tea was first discovered in China
  • There are two main varieties of tea - Chinese and Assamese
  • Tea bushes are pruned to maintain a convenient height for plucking
  • The best tea is made from the first two leaves and a bud

Important Vocabulary:

Plantation: A large estate where cash crops are grown
Pruning: Trimming by cutting away dead or overgrown branches
Sprouting: Beginning to grow or develop
Legends: Traditional stories sometimes regarded as historical but not verified

How does the author use the journey of Rajvir and Pranjol to educate readers about tea cultivation?
